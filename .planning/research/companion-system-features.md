## DELIVERABLE 2: Ideas to Incorporate

### HIGH PRIORITY — Design Patterns

#### IDEA 1: "What Was Said" + "Underwriting Commentary" Dual-Voice Pattern
The HNGE report uses a consistent dual-voice pattern throughout:
- **"What Was Said"**: Factual summary of the data (objective)
- **"Underwriting Commentary"**: D&O risk interpretation (analytical)

This is the single most impactful design pattern in the report. Every subsection ends with this pair, making it crystal clear what is fact vs interpretation. We should adopt this for every section of our worksheet. The factual layer could come from our brain signals; the commentary layer could be LLM-generated using the signal results as context.

#### IDEA 2: Page-0 Decision Dashboard
The HNGE first page packs everything an underwriter needs for a 30-second verdict:
- Company identity + sector + ETF
- 4 KPI stats (MCap, Revenue, Price, Employees)
- Score gauge + tier badge
- 6 mini-cards: Market Cap & Valuation (with decile), Stock Price & Range (with slider), Revenue & Growth (with sparkline + forward estimate), Profitability & Cash Flow (with sparkline), Balance Sheet (with composition bar), Litigation Status (with clear/watch list)
- Stock chart with risk stat badges (SI%, Vol ratio, Max Drop, Insider Sales)
- Key Risk Findings (top 5-7 as numbered list with severity)

We should redesign our Key Stats page to match this density.

#### IDEA 3: D&O Litigation Theory Connections
Every finding in the HNGE report is explicitly connected to the specific SCA theory it enables or defeats:
- "Consistent Beat-and-Raise — Removes the #1 Allegation Vector"
- "Fortress Balance Sheet — Eliminates Financial Distress Claims"
- "Heavy Insider Selling — Plaintiff Optics Problem"

Our signals evaluate risk but don't explain WHY each finding matters in terms of litigation theory. This is the difference between a data report and an underwriting tool.

#### IDEA 4: Filing Probability Decomposition
The HNGE report decomposes the 18-month filing probability into additive components:
```
Base rate (Healthcare D&O):     3.5-4.5%
+ Post-IPO uplift:             +1.5-2.5%
+ Peer cohort scrutiny:        +0.3-0.5%
+ FDA binary catalyst:         +0.3-0.8%
- Beat-and-raise offset:       -0.3--0.5%
- Declining short interest:    -0.1--0.2%
- No prior litigation:         -0.1%
= Estimated 18-month:          4.8-7.8%
```
This is far more defensible than a single probability number. Each component is auditable and adjustable.

#### IDEA 5: Scenario Score Sensitivity (Tornado Chart)
The report shows how each scenario would change the score:
- Current: 29 PREFERRED
- Best case (2yr): 18 Deep PREFERRED
- Moderate stress: 45 AVERAGE
- Severe stress: 51 ELEVATED
- Worst case (SCA + FDA): 71 HIGH

This gives underwriters a range, not a point estimate. We should add scenario modeling to our scoring.

### HIGH PRIORITY — Missing Sections

- Market size and growth narrative
- Competitor profiles with market share %, pricing, and D&O implications
- Peer contagion tracking (other companies in the sector with active SCAs)
- Sector-specific D&O concerns (4-5 issues with D&O implication for each)

This section is industry-specific by definition and maps to our known gap in sector-specific analysis.

We have some regulatory signals but don't present them as a comprehensive regulatory map.

#### IDEA 16: Drop Investigation Framework
Every >10% stock decline analyzed with:
- Period (start-end dates)
- Drop magnitude
- Sector move over same period
- S&P 500 move over same period
- Attribution (COMPANY / MARKET / SECTOR)
- Likely cause
- D&O risk level

The attribution is key — company-specific drops are far more D&O-relevant than market-driven drops.

#### IDEA 17: Return Correlation & Idiosyncratic Risk
- Correlation vs sector ETF
- Correlation vs SPY
- R-squared vs both
- Idiosyncratic risk % (what fraction of price movement is company-specific)

High idiosyncratic risk means price drops are more attributable to management actions.

#### IDEA 18: Volume Anomaly Detection
Days where trading volume exceeds 2x the 20-day average, cross-referenced with:
- Day return
- Known events (earnings, insider filing, news)

#### IDEA 19: Short-Seller Report Check
Named short-seller monitoring:
- Citron, Hindenburg, Spruce Point, Muddy Waters, Kerrisdale, others
- Whether any have published on this company
- Short interest trend (declining = bears losing conviction)

#### IDEA 20: Insider Activity Detail Table
Per-insider breakdown:
- Name, position
- Total sales ($ and % of outstanding)
- Transaction count
- 10b5-1 plan status (Confirmed / Likely / Open market)
- Period of activity

We have aggregate insider data but not this per-person granularity.

#### IDEA 21: Officer Background & Litigation History
Per-officer investigative table:
- Prior companies
- SEC/SCA history at prior companies (CLEAN / PRIOR SCA DEFENDANT)
- Personal litigation
- Suitability notes

The HNGE report found that CFO Budge was a named defendant in the Pluralsight SCA ($20M settlement). This is the kind of finding that changes underwriting terms.

#### IDEA 22: Shareholder Rights & Charter Provisions Inventory
