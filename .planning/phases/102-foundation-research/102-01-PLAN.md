Create `src/do_uw/brain/framework/rap_taxonomy.yaml` defining the Host/Agent/Environment risk taxonomy.

The three top-level categories and their meaning in D&O underwriting:

**Host (H) -- What the company IS:**
Structural, inherent characteristics that create baseline risk exposure. These are slow-moving attributes that define the company's risk profile independent of any specific actions or events. Host risk is the "surface area" for D&O claims.

Subcategories to define:
- `host.identity` -- Size, sector, public tenure, market cap tier (BIZ.SIZE.*, BIZ.CLASS.*)
- `host.structure` -- Business model, complexity, subsidiaries, related parties, holding structure (BIZ.MODEL.*, BIZ.STRUC.*, BIZ.STRUCT.*, BIZ.OPS.*)
- `host.financials` -- Balance sheet position, liquidity, debt structure, profitability baseline (FIN.LIQ.*, FIN.DEBT.*, FIN.PROFIT.*, FIN.SECTOR.*)
- `host.governance_structure` -- Board composition, rights provisions, structural governance features (GOV.BOARD.*, GOV.RIGHTS.*, subset of GOV.EXEC.* that are structural like profiles)
- `host.dependencies` -- Customer/supplier concentration, key person, regulatory dependency (BIZ.DEPEND.*, BIZ.COMP.*)
- `host.environment_exposure` -- Macro sensitivity, sector risk overlay, regulatory intensity (ENVR.*, SECT.*, FWRD.MACRO.* that are structural)

**Agent (A) -- What HAPPENED or is happening:**
Actions, events, behaviors, and decisions by directors, officers, or the company that change risk during the policy period. These are dynamic signals that indicate specific claim-triggering conduct or events.

Subcategories to define:
- `agent.financial_conduct` -- Accounting manipulation, restatements, earnings management, forensic anomalies (FIN.ACCT.*, FIN.FORENSIC.*, FIN.QUALITY.*, FIN.TEMPORAL.*)
- `agent.disclosure_conduct` -- Guidance practices, disclosure quality, NLP-detected changes in filings (FIN.GUIDE.*, FWRD.DISC.*, NLP.*, DISC.YOY.*)
- `agent.executive_conduct` -- Insider trading patterns, executive departures, cluster selling (GOV.INSIDER.*, EXEC.INSIDER.*, EXEC.DEPARTURE.*, EXEC.TENURE.*)
- `agent.corporate_events` -- M&A, IPO, capital changes, restatements-as-events (BIZ.EVENT.*, FWRD.EVENT.*)
- `agent.compensation_decisions` -- Pay structure, clawbacks, say-on-pay, related party transactions (GOV.PAY.*, GOV.EXEC.character_conduct)
- `agent.litigation_actions` -- Active SCA, derivative suits, SEC enforcement, regulatory actions (LIT.SCA.*, LIT.REG.*, LIT.OTHER.*)

**Environment (E) -- What's AROUND the company:**
External market conditions, third-party actions, and contextual factors that the company doesn't control but that amplify or mitigate D&O claim risk.

Subcategories to define:
- `environment.market_signals` -- Stock price behavior, volatility, analyst sentiment, valuation (STOCK.PRICE.*, STOCK.VALUATION.*, STOCK.ANALYST.*, STOCK.TRADE.*)
- `environment.market_patterns` -- Short attacks, death spirals, informed trading, event collapse (STOCK.PATTERN.*, STOCK.SHORT.*)
- `environment.ownership` -- Institutional ownership, activist campaigns, ownership concentration (STOCK.OWN.*, GOV.ACTIVIST.*)
- `environment.peer_context` -- Peer benchmarking, industry patterns, peer contagion (FIN.PEER.*, LIT.PATTERN.*, BIZ.COMP.peer_litigation)
- `environment.external_warnings` -- News, blind spots, third-party sentiment, whistleblower signals (FWRD.WARN.*, BASE.NEWS.*, FWRD.NARRATIVE.*)
- `environment.defense_posture` -- Legal defenses, safe harbors, forum selection (LIT.DEFENSE.*)

For each subcategory, document:
1. `description` -- what this subcategory represents in D&O risk terms
2. `signal_prefixes` -- which signal ID prefixes route here
3. `d_and_o_rationale` -- why this belongs in Host/Agent/Environment specifically (not the other two)
4. `claim_relevance` -- how this subcategory connects to actual D&O claim theories

Also define:
- `classification_rules` -- explicit rules for edge cases (e.g., GOV.BOARD.departures is Agent even though it's in the governance domain, because it's an event/action)
- `dual_aspect_signals` -- signals that have both structural (Host) and behavioral (Agent) dimensions, with guidance on which takes priority

Note on BASE.* signals: These are data acquisition signals (filings retrieved, searches performed) not risk evaluations. Classify them as `host.data_foundation` -- they describe what data the system has, which is a structural attribute of the analysis.

Note on EXEC.AGGREGATE.* and EXEC.PROFILE.*: These are structural assessments (Host), not events (Agent). EXEC.CEO.risk_score and EXEC.CFO.risk_score are composites that span H/A but should be classified based on what dominates their computation.

import yaml, pathlib, sys

# Load mapping
with open('src/do_uw/brain/framework/rap_signal_mapping.yaml') as f:
    mapping = yaml.safe_load(f)

# Load all signal IDs from YAML files
signal_dir = pathlib.Path('src/do_uw/brain/signals')
actual_ids = set()
for yf in signal_dir.rglob('*.yaml'):
    with open(yf) as f:
        signals = yaml.safe_load(f)
    if isinstance(signals, list):
        for s in signals:
            if 'id' in s:
                actual_ids.add(s['id'])

# Validate
mapped_ids = {m['signal_id'] for m in mapping['mappings']}
missing = actual_ids - mapped_ids
extra = mapped_ids - actual_ids
valid_classes = {'host', 'agent', 'environment'}
bad_class = [m['signal_id'] for m in mapping['mappings'] if m['rap_class'] not in valid_classes]

print(f'Actual signals: {len(actual_ids)}')
print(f'Mapped signals: {len(mapped_ids)}')
print(f'Missing from mapping: {len(missing)}')
if missing: print(f'  Missing: {sorted(missing)[:10]}...')
print(f'Extra in mapping: {len(extra)}')
print(f'Invalid rap_class: {len(bad_class)}')

# Distribution
from collections import Counter
dist = Counter(m['rap_class'] for m in mapping['mappings'])
print(f'Distribution: {dict(dist)}')
for cls, count in dist.items():
    pct = count / len(mapped_ids) * 100
    print(f'  {cls}: {count} ({pct:.1f}%)')
    assert pct >= 15, f'{cls} has only {pct:.1f}% -- too few signals'

assert len(missing) == 0, f'{len(missing)} signals missing from mapping'
assert len(extra) == 0, f'{len(extra)} extra signals in mapping'
assert len(bad_class) == 0, f'{len(bad_class)} signals with invalid rap_class'
print('ALL VALIDATIONS PASSED')
