This framework codifies that translation.

**Section 1: Decision Tiers**

Define 6 decision tiers (not 3-4 -- underwriting has more nuance than traffic lights):

```yaml
decision_tiers:
  - id: PREFERRED
    label: "Preferred Risk"
    description: "Strong fundamentals across all H/A/E dimensions. Actively seek to write."
    color: "#2b8a3e"  # green
    entry_criteria:
      host_composite: "<=0.25"  # Low structural risk
      agent_composite: "<=0.20"  # Clean conduct
      environment_composite: "<=0.30"  # Favorable market
      crf_count: 0  # No critical red flags
    underwriting_posture: "Actively compete for this risk. Willing to stretch on price or structure."

  - id: STANDARD
    label: "Standard Risk"
    description: "Acceptable risk profile with no major concerns. Normal underwriting terms."
    color: "#1864ab"  # blue
    entry_criteria:
      host_composite: "<=0.45"
      agent_composite: "<=0.40"
      environment_composite: "<=0.50"
      crf_count: 0
    underwriting_posture: "Write at standard terms. No special conditions needed."

  - id: CAUTIOUS
    label: "Cautious -- Write with Conditions"
    description: "Elevated risk in one or more dimensions. Writable with appropriate terms."
    color: "#e67700"  # amber
    entry_criteria:
      any_composite_above: 0.50
      or_crf_count: 1  # Max 1 CRF, and it must be manageable
    underwriting_posture: "Write with conditions. Price above standard. Consider exclusions, monitoring, or reduced limit."

  - id: ELEVATED
    label: "Elevated -- Referral Required"
    description: "Significant risk requiring senior review. May be writable at appropriate terms."
    color: "#c92a2a"  # red
    entry_criteria:
      any_composite_above: 0.65
      or_crf_count: ">=2"
    underwriting_posture: "Refer to senior UW / committee. Document rationale if proceeding."

  - id: ADVERSE
    label: "Adverse -- Decline Unless Exceptional Circumstances"
    description: "Material risk across multiple dimensions. Normally decline."
    color: "#862e9c"  # purple
    entry_criteria:
      host_composite: ">0.70"
      and_agent_composite: ">0.60"
      or_crf_count: ">=3"
    underwriting_posture: "Decline unless exceptional market conditions or strategic relationship justifies."

  - id: PROHIBITED
    label: "Prohibited -- Decline"
    description: "Risk profile exceeds underwriting appetite. Non-negotiable decline."
    color: "#343a40"  # dark
    entry_criteria:
      active_fraud: true
      or_active_sec_enforcement: true
      or_zone_of_insolvency: true
      or_crf_veto: true  # CRF ELECTRE discordance triggered
    underwriting_posture: "Decline. No exceptions. Document reason for audit trail."
```

**Calibration notes for each tier:**
- Composite score ranges are conceptual (0-1 normalized) -- actual calibration happens in Phase 107 implementation
- Entry criteria use OR logic between conditions (any condition met = tier applies)
- Highest applicable tier wins (PROHIBITED > ADVERSE > ELEVATED > etc.)
- CRF (Critical Red Flag) counts interact with tier assignment but don't solely determine it

**Section 3: Liberty Excess-Only Calibration**

Liberty is ALWAYS excess, NEVER primary. This fundamentally shapes the decision framework:

```yaml
liberty_calibration:
  position: "excess_only"
  products: ["ABC", "Side_A"]

  attachment_considerations:
    description: "Liberty's risk varies dramatically by attachment point"
    rules:
      - "At low attachment ($5-10M xs SIR), Liberty's exposure is nearly primary-like. Apply more conservative tier thresholds."
      - "At high attachment ($50M+), many risk factors are attenuated. Host risks matter more than Agent risks (because Agent risks = claims that may not reach Liberty's layer)."
      - "For Side A, Agent risks matter more (Side A responds to individual director liability, not entity)."
      - "Liberty never provides the SIR or the primary -- structural features of the account below Liberty's attachment are critical context."

  product_considerations:
    ABC:
      description: "Side A + B + C (entity securities) coverage"
      risk_emphasis: "All H/A/E dimensions relevant. Entity coverage means severity (damages quantum) matters most."
      layer_note: "ABC at low attachment = higher risk weight. ABC at high attachment = severity-dependent."
    Side_A:
      description: "Directors & Officers personal coverage only"
      risk_emphasis: "Agent conduct dominates. Individual director/officer behavior drives claims against the person."
      layer_note: "Side A DIC at any attachment = focus on Agent signals and governance controls."

  tower_context:
    description: "The quality and structure of layers below Liberty affects Liberty's risk"
    factors:
      - "Strong primary carrier (Chubb, Travelers) at SIR = better defense coordination"
      - "Multiple quota share layers below = more parties with aligned interests"
      - "Thin tower (Liberty close to SIR) = higher effective exposure"
      - "Full tower (Liberty at high excess) = significant buffer"

  real_world_patterns:
    description: "How Liberty actually makes D&O decisions (from 25 years underwriting experience)"
    patterns:
      - pattern: "Follow the lead"
        description: "When Chubb or Travelers is on the primary, Liberty takes comfort in their underwriting judgment. Lead carrier's presence is a positive signal."
        framework_impact: "Reduce effective tier by one level when strong lead is present."

      - pattern: "Market discipline"
        description: "If the rest of the market is willing to write the tower, it provides external validation. A full tower with quality carriers = market has vetted the risk."
        framework_impact: "Tower completeness and carrier quality are Environment factors that affect tier."

      - pattern: "Attachment point comfort"
        description: "At $50M excess, a $10M fraud allegation settles well below Liberty's layer. Liberty's real risk is catastrophic claims -- which correlate more with Host (company size, complexity) than Agent (specific conduct)."
        framework_impact: "At high attachment, weight Host composite more than Agent composite."

      - pattern: "Renewal vs. new business"
        description: "Renewals with clean claims history get favorable treatment. New business requires more scrutiny."
        framework_impact: "Prior policy period (no claims) is a mitigating factor equivalent to reducing Agent composite."

      - pattern: "Broker relationship"
        description: "Major brokers (Marsh, Aon, WTW) package risks with submission quality and market knowledge. Their involvement is a soft positive."
        framework_impact: "Submission quality and broker tier are process factors, not scored but noted."
```

**Section 4: H/A/E Interaction Model**

Document how the three dimensions interact in underwriting judgment:

```yaml
interaction_model:
  description: "H/A/E dimensions are multiplicative, not additive. This section defines the interaction semantics."

  key_interactions:
    - interaction: "High Host + High Agent"
      meaning: "Big, complex company with bad management behavior. Classic catastrophic SCA scenario."
      examples: "Enron, Wirecard, FTX"
      underwriting_impact: "Multiplicative amplification. Tier jumps 2+ levels above what additive would suggest."

    - interaction: "High Host + Low Agent"
      meaning: "Big, complex company with clean management. Structural risk is there but not being exploited."
      examples: "Large banks in stable periods, mature industrial companies"
      underwriting_impact: "Writable at standard-to-cautious. Host risk is the premium driver, not the deal-breaker."

    - interaction: "Low Host + High Agent"
      meaning: "Small/simple company with bad management behavior. Potential loss is capped by company size."
      examples: "Small-cap fraud, micro-cap manipulation"
      underwriting_impact: "For excess carrier: manageable if attachment is above likely loss quantum. For Side A: still concerning."

    - interaction: "High Agent + High Environment"
      meaning: "Bad behavior in a hostile market. Short sellers circling, stock declining. Claim is imminent or active."
      examples: "Post-Hindenburg Research report companies"
      underwriting_impact: "Near-certain claim filing. Only write if already on the tower at renewal with strong attachment."

    - interaction: "Low Agent + High Environment"
      meaning: "Clean company caught in market turmoil. Sector-wide litigation wave or macro event."
      examples: "Companies in sector-wide downturn, COVID-era claims"
      underwriting_impact: "Writable -- environmental factors are transient. Price the cycle, don't decline the company."

    - interaction: "High all three"
      meaning: "Maximum risk. All dimensions elevated."
      examples: "Active fraud investigation at large company in hostile market"
      underwriting_impact: "PROHIBITED tier. No exceptions."

  crf_override:
    description: "Critical Red Flags (CRFs) are non-compensatory. No amount of favorable Host or Environment can offset an active fraud investigation (Agent)."
    mechanism: "ELECTRE discordance -- CRF veto overrides composite scores"
    examples:
      - "Active SEC enforcement action = PROHIBITED regardless of Host/Environment"
      - "Announced restatement of material magnitude = minimum ELEVATED"
      - "Zone of insolvency = PROHIBITED regardless of Agent/Environment"
```
