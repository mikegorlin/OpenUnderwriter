**Step 1: Define the complete SCAC claim type universe.**

Stanford SCAC categorizes securities class actions by allegation type. The complete list of D&O-relevant claim types (from SCAC data, Cornerstone Research annual reports, and NERA litigation trends):

Securities fraud claims:
- 10b-5 fraud (misrepresentation/omission of material facts)
- Section 11 (registration statement liability -- IPO/secondary offerings)
- Section 12 (prospectus liability)
- Section 14(a) (proxy statement fraud)
- Section 15 (control person liability)
- Scienter-based fraud (intentional or reckless)
- Non-scienter strict liability (Section 11)

Trigger categories (what causes the filing):
- Accounting irregularities / restatement
- Failed merger / acquisition issues
- IPO/SPO allocation or pricing issues
- Product/drug failures (clinical trial, FDA)
- Customer/vendor loss announcements
- Guidance miss / earnings surprise
- Insider trading allegations
- Regulatory investigation disclosure
- Stock drop without specific catalyst
- Cryptocurrency/blockchain claims
- SPAC-related claims
- ESG/sustainability misrepresentation
- Cyber breach / data privacy
- AI/technology misrepresentation
- Pandemic-related operational claims

Derivative and other D&O claims:
- Caremark duty of oversight (board failure to monitor)
- Corporate waste
- Self-dealing / conflict of interest
- Books and records demands (220 actions)
- ERISA breach of fiduciary duty
- Merger objection (duty of care/loyalty in M&A)
- Whistleblower retaliation
- Employment class actions (when D&O-covered)
- Bankruptcy-related claims (zone of insolvency, deepening insolvency)

**Step 2: For each claim type, document the H/A/E causal pathway.**

Every claim arises from the interaction of Host, Agent, and Environment factors. Map each claim type to:

```yaml
claim_types:
  - id: "10b5_fraud_accounting"
    name: "10b-5 Fraud -- Accounting Irregularities"
    scac_category: "Rule 10b-5"
    trigger: "Restatement or accounting error disclosure"
    host_factors:
      subcategories: ["host.financials", "host.structure"]
      explanation: "Complex financial structure creates opportunity for manipulation"
      example_signals: ["FIN.DEBT.structure", "BIZ.STRUC.nongaap"]
    agent_factors:
      subcategories: ["agent.financial_conduct", "agent.disclosure_conduct"]
      explanation: "Management decisions to manipulate or misrepresent financials"
      example_signals: ["FIN.ACCT.restatement", "FIN.FORENSIC.m_score_composite"]
    environment_factors:
      subcategories: ["environment.market_signals", "environment.peer_context"]
      explanation: "Stock drop creates plaintiff standing; peer comparison reveals anomalies"
      example_signals: ["STOCK.PRICE.recent_drop_alert", "FIN.PEER.margin_compression"]
    causal_chain: "restatement_to_sca"  # Reference to existing causal_chains.yaml
    existing_peril: "SECURITIES"
    allegation_theory: "A_DISCLOSURE"
    frequency: "common"
    severity: "high"
    taxonomy_coverage: "full"  # full | partial | gap
```

**Step 3: Identify coverage gaps.**

After mapping all claim types, analyze:
1. Are there claim types where no Host signals exist? (structural blind spots)
2. Are there claim types where no Agent signals exist? (behavioral blind spots)
3. Are there claim types where no Environment signals exist? (contextual blind spots)
4. Are there emerging claim types (AI, ESG, crypto, SPAC) with thin signal coverage?

For any gaps, document:
- What additional signals or subcategories would be needed
- Whether the gap is a true blind spot or acceptable given Liberty's excess-only position
- Priority for future signal development (v8.0+)

Document any chains that don't fit the taxonomy cleanly, with explanation.
