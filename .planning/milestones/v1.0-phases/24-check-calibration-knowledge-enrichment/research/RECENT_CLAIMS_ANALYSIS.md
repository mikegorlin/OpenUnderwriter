# Recent D&O Claims & Settlements Analysis (2022-2026)

> Research Date: 2026-02-11
> Sources: Stanford SCAC, Cornerstone Research, NERA, Allianz, Woodruff Sawyer, D&O Diary, WTW, Aon, multiple law firm publications
> Purpose: Identify patterns the current system may be missing and calibrate scoring/checks

## 1. Filing Trends (2022-2026)

### Overall Filing Volume

| Year | Total SCA Filings | Core Filings (ex-M&A) | Change |
|------|------------------|-----------------------|--------|
| 2022 | ~218 | ~209 | Baseline |
| 2023 | ~213 | ~206 | -1.4% |
| 2024 | 226 | 221 | +7.3% |
| 2025 | 207 | 201 | -8.8% |

**Key takeaway**: Filing counts are relatively stable at 200-225/year. The real story is not volume -- it is SIZE.

### Disclosure Dollar Loss (DDL) -- The Real Story

| Year | Total DDL | Mega Filings (DDL > $5B) | DDL Share from Mega |
|------|-----------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| 2023 | ~$355B | 17 | ~65% |
| 2024 | $429B | 27 | 75% |
| 2025 | $694B | Record high | 81% |

- DDL surged 62% from 2024 to 2025, reaching an ALL-TIME RECORD
- Average DDL per filing nearly doubled vs. the 1997-2023 historical average ($237M)
- Maximum Dollar Loss (MDL) in 2025 was the third highest on record
- **SYSTEM IMPLICATION**: Our DDL calculations must weight mega-cap companies more heavily. A filing against a $500B+ market cap company has outsized DDL impact.

### Industry Breakdown (2024-2025)

| Industry | Share of Core Filings | Trend |
|----------|----------------------|-------|
| Technology + Healthcare | 56-57% | Dominant, stable |
| Healthcare (alone) | 31% in 2025 | Largest single sector |
| Consumer Non-Cyclical | 78 cases (2025) | +16% YoY |
| Health Care Products subsector | Up ~45% in 2025 | Accelerating |

**What this means**: Tech and healthcare together account for MORE THAN HALF of all filings. Our scoring should apply elevated baseline risk for these sectors.

### Motion to Dismiss Outcomes

| Year | Total Resolutions | Dismissals | Settlements | Dismissal Rate |
|------|-------------------|------------|-------------|----------------|
| 2024 | 217 | 124 | 93 | 57% |
| 2025 | 234 | 155 | 79 | 66% |

- Record 139 standard-case dismissals in 2025 (up 32% from 2024)
- Only 2 cases settled before motion to dismiss in 2024 (vs. avg of 7/year from 2015-2023)
- **SYSTEM IMPLICATION**: ~57-66% of filed cases are dismissed. This should inform our "probability of adverse outcome" modeling. A filing alone is not dispositive -- survival past MTD is the real signal.

### Filing Type Shifts

**What's Up**:
- AI-related filings: 13 in 2024, 17 in 2025 (8% of all filings). More than doubled since 2023
- Cybersecurity-related SCAs: New wave in late 2025 (Coupang, F5)
- Accounting-related filings: Increased in 2024-2025
- Non-accounting/event-driven claims: Frequency more than doubled over past decade
- Private credit sector: New category emerging (Blue Owl Capital, Dec 2025)

**What's Down**:
- COVID-related: Dropped to 3 in 2025 (from 15 in 2024). None filed after July 2025. Effectively dead.
- SPAC filings: 9 in 2024, down significantly from peak years (33 in 2021, 24 in 2022)
- Crypto filings: 8 in 2024 (down from 23 in 2022), though 2025 H1 nearly equaled all of 2024
- Merger objection suits: Continuing post-Trulia decline, now shifting to federal courts

### Settlement as Percentage of DDL

- Total DDL in 2024: ~$429B
- Total settlements in 2024: ~$4.75B
- **Settlement/DDL ratio: ~1.1%**
- This ratio has been relatively stable, suggesting settlement amounts scale roughly with DDL but at a fraction

### Settlement Characteristics That Drive Higher Amounts

Based on 2024-2025 data, higher settlements correlate with:
1. **Institutional investor as lead plaintiff** (cases without institutional leads settle for less)
2. **Accounting irregularities** (auditor issues, restatements)
3. **Regulatory investigations** (parallel SEC/DOJ actions)
4. **Company NOT bankrupt/delisted** (bankrupt companies settle for less)
5. **Larger DDL** (bigger stock drops = bigger settlements)
6. **Survival past motion to dismiss** (the biggest filter)

### SPAC-Specific Settlements (2024)

- 17% of de-SPACs were sued in 2024 (vs. 13% of traditional IPOs, 3% of mature companies)

### Derivative Settlement Trends

- 13 of the 18 shareholder derivative settlements exceeding $100M occurred in the past 5 years
- Boeing: $237.5M (largest-ever Caremark cash settlement)
- Derivative suits are SURGING in both frequency and severity
- **SYSTEM IMPLICATION**: Our system needs to weight derivative risk more heavily. It is no longer a secondary concern.

## 3. Notable Cases -- Detailed Analysis

### Case 1: Super Micro Computer (SMCI) -- The Perfect Storm

**Timeline**:
- Feb 2021 - Sep 2024: Class period for securities fraud allegations
- Aug 2024: Hindenburg Research short report published
- Sep 26, 2024: WSJ reports DOJ investigation; stock drops 12%
- Oct 2024: Ernst & Young RESIGNS as auditor, stating it could no longer rely on management representations
- Nov 2024: Stock drops 40%+ on auditor resignation
- Multiple securities class actions filed (Hagens Berman, BFA, Labaton, Rosen)

**Allegations**: False/misleading statements about financial performance, internal controls, related party transactions

**Red Flags Present in Hindsight**:
1. Prior history: SMCI had PREVIOUS SEC enforcement issues (2020 settlement for accounting violations)
2. Rapid revenue growth that outpaced industry norms
3. Short seller attention (Hindenburg)
4. Related-party transactions flagged in filings
5. Internal control weaknesses disclosed but downplayed
6. Auditor change (EY was relatively new as auditor)

**Lesson**: A company with PRIOR SEC issues that shows rapid growth with internal control weaknesses is a compound risk. Our system should have a "recidivist" flag that amplifies risk when prior enforcement history exists.

### Case 2: CrowdStrike (CRWD) -- Event-Driven Catastrophe

**Timeline**:
- Jul 19, 2024: Faulty Falcon Sensor update causes GLOBAL outage affecting 8.5M Windows computers
- Jul 30, 2024: Securities class action filed (W.D. Texas)
- Stock declined ~30% ($12.5B market cap loss)
- 2025: Court DISMISSED the case -- most statements were non-actionable "puffery"

**Allegations**: Failed to disclose deficient controls for testing updates, risk of major outages

**Outcome**: DISMISSED. Judge found:
- Most statements were non-actionable puffery
- Two statements about federal compliance "could plausibly be misleading"
- But plaintiffs failed to establish scienter (intent or recklessness)

**Lesson**: Event-driven cases from operational failures (not fraud) have a HIGH dismissal rate. Product liability =/= securities fraud. Our scoring should distinguish between fraud-based risk (high survival rate) and event-driven risk (high dismissal rate).

### Case 3: Boeing (BA) -- Decade of Accountability

**Key Developments (2024-2025)**:
- Jul 2024: DOJ plea agreement reached (later rejected by court in Dec 2024)
- Dec 2024: Court rejected plea deal
- May 2025: New non-prosecution agreement; Boeing to pay/invest $1.1B+
- Nov 2025: NPA approved by federal judge

**Derivative Settlement** (approved 2022, effects through 2024+):
- $237.5M -- largest-ever Caremark settlement
- Board reforms: Required aerospace safety expertise on board, separated CEO/Chair roles
- Established 5-year Ombudsperson Program

**Lesson**: Boeing demonstrates that D&O exposure can persist for YEARS after the initial event. The 737 MAX crashes were in 2018-2019, and Boeing is STILL paying (literally) in 2025. Our system should track "exposure tail" -- how long a company remains in the liability zone after initial events.

### Case 4: Tempus AI (TEM) -- AI-Washing Template

**Timeline**:
- Jun 12, 2025: Securities class action filed (N.D. Illinois)
- May 28, 2025: Spruce Point Capital short report; stock drops ~20%
- Class Period: Aug 6, 2024 - May 27, 2025

**Allegations**:
- Company touted itself as "AI healthcare company" but generated most revenue from acquisitions, genomic testing, and data licensing
- Inflated contract values (many with related parties, non-binding)
- SoftBank JV appeared to be "round-tripping" capital to create revenue
- Acquired company Ambry had "aggressive and potentially unethical" business practices
- AstraZeneca reduced financial commitments through "pass-through payments"

**Lesson**: AI-washing is the new "greenwashing." Companies that rebrand themselves as "AI companies" to get higher valuations but derive revenue from traditional sources are targets. Our system needs an "AI-washing risk" indicator that compares AI revenue claims to actual revenue sources.

### Case 5: AppLovin (APP) -- Short Seller Triple Attack

**Timeline**:
- Mar 5, 2025: First securities class action filed
- Feb 26, 2025: Stock falls 12.2% on initial report
- Mar 26, 2025: Muddy Waters report; stock falls 20.1%
- Class Period: May 10, 2023 - Feb 25, 2025

**Allegations**:
- AXON 2.0 AI platform capabilities overstated
- Exploited app permissions for "silent, backdoor" app installations
- Reverse-engineered Meta's advertising data
- Manipulative ad practices to inflate click-through and download rates

**Lesson**: Multiple short seller reports in quick succession (Culper, Panda, Muddy Waters) are a devastating signal. When 3+ short sellers independently target a company, the likelihood of a viable claim increases significantly. Our system should track short interest AND number of independent short seller reports.

### Case 6: Blue Owl Capital (OWL) -- Private Credit Unraveling

**Timeline**:
- Dec 2025: Securities class action filed
- Jan 2026: Second lawsuit filed
- Class Period: Feb 6, 2025 - Nov 16, 2025
- Oct 30, 2025: Q3 missed estimates; performance revenue down 33% YoY
- Nov 5, 2025: Announced merger of BDC vehicles
- Nov 16, 2025: FT revealed investor redemptions blocked, values down ~20%
- Nov 19, 2025: Merger terminated

**Allegations**: Misrepresented liquidity, redemption conditions, merger risks

**Lesson**: Private credit is an EMERGING D&O exposure category. The private credit sector has grown enormously, and as rates shift, liquidity mismatches become apparent. Our system should treat private credit/alternative asset managers with elevated baseline risk. The rapid sequence of events (missed estimates -> merger announcement -> redemption freeze -> merger termination) is a classic pattern of deteriorating liquidity.

### Case 7: Coupang (CPNG) -- Cyber Breach Disclosure Failure

**Timeline**:
- May-Nov 2025: Former employee accessed customer data for ~6 months undetected
- Nov 18, 2025: Coupang became aware of breach
- Nov 29, 2025: Public disclosure (~11 days after discovery)
- 33.7 million customers affected
- $8B+ market cap wiped out
- $1.17B compensation plan announced
- Additional 165K users' data leaked subsequently
- Interim CEO investigated by South Korean authorities for potential perjury

**Lesson**: This is the new template for cyber-breach securities litigation. The allegations are not just about the breach -- they are about (1) inadequate cybersecurity protocols, (2) delayed disclosure, (3) failure to report in SEC filings, and (4) a former employee being the threat vector. Our system needs to assess: cybersecurity governance disclosure quality, insider access controls, and breach notification timeline history.

### Case 8: F5 Networks (FFIV) -- Source Code Theft

**Timeline**:
- Aug 9, 2025: F5 discovers cybersecurity incident
- Oct 15, 2025: Reveals hackers exfiltrated BIG-IP product development source code
- Oct 27, 2025: Dismal revenue forecasts (0-4% growth) blamed on breach
- Stock drops 10% on disclosure

**Allegations**: Initially claimed no material impact; subsequent disclosures revealed significant business damage

**Lesson**: Cybersecurity incidents that compromise PRODUCT source code are fundamentally different from customer data breaches. Source code theft directly impacts competitive position and future revenue. Our system should distinguish between data breach types.

## 4. Declining Claim Types

### COVID-Related Claims -- Effectively Dead
- 2024: 15 filings
- 2025: 3 filings (none after July)
- Most faced high dismissal rates
- Some diagnostic/vaccine company cases settled for meaningful amounts
- **STATUS**: No longer a material risk factor. Can be deprioritized in scoring.

### SPAC Litigation -- Past Peak but Not Gone
- Filing volume: Down significantly (9 in 2024 vs. 33 in 2021)
- BUT: Settlement activity increased ($305.5M in 15 settlements in 2024)
- De-SPAC litigation risk (17%) still much higher than traditional IPOs (13%) or mature companies (3%)
- Delaware courts allowing fiduciary duty claims to proceed (Feb 2026 ruling)
- 144 SPAC IPOs priced in 2025 (150% increase over 2024) -- new vintage will generate future claims
- **STATUS**: Declining filing volume but persistent risk. SPAC-originated companies should retain elevated risk for 3-5 years post-de-SPAC.

### ESG/Greenwashing -- Declining but Evolving
- Global greenwashing cases: Down 12% YoY (first decline in 6 years)
- Banking/financial services: Down 20% YoY
- BUT: High-severity cases up 30%
- 30% of companies linked to greenwashing in 2022-23 were repeat offenders in 2024
- In the US: The bigger risk is now ANTI-ESG backlash (red state AG lawsuits, anti-ESG activist suits)
- Companies are "greenhushing" (reducing ESG communications to avoid litigation)
- **STATUS**: Traditional greenwashing declining, but the risk has BIFURCATED -- companies face litigation risk for both over-claiming AND under-delivering on ESG commitments. Anti-ESG suits are the new vector.

### Merger Objection Suits -- Transformed, Not Dead
- Post-Trulia (2016): Disclosure-only settlements disfavored in Delaware
- Plaintiffs shifted to federal courts to avoid Delaware's "plainly material" standard
- 7th Circuit cracking down on "mootness fee" strike suits (2024)
- Filing rates dropped from 90-94% of large deals (2010-2014) to ~73% (2017)
- **STATUS**: Lower filing rate but still filed in most large deals. Judicial hostility increasing.

## 5. Rising Claim Types

### AI-Related Claims -- The Fastest Growing Category
- 53 AI-related SCAs filed since March 2020
- 13 in 2024, 17 in 2025 (8% of all filings)
- 12 filed in H1 2025 alone
- Primary allegation theory: "AI-washing" -- overstating AI capabilities to inflate valuations
- Examples: Tempus AI, AppLovin
- **Trajectory**: Accelerating. Expect 25+ AI-related filings in 2026.

**AI-washing pattern**: Company rebrands or repositions as "AI company" -> stock rises on AI narrative -> short seller or investigative report reveals AI revenue is minimal or capabilities overstated -> stock drops -> SCA filed.

### Cybersecurity Breach Claims -- Evolving Legal Framework
- New wave of data breach SCAs in late 2025 (Coupang, F5)
- Caremark claims for cybersecurity oversight gaining traction (SolarWinds precedent)
- NYU Prof. Arlen argues cybersecurity Caremark claims should succeed for "mission critical" companies that made misleading cybersecurity statements
- SEC cybersecurity disclosure rules (effective Dec 2023) creating new obligation set
- **Trajectory**: Increasing. SEC disclosure rules make it easier to prove what companies knew and when.

### Private Credit/Alternative Asset Manager Claims
- Blue Owl Capital (Dec 2025) -- first major private credit SCA
- Liquidity mismatches, redemption restrictions, and NAV declines are the core issues
- Private credit AUM has grown enormously; more companies with D&O exposure
- **Trajectory**: Early stage. Will accelerate if interest rates shift or credit quality deteriorates.

### Derivative Suits (Caremark Oversight)
- 13 of 18 derivative settlements >$100M occurred in last 5 years
- Boeing $237.5M (largest Caremark settlement ever)
- Regions Financial (Sep 2025): Caremark claim SURVIVED dismissal for bank oversight failure
- New Caremark vectors: Cybersecurity oversight, AI governance, product safety
- **Trajectory**: Surging. Delaware courts have expanded what counts as "mission critical" oversight.

### Tariff/Trade Exposure Claims
- Tariffs at highest level in over a century (2025)
- D&O exposure from: Failure to disclose supply chain tariff risk, materially misleading statements about supply chain dynamics
- DOJ identifying algorithmic pricing as antitrust risk in tariff environment
- **Trajectory**: New and growing. Any company with significant China/international supply chain exposure should flag this.

### Delaware Reincorporation ("DExit") -- Structural Shift
- 18 of 28 companies with 2025 proxy proposals proposed LEAVING Delaware
- 13 went to Nevada, 2 to Texas
- Drivers: Tornetta v. Musk, Maffei v. Palkon decisions viewed as anti-business
- Nevada/Texas offer broader exculpation, director protection, settlement indemnification
- Texas launched dedicated Business Court (Sep 2025)
- **SYSTEM IMPLICATION**: State of incorporation affects D&O risk profile. Delaware = higher litigation risk but more predictable. Nevada/Texas = potentially lower risk but less developed case law.

### Individual Accountability in Corporate Enforcement
- SEC under Atkins: "Back to basics" but targeting individual bad actors with stricter penalties
- DOJ antitrust: Focus expanding from violators to oversight responsibilities of corporate officials
- Nov 2024: DOJ updated antitrust compliance guidance -- now applies to civil AND criminal investigations
- Board members increasingly named personally in enforcement actions
- **Trajectory**: Increasing. Even as SEC corporate enforcement declines (-30%), individual targeting increases.

### SEC Enforcement Decline -- Paradoxical Risk
- SEC enforcement actions: 313 in FY2025 (lowest in decade, -27% from FY2024)
- Only 4 actions against public companies initiated under new leadership
- Total monetary settlements: -45% to $808M
- SEC staff reduced from 5,400 to 4,100 (-24%)
- Crypto enforcement cases dismissed/closed (Coinbase, Binance, Gemini)
- **BUT**: State AGs are filling the gap, particularly in privacy, antitrust, consumer protection
- **PARADOX**: Lower SEC enforcement may actually INCREASE D&O risk because (1) private litigation fills the void, (2) state AGs become more aggressive, (3) companies may become complacent about compliance

### State Attorney General Enforcement
- "Sharp uptick" in multistate AG actions in 2025
- Consumer Protection and Affordability Working Group formed
- AI sweeps targeting fraudulent AI investment schemes
- Texas investigating DeepSeek (Feb 2025)
- 220+ privacy enforcement cases/settlements documented (2020-2024)
- **Trajectory**: Accelerating, especially as federal enforcement retreats.

## 6. Surprise Cases -- What Underwriters Didn't See Coming

### Surprise 1: Super Micro Computer (SMCI)
**Why it was a surprise**: SMCI was a market darling riding the AI/data center boom. Stock went from ~$10 to $120+ in two years. Most underwriters focused on the upside narrative.
**What was missed**: Prior SEC settlement (2020), rapid revenue growth without proportionate infrastructure improvement, related-party transactions, internal control weaknesses that were disclosed but not emphasized.
**Signal present in hindsight**: The 2020 SEC settlement should have been a persistent red flag. Companies with prior enforcement actions have ELEVATED recidivism rates.

### Surprise 2: CrowdStrike
**Why it was a surprise**: CrowdStrike was the gold standard of cybersecurity. No fraud alleged. Pure operational failure.
**What was missed**: Concentration risk -- a single update pushed to 8.5M endpoints simultaneously without adequate staged rollout.
**Signal present in hindsight**: The company's market position itself was the risk. When one company's product is embedded in critical infrastructure globally, the blast radius of any failure is enormous. Market dominance = concentration risk.
**Outcome**: Case was dismissed. But the stock drop was real and the D&O claim was filed.

### Surprise 3: Blue Owl Capital -- Private Credit Liquidity
**Why it was a surprise**: Private credit was seen as a growth story with stable returns. Liquidity risk was acknowledged in theory but not priced.
**What was missed**: The gap between stated liquidity terms and actual ability to honor redemptions under stress. When the merger was announced and then terminated within 14 days, the liquidity fiction collapsed.
**Signal present in hindsight**: Rapid AUM growth in illiquid asset classes + rising redemption requests + declining performance revenue = liquidity crisis brewing.

### Surprise 4: Kaspi.kz -- Geopolitical Exposure
**Why it was a surprise**: Kazakh fintech company listed on Nasdaq. Underwriters focused on business fundamentals.
**What was missed**: The company's bank subsidiary was allegedly being used to help Russians evade sanctions.
**Signal present in hindsight**: Any company operating in former Soviet states with Russian economic ties has sanctions compliance exposure. Geographic risk mapping should include adjacent-country risks.

### Surprise 5: AppLovin -- Short Seller Cascade
**Why it was a surprise**: AppLovin's stock had risen enormously on AI narrative. Three different short sellers published reports within weeks.
**What was missed**: The allegations were about fraudulent app installations and reverse-engineering competitor data -- not typical financial fraud. The ad-tech industry has structural integrity issues that traditional financial analysis doesn't capture.
**Signal present in hindsight**: Meteoric stock rise (APP rose ~750% in 2024), revenue growth that outpaces industry norms, business model dependent on opaque algorithmic processes.

### Surprise 6: Alphabet Google+ ($350M Settlement)
**Why it was a surprise**: Google+ was shut down in 2019. The breach was disclosed in 2018. The settlement came in 2024 -- SIX YEARS later.
**Signal present in hindsight**: Large-cap tech companies settle on their own timeline. The exposure tail can be extremely long.

## 7. Leading Indicators -- What Signals Were Present

Based on analysis of 2024-2025 cases, the following leading indicators were present before claims materialized:

### Tier 1: Strongest Predictors (Present in 80%+ of Filed Cases)

1. **Stock price decline >20% on specific disclosure/event** -- This is the triggering event for virtually all SCAs.
2. **Short seller reports** -- Present before filing in SMCI, Tempus AI, AppLovin and many others. Multiple independent short reports are especially predictive.
3. **Regulatory investigation announcement** -- DOJ, SEC, FTC, or state AG investigation disclosed. Present in SMCI, Boeing, and others.

### Tier 2: Strong Predictors (Present in 50-80% of Filed Cases)

4. **Auditor issues** -- Auditor resignation (SMCI), auditor change, going concern qualification, or material weakness in internal controls.
5. **Restatements or delayed filings** -- Companies that miss filing deadlines or restate financials.
6. **Executive departures** -- Especially CFO, Chief Accounting Officer, or General Counsel departing unexpectedly.
7. **Insider selling** -- Heavy insider selling in the period before negative disclosure.

### Tier 3: Moderate Predictors (Present in 30-50% of Filed Cases)

8. **Revenue quality concerns** -- Related-party revenue, channel stuffing, aggressive revenue recognition.
9. **Rapid growth narrative** -- Companies whose stock price has risen dramatically on growth narrative (AI, fintech, etc.) are more likely to face "washing" claims.
10. **Industry-specific regulatory changes** -- New SEC cybersecurity rules, AI regulations, etc. create new disclosure obligations that can be violated.

### Tier 4: Emerging Predictors (Growing in Importance)

11. **Concentration risk** -- Single product/customer dependency (CrowdStrike)
12. **Private credit/illiquid asset exposure** -- Liquidity mismatch risk (Blue Owl)
13. **Foreign regulatory exposure** -- Sanctions, GDPR, foreign government investigations (Kaspi.kz, Coupang)
14. **AI capability claims vs. revenue reality** -- Gap between AI narrative and actual AI revenue (Tempus AI, AppLovin)

## 8. Implications for Our System

### 8.1 Checks/Signals We Need to Add or Strengthen

#### NEW CHECKS NEEDED:

1. **AI-Washing Indicator**
   - Compare company's AI-related claims (10-K, press releases) to actual AI-derived revenue
   - Flag companies that repositioned as "AI companies" in 2023-2025 without proportionate AI revenue
   - Severity: MEDIUM-HIGH (fastest growing allegation type)

2. **Recidivist Flag**
   - Check for PRIOR SEC enforcement actions, settlements, or consent decrees
   - Companies with prior enforcement have elevated claim probability
   - SMCI had a 2020 SEC settlement and was hit again in 2024
   - Severity: HIGH (strong predictor of future claims)

3. **Short Seller Report Tracking**
   - Count of independent short seller reports targeting the company
   - 1 report = elevated monitoring; 2+ reports = high risk
   - Track: Hindenburg, Muddy Waters, Citron, Spruce Point, Culper, Panda, etc.
   - Severity: HIGH (one of the strongest leading indicators)

4. **Private Credit/Alternative Asset Liquidity Risk**
   - For financial companies: Compare redemption terms to asset liquidity
   - Flag: AUM growth rate vs. performance revenue trend
   - Blue Owl pattern: Growth + declining performance + redemption pressure = crisis
   - Severity: MEDIUM (emerging but growing)

5. **Cybersecurity Governance Quality Score**
   - SEC cybersecurity disclosure rules compliance
   - Board-level cybersecurity oversight committee existence
   - Time between breach discovery and disclosure
   - History of prior breaches
   - Severity: MEDIUM-HIGH (increasing filing trend)

6. **State of Incorporation Risk Modifier**
   - Delaware = higher litigation frequency but predictable
   - Nevada/Texas = potentially lower exposure
   - Track reincorporation proposals as potential signal of governance concerns
   - Severity: LOW-MEDIUM (structural rather than event-driven)

7. **Tariff/Supply Chain Exposure Flag**
   - Percentage of revenue/COGS from tariff-affected countries
   - Supply chain concentration in single geography
   - Disclosure quality around trade policy risk
   - Severity: MEDIUM (rising with 2025-2026 tariff escalation)

8. **Concentration Risk Indicator**
   - Market share dominance (CrowdStrike pattern)
   - Customer concentration
   - Product concentration
   - Geographic revenue concentration
   - Severity: MEDIUM (event-driven risk amplifier)

#### EXISTING CHECKS TO RECALIBRATE:

9. **Derivative Suit Risk Weight** -- INCREASE
   - Current weighting likely underestimates derivative risk
   - 13 of 18 settlements >$100M in last 5 years
   - Caremark claims expanding to cybersecurity, AI governance, product safety
   - Recommendation: Increase derivative risk weight by 30-50%

10. **Industry Baseline Risk** -- RECALIBRATE
    - Technology + Healthcare = 57% of all filings
    - Healthcare products subsector up 45% in 2025
    - Consumer non-cyclical up 16%
    - Financial companies (private credit) = emerging risk
    - Recommendation: Ensure sector scoring reflects 2024-2025 filing distributions

11. **DDL/Market Cap Severity** -- RECALIBRATE
    - Average DDL nearly doubled vs. historical norm
    - Mega-cap filings driving record DDL
    - Settlement/DDL ratio stable at ~1.1%
    - Recommendation: Update DDL benchmarks using 2024-2025 data

12. **Filing Survival Rate** -- ADD TO SCORING
    - 57-66% of cases are DISMISSED at motion to dismiss
    - Cases that survive MTD settle for significantly more
    - Recommendation: Add "MTD survival probability" factor based on allegation type
    - Accounting fraud: Higher survival rate
    - Event-driven (no fraud): Lower survival rate
    - AI/cyber allegations: Still being established

Based on 2024-2025 data, recommended scoring weight changes:

| Factor | Current Direction | Recommended Change | Rationale |

| Short seller activity | Likely exists | INCREASE weight | Strongest leading indicator |

| Derivative risk | Likely underweighted | INCREASE 30-50% | 13/18 mega settlements in 5 years |

### 8.4 Temporal Patterns to Track

1. **Claim maturation cycle**: Average time from triggering event to SCA filing is shortening (often days to weeks)
2. **Settlement lag**: Average time from filing to settlement remains 3-5 years
3. **Exposure tail**: Major cases (Boeing) can persist 5+ years
4. **Seasonal patterns**: Q4 earnings season (Oct-Nov) and annual report filing season (Feb-Mar) see filing spikes
5. **Short seller timing**: Reports often timed to coincide with options expiration or earnings
