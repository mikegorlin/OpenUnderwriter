Key audit questions per factor:
- **F1 (20pts, Prior Litigation)**: Is 20% the right weight? NERA data shows prior-SCA companies have 4.2x filing rate -- does 20 max points adequately capture this? Is the decay curve (20->18->15->10 over time) calibrated to recidivism data?
- **F2 (15pts, Stock Decline)**: The 20% threshold for "Normal" -- is this too generous? Stanford SCAC data shows median class period return of -35% to -50%. Are the insider amplifier multipliers (1.5x-2.5x) calibrated to scienter evidence?
- **F3 (12pts, Restatement/Audit)**: Historical lift of 4.5x is highest -- should max_points be higher than 12? Material weakness at only 5 points -- is this enough given MW-to-restatement correlation?
- **F4 (10pts, IPO/SPAC/M&A)**: SPAC litigation peaked 2021-2023 -- should this decay? IPO vulnerability window of 18 months -- is this supported by filing data?
- **F5 (10pts, Guidance Misses)**: 4+ misses = 8 points. Is the 8-quarter window the right lookback? Do serial missers have demonstrably higher claim rates?
- **F6 (8pts, Short Interest)**: Sector-relative approach is correct. Is 8 max points enough given short seller report correlation with litigation?
- **F7 (9pts, Volatility)**: Is volatility a leading indicator or lagging? Should it have more/fewer points than F6?
- **F8 (8pts, Financial Distress)**: Going concern at 6 points + CRF-004 ceiling -- is this double-counting or appropriate layering?
- **F9 (6pts, Governance)**: Historical lift of only 1.3x -- is 6 points too many? Or is governance under-weighted because it's hard to measure?
- **F10 (2pts, Officer Stability)**: Is 2 points enough for officer turnover as a leading indicator?

**2. Tier Boundary Audit**
For each tier (WIN/WANT/WRITE/WATCH/WALK/NO_TOUCH):

- Are the probability ranges (e.g., WALK = 15-20%) consistent with industry filing rates?
- Calibration recommendations

**3. Red Flag Gate Audit (11 CRFs)**
For each CRF in red_flags.json:
- Current ceiling (WALK vs WATCH)
- Justification: Why this ceiling level? Reference claims data on outcomes when this condition exists
- Assessment: Are there missing red flags? Are any gates too harsh or too lenient?
- Specific concern: CRF-001 (Active SCA) caps at WALK/30 -- should this be NO_TOUCH? Or is the current "prior carrier's problem" logic for new business correct?

**4. Governance Weights Audit**
For each of the 7 dimensions in governance_weights.json:
- Current weight and threshold
- ISS/Glass Lewis research supporting these governance quality indicators
- Assessment: Are the weights balanced? Is say_on_pay at 15% appropriate given Say-on-Pay advisory vote correlation with litigation?

**5. Sector Baselines Audit**
For each metric in sectors.json (short interest, volatility, leverage, cash runway, guidance, insider trading):
- Current baselines by sector
- Data source verification (are the sources cited still current?)
- Assessment: Are there sectors with outdated baselines? Any missing sectors?
- Specific concern: claim_base_rates marked "NEEDS CALIBRATION" -- provide calibrated values based on latest Cornerstone/NERA data

**6. Calibration Recommendations Summary**

- Priority (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW)

- Rationale for the change
- Expected impact on tier distribution

The document should be thorough but practical. Use tables for structured comparisons. Reference specific research publications where possible (NERA 2024 Full-Year Review, Cornerstone 2024 Securities Class Action Filings, Stanford SCAC Annual Report, Aon D&O Market Update, Marsh D&O Pricing Benchmarking).

IMPORTANT: This is a knowledge audit document. Use your training knowledge about D&O insurance industry research, securities litigation statistics, and underwriting practices.
