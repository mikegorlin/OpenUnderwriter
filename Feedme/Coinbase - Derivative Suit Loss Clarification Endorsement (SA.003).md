# DERIVATIVE SUIT LOSS CLARIFICATION ENDORSEMENT

**Policy Form: SA.003 (0208 Ed.) Side-A Directors and Officers Liability Insurance Policy**

This endorsement amends the **Side-A Directors and Officers Liability Insurance Policy** to which it is attached.

It is understood and agreed that this Policy is amended as follows:

**1.** With respect to any **Claim** that constitutes a shareholder derivative action brought on behalf of or in the right of the **Company**, any **Loss** consisting of settlements, judgments, or other monetary amounts paid or payable to or on behalf of the **Company** by or on behalf of any **Insured Person** in connection with such **Claim** shall not be deemed paid as indemnification or advancement by the **Company** for purposes of Section I (Insuring Agreement) of this Policy, and shall not be deemed indemnification or advancement to which an **Insured Person** may be entitled for purposes of Section IV.D of this Policy.

**2.** The determination under Paragraph 1 above shall apply irrespective of:

(a) the **Company's** state or jurisdiction of incorporation, formation, domestication, or conversion;

(b) whether applicable law permits, requires, or prohibits the **Company** from indemnifying or advancing to the **Insured Person** such **Loss**; or

(c) whether the **Company** has adopted or maintained provisions in its certificate of incorporation, bylaws, or other governing documents authorizing indemnification of **Insured Persons** for such **Loss**.

**3.** For the avoidance of doubt:

(a) This endorsement applies solely to settlements, judgments, and other monetary amounts in derivative actions as described herein, and does not alter the treatment of **Costs of Defense** or any other category of **Loss** under this Policy; and

(b) No demand upon or refusal by the **Company** to indemnify or advance shall be required as a condition precedent to coverage under this Policy for the **Loss** described in Paragraph 1.

All other terms, conditions, and limitations of this Policy remain unchanged.

## Drafting Notes (Internal — Not Part of Endorsement)

### Why This Endorsement Is Needed on the SA.003

1. **No "Non-Indemnifiable Loss" defined term.** Unlike Chubb or AIG Side A DIC forms, the SA.003 does not define "Non-Indemnifiable Loss." Coverage triggers via the Insuring Agreement's exclusion of Loss "paid as indemnification or advancement." The endorsement works within this architecture.

2. **Section IV.D "entitled to" language.** The excess clause states coverage is excess over "all indemnification and advancement to which an Insured Person may be entitled." In a permissive indemnification state (Texas), a carrier could argue the individual is "entitled" to indemnification even if the company hasn't paid. Paragraph 1 neutralizes this argument for derivative settlements/judgments.

3. **Redomestication context.** Coinbase converted from Delaware (non-permissive — derivative settlements not indemnifiable) to Texas (permissive — derivative settlements indemnifiable under TBOC Chapter 8). Paragraph 2(a) covers all methods of changing state of organization, including statutory conversion.

4. **Defense cost carve-out.** Paragraph 3(a) ensures derivative defense costs (defined as "Costs of Defense" in Section II.E, included in "Loss" under Section II.M) continue to flow through corporate indemnification/Side B. The endorsement only addresses the settlement/judgment piece.

5. **No condition precedent.** Paragraph 3(b) prevents a chicken-and-egg scenario in permissive states where the carrier demands the company first formally refuse to indemnify before Side A responds.

### Key SA.003 Defined Terms Used
- **Claim** — Section II.B
- **Company** — Section II.C (Corporation + Subsidiaries)
- **Loss** — Section II.M (includes Costs of Defense)
- **Insured Person** — Section II.K
- **Costs of Defense** — Section II.E
- **Insurance Program** — Section II.J
