# Stanford Securities Litigation Analytics (SSLA) - Biopharma Data (Feb 2018)
Source: https://sla.law.stanford.edu/news/biopharma

## Key Findings (2011-2017, firms under $1B market cap):

### Filing Volume:
- 183 lawsuits against biopharma (17% of all filings 2011-2017)
- Biopharma ranks #2 by sector for absolute filing volume (after IT)
- 116 class actions filed against small biopharma firms specifically

### Allegation Types:
- Only 6% involve accounting-related misstatements
- 89% of non-accounting cases involve drug development misstatements
- Drug development cases allege: general operations, safety/efficacy, regulatory compliance

### Stock Drops (Figure 4):
- Biopharma stock drops are SUBSTANTIALLY LARGER than non-biopharma
- Mean and median drops in biopharma cases are substantially larger than other sectors
- This is critical for severity assessment

### Dismissal Rates (Figure 5):
- 511 cases resolved total (100 biopharma, 411 non-biopharma)
- Biopharma dismissal rate: 62%
- Non-biopharma dismissal rate: 46%
- Biopharma dismissed at MUCH higher rate

### Settlement Data (Figure 6):
- Mean settlements: essentially the SAME for biopharma vs non-biopharma
- Median settlements: SUBSTANTIALLY DIFFERENT (biopharma lower)
- Non-biopharma settlements more skewed toward higher amounts
- Lower variance in biotech settlements = more predictable for insurers
- As % of stock drop: biopharma settlements are SLIGHTLY SMALLER

### Settlement Timing (Figure 7):
- Among cases NOT dismissed, biopharma settlements occur LATER
- Higher rate of biopharma settlements in discovery phase
- Defendants resist early settlement hoping for dismissal
- This means higher defense costs when cases survive

### Implied Settlement Rate:
- Biopharma firms face settlements at 8% rate over 7 years
- Non-biopharma firms face settlements at 7% rate over 7 years
- Nearly identical when probability-adjusted!

### Key Insight for Underwriters:
"The lower variance of biotech settlements suggests that insurers may be able to predict the cost of biotech insurance policies more accurately than they can predict others."
