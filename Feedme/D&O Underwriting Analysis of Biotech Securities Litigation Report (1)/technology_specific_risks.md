# Technology-Specific D&O Risk Data

## Gene Therapy Specific Risks
### Clinical Holds - The Unique Gene Therapy Risk
- Gene therapy clinical holds are a UNIQUE risk not present in traditional small molecule development
- Wills et al. (2023, PMC): Clinical holds can delay and disrupt cell and gene therapy development programs and may produce long-lasting adverse financial effects for sponsors
- Rocket Pharmaceuticals (2025): FDA put clinical hold on Phase 2 study after patient adverse event. Stock crashed 62%, $1.6B market value destroyed. Securities class action filed.
- Intellia Therapeutics (2026): FDA clinical hold on late-stage gene therapy trial
- REGENXBIO: Gene therapy clinical hold led to securities litigation scrutiny

### Gene Therapy D&O Risk Factors:
1. Clinical holds can occur at ANY phase (not just Phase II/III)
2. Safety signals (immunogenicity, insertional mutagenesis) can emerge LATE
3. Manufacturing/CMC issues unique to gene therapy (viral vector production)
4. Durability of response is unknown - creates post-approval litigation risk
5. High price points ($1M-$3M per treatment) create reimbursement/commercial risk

## SPAC-Related Biotech Litigation
### Cornerstone Research Data:
- SPAC case median settlement: $12.0 million
- SPAC case average settlement: $16.7 million
- 21% and 66% SMALLER than median and average for non-SPAC cases respectively

### Key SPAC Biotech Cases:
- Ginkgo Bioworks: Sued after short-seller attack post-SPAC merger (2021)
- Clover Health: Multiple D&O claims including securities class action and derivative suits post-merger
- De-SPAC companies face DUAL exposure: both pre-merger and post-merger claims

### SPAC D&O Insurance Considerations:
- D&O coverage is changing with rise in SPAC-related litigation
- Coordinating coverage between SPAC sponsor entity and target company is crucial
- Run-off coverage for SPAC entity vs. go-forward coverage for merged entity

## Medical Device Specific Risks
### Regulatory Pathways Create Different Risk Profiles:
- 510(k) pathway: Lower regulatory risk but potential for post-market safety issues
- PMA pathway: Higher regulatory risk, more similar to drug approval
- De Novo pathway: Moderate risk, novel device category

### Device-Specific Litigation Triggers:
1. FDA warning letters
2. Product recalls (Class I, II, III)
3. Post-market surveillance failures
4. Manufacturing quality issues (483 observations)

## Biosimilar/BPCIA Risks
- Patent dance litigation under BPCIA creates unique D&O exposure
- Interchangeability designation disputes
- Market share erosion claims for reference biologic companies

## SCA Filing Trends (ainvest.com, Nov 2025):
- SCAs surged 22% in 2024
- Driven by regulatory misrepresentation claims (34%)
- Pre-approval phase risks (57%)

## Sidley Austin Key Insight:
"Securities class actions against life sciences companies are mostly second-order problems. The first-order problem is a business or regulatory setback."
