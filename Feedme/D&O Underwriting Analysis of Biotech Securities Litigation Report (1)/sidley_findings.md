# Sidley Austin - Securities Litigation Against Life Sciences Companies: 2024

## Key Statistics (from GoodLifeSci blog post, April 4, 2025)

### Dismissal Success Rate
- 2024: Life sciences companies succeeded in 59% of motions to dismiss (24 of 41 decisions)
- Consistent with past trends: fluctuated in 50-60% range historically

### New Filings
- 44 new class actions against publicly traded life sciences companies in 2024
- Upward trend from 2023 (34 new class actions) and 2022 (37 new class actions)
- COVID-19 related filings dropped to 2 in 2024

### Pre-Approval vs Mature Products
- Pre-approval cases: companies prevailed in 57% (13 of 23)
- Mature product cases: companies prevailed in 61% (11 of 18)
- NOTE: In most years, companies fare BETTER in pre-approval cases than mature products
- 2024 was a departure from this trend

### Key Litigation Themes
1. Securities class actions are "second-order problems" - first-order is business/regulatory setback
2. Under PSLRA, heightened pleading standards for fraud claims
3. Scienter requirement: more than just knowledge of contrary facts - must show intent to defraud
4. Interim FDA communications: safer to discuss clinical trial results generally than characterize FDA's position
5. Forms 483: no standalone duty to disclose, but must review/revise risk disclosures after receiving
6. Product launch cases: mixed results, coverage hurdles as key issue

### Geographic Concentration
- Cases concentrated in three regions (Ninth Circuit/California prominent)

### Appellate Results
- Companies won affirmance of dismissal in ALL 8 appellate cases in 2024

## Additional Key Findings from 2024 Report

### Five Takeaways from New Complaints Filed in 2024
1. 44 new class actions filed in 2024 (up from 34 in 2023, 37 in 2022)
2. COVID-19 filings dropped to just 2 in 2024
3. Geographic concentration: 14 in Ninth Circuit (CA), 9 in Second Circuit (NY), 8 in First Circuit (MA); far fewer in Third Circuit
4. 19 of 44 new cases (43%) against mature products companies (down from 53% in 2023)
5. Majority of mature product cases arise from sales performance setbacks, not regulatory issues

### Key Litigation Themes for D&O Underwriting
- Pre-approval vs mature: companies historically fare BETTER in pre-approval cases (inherently unknowable outcomes)
- NDA/BLA pending: companies safer discussing clinical trial results generally; dangerous to characterize FDA's position
- Forms 483: no standalone duty to disclose, but must revise risk disclosures after receiving
- Scienter: requires intent to defraud, not just knowledge of contrary facts
- Product launch: coverage hurdles as key risk area

### Historical Filing Trends (from NERA/Cornerstone data)
- 229 total federal SCA filings in 2024
- Life sciences represent ~20-22% of all SCA filings
- Annualized litigation rate ~3.8% for US exchange-listed companies
