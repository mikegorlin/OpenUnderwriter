# PLUS/BakerHostetler Biotech Securities Claims Myths Study (2005-2022)

## Source: D&O Diary Guest Post, July 19, 2022
Authors: Doug Greene (BakerHostetler), Genevieve York-Erwin (BakerHostetler), Mike Tomasulo (Baldwin Risk Partners)

## Study Methodology
- Reviewed ALL district court decisions on motions to dismiss biotech securities cases
- Criteria: development-stage biotech companies bringing first drug/device to market
- Only cases where alleged false/misleading statements concerned clinical trials or FDA approval process
- Period: 2005-2022

## Key Statistics
- 2005-2017: 70 decisions, 68.6% resulted in complete dismissals
- 2012-2017: 76% dismissal rate (higher than earlier period)
- 2005-2011: 56% dismissal rate
- 2017-2022 update: 44 additional cases, 68.2% complete dismissals
- Overall: ~68% dismissal rate for development-stage biotech cases

## Four Myths Debunked

### Myth #1: Cases against biotech companies for failed clinical trials are risky and expensive
FACT: These cases are LESS risky than average securities class actions.
- ~2/3 dismissed in full
- Structural elements make them less expensive to defend
- Typically one corrective disclosure (single event)
- Small number of employees = fewer witnesses/documents
- Key witnesses are doctors/scientists (sympathetic)
- Excellent candidates for trial if case survives

### Myth #2: Management puts company at risk speaking positively about clinical trial expectations
FACT: Statements of opinion are protected under Omnicare (2015)
- Forward-looking statements protected by PSLRA safe harbor
- Optimistic statements about FDA approval prospects generally defensible
- Key: avoid characterizing FDA's position directly

### Myth #3: Biotech companies are uniquely vulnerable to securities fraud claims
FACT: Data shows biotech companies are actually MORE likely to win dismissal
- 68% dismissal rate vs lower rates for general securities class actions
- Inherent uncertainty of drug development actually helps defense (no scienter)

### Myth #4: D&O insurance for biotech is necessarily expensive due to high risk
FACT: Risk is manageable and often overpriced relative to actual claims experience
- Insurers have traditionally overestimated the risk
- Better understanding of claims data should lead to more competitive pricing

## Key Drivers of Dismissal Success
1. Falsity: Courts recognize that clinical trial outcomes are inherently uncertain
2. Scienter: Difficult for plaintiffs to prove executives knew statements were false when outcomes unknowable
3. Loss causation: Single corrective disclosure simplifies analysis
4. PSLRA safe harbor: Forward-looking statements with meaningful cautionary language protected
5. Omnicare: Opinion statements protected unless plaintiff shows speaker didn't hold opinion or omitted known contrary facts
