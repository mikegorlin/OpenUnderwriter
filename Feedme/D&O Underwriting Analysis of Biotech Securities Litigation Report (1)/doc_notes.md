### Page 19 (Slide 19): Title - "Matrixx Decision"

### Page 20 (Slide 20): Matrixx Decision
- Background: Carter-Wallace Inc, Securities Litigation (2nd Cir 1988) - bright line rule: failure to disclose adverse drug effects not material unless statistically significant
- April 2004: shareholders sued Matrixx (Zicam cold remedy / anosmia link)
- Lower court relied on Carter-Wallace, dismissed for lack of statistical significance
- 9th Circuit reversed: rejected statistical significance standard; scienter can be raised as "extreme departure from standards of ordinary care"
- Supreme Court Ruling (March 22, 2011): unanimously affirmed 9th Circuit
  - Medical professionals and FDA can rely on evidence not statistically significant
  - Company omitted info not because of statistical significance but because of market effect
  - Typo: "asses" should be "assess"

### Page 21 (Slide 21): Matrixx Decision (continued)
- Conclusion: Supreme Court ruling makes it harder to dismiss securities claims based on company decisions about what adverse effects to disclose
