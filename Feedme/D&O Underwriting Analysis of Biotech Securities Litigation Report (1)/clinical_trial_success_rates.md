# Clinical Trial Success Rates Data for D&O Underwriting

## Source: CCRPS 2026-27 Analysis (compiled from BIO, Nature, IQVIA datasets)

### Key Phase Transition Insight for Underwriters
Phase I fails on tolerability/PK. Phase II is the "graveyard" - where weak biology and weak endpoints get exposed. Phase III fails when effect size collapses at scale or safety risk becomes non-negotiable.

### Therapeutic Area Risk Map (for D&O underwriting purposes)

| Therapeutic Area | Overall LOA | Primary Bottleneck | D&O Risk Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oncology (solid tumors) | Single-digit overall | Phase II | HIGHEST stock drop risk - frequent failures, large patient populations |
| Oncology (hematologic) | Higher than solid tumors | Phase II/III | Moderate - clearer endpoints reduce surprise |
| CNS (Alzheimer's/neurodegeneration) | Historically LOW | Phase II and III | VERY HIGH - massive investment, frequent failures, huge stock drops |
| CNS (psychiatry) | Depressed by placebo response | Phase II | HIGH - placebo response creates "surprise" failures |
| Pain | Often lower | Phase II | HIGH - endpoint noise creates unexpected failures |
| Hepatology (NASH/MASH) | Historically difficult | Phase II/III | HIGH - endpoint controversy, regulatory uncertainty |
| Immunology (RA/IBD/psoriasis) | Moderate | Phase II | MODERATE - established endpoints |
| Cardiovascular (chronic) | Moderate but expensive | Phase III | MODERATE-HIGH - Phase III failures are expensive |
| Metabolic (type 2 diabetes) | Higher than average | Phase III | LOWER - established endpoints and comparators |
| Rare disease (non-oncology) | Often above average | Phase III | MODERATE - smaller patient pools, but orphan drug advantages |
| Cell therapy | High upside, manufacturing risk | Phase I/II | HIGH - manufacturing failures, novel modality uncertainty |
| Gene therapy | Durability + safety dependent | Phase III/post-approval | VERY HIGH - safety signals can emerge late, durability unknown |
| Vaccines | Higher when correlates clear | Phase II/III | MODERATE - but pandemic-era scrutiny increased litigation risk |
| Dermatology | Moderate to higher | Phase II | LOWER - established endpoints |
| Obesity/weight management | Operationally feasible | Phase III | MODERATE - safety-sensitive, long-term monitoring |

## Source: BIO Clinical Development Success Rates 2011-2020

### Phase Transition Probabilities (All Indications)
- Phase I to Phase II: ~52%
- Phase II to Phase III: ~28.9% (THE KEY FAILURE POINT)
- Phase III to NDA/BLA: ~57.8%
- NDA/BLA to Approval: ~90.6%
- Overall LOA (Phase I to Approval): ~7.9%

### By Modality (from BIO and Nature 2025 data):
- Small molecules: ~7-10% overall LOA
- Biologics (monoclonal antibodies): ~12-15% overall LOA
- Gene therapies: LOWER phase transition success (38.5% Phase I to II for ex vivo per Elsallab 2024)
- Cell therapies: Variable, manufacturing-dependent
- ADCs (antibody-drug conjugates): Improving but historically lower
- mRNA therapeutics: Rapidly evolving, limited long-term data

### By Disease Prevalence:
- Orphan/rare disease drugs: Often HIGHER success rates (regulatory advantages, unmet need, smaller trials)
- Non-oncology orphan drugs: Biologics showed higher approval rates; gene therapies showed lower phase transition success (Parker 2025)

## Source: IQVIA Global Trends in R&D 2024
- Composite success rate jumped to 10.8% in 2023 across all therapy areas
- After falling to 10-year low in 2022
- Driven by improvements in oncology and rare disease

## Source: Nature (Zhou 2025) - Dynamic Clinical Trial Success Rates
- Huge variation: 7% to 20% in reported clinical trial success rates among existing studies
- Variation driven by methodology differences, time periods, and therapeutic area mix

## KEY UNDERWRITING IMPLICATION:
The phase of development AND therapeutic area together determine the probability of a stock-drop event. A Phase II oncology company has fundamentally different risk than a Phase III rare disease company. Underwriters who treat "biotech" as a monolithic category are mispricing risk in both directions.
