# BHSI / Risk & Insurance: Navigating Shareholder Litigation Risk in Life Sciences
## Source: Risk & Insurance, March 2, 2026 (Sponsored by Berkshire Hathaway Specialty Insurance)

### Key Inflection Points for D&O Exposure:
1. Pivotal trial readouts
2. NDA/BLA submissions
3. FDA decision dates (PDUFA dates)
4. ANY phase transition (Phase I to II, Phase II to III)
5. Transition from development-stage to commercial-stage company

### The Development-to-Commercial Transition Risk:
"The shift from a development-stage company to a commercial-stage company can be a particularly delicate period for many biotech firms. After spending a decade focused almost exclusively on research and development, they suddenly must build capabilities in sales and marketing, bring products to market, and engage insurers to secure reimbursement." - Marincel (BHSI)

### Regulatory Uncertainty as D&O Risk Driver:
"As guidance, leadership, and priorities have shifted, it's become more difficult to forecast whether drugs will be approved, whether approvals will happen on time, or whether there will be complications." - Marincel (BHSI)

FDA has maintained PDUFA date commitments despite workforce reductions, but standards and data required for approval have become less predictable.

### Disclosure Discipline as Risk Mitigation:
Key risk mitigation practices:
1. Consistent, balanced communications across ALL channels (press releases, earnings calls, investor presentations, regulatory filings)
2. Common pitfall: different channels telling slightly different stories
3. Risk factor disclosures should be UPDATED to reflect current uncertainties, not static boilerplate
4. "Companies should avoid using risk factors that everyone else uses in their public filings"
5. Books and records demands should be treated as early warning signals

### Insurance Considerations:
Not all D&O coverage is created equal for life sciences. Carriers with deep sector expertise provide value beyond just coverage.
