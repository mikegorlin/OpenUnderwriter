# Fact-Checking Findings for Swiss Re Document

## 1. Carter-Wallace Case
- Swiss Re doc says: "Carter-Wallace Inc, Securities Litigation (2nd Cir 1988)"
- ACTUAL: In Re Carter-Wallace, Inc. Securities Litigation, 150 F.3d 153 (2d Cir. **1998**), NOT 1988
- ERROR: The year is wrong by a decade. The case was decided July 13, 1998.
- The case involved Felbatol (epilepsy drug) and adverse side effects
- The Second Circuit actually DID NOT establish a bright-line statistical significance rule - it was more nuanced. The document's characterization may be oversimplified.

## 2. Matrixx Initiatives v. Siracusano
- Swiss Re doc says: Supreme Court ruling March 22, 2011 - CORRECT
- Unanimous decision - CORRECT
- Zicam cold remedy / anosmia link - CORRECT
- 9th Circuit reversed district court - CORRECT
- Swiss Re doc says lawsuit filed "April 2004" - need to verify; the class period and filing dates vary
- The Matrixx decision is correctly characterized in substance

## 3. FDA NDA Review Timeline
- Swiss Re doc says: "By law the FDA can take up to 6 months to review the NDA"
- ACTUAL per FDA/PDUFA:
  - Standard review: 10 months from receipt of NDA
  - Priority review: 6 months from receipt of NDA
- ERROR: The document states "up to 6 months" which is only correct for priority review, not standard review. Most NDAs receive standard review (10 months).

## 4. IND vs NDA Confusion
- Swiss Re doc says: "After preclinical testing, the company files an NDA with the FDA"
- ACTUAL: After preclinical testing, the company files an **IND** (Investigational New Drug application)
- ERROR: NDA (New Drug Application) is filed AFTER clinical trials are complete, not before
- This is a fundamental regulatory error that would mislead underwriters

## 5. Genentech/Roche Acquisition
- Swiss Re doc says: "Genentech purchased by Roche in 2009" - CORRECT
- Roche acquired outstanding public shares for $95/share, ~$46.8 billion total
- Completed March 2009

## 6. Novo Nordisk as "Largest Independent Biotech"
- Swiss Re doc states Novo Nordisk market cap ~$76.9B as "largest independent biotech"
- ISSUES:
  - Novo Nordisk is primarily classified as a PHARMACEUTICAL company, not a biotech
  - It is Danish (headquartered in Denmark), not typically categorized as "independent biotech"
  - At time of document creation, this may have been debatable, but the classification is questionable
  - As of 2026, Novo Nordisk market cap is ~$171.6B
  - Traditional "largest independent biotech" labels went to companies like Amgen, Gilead, or Regeneron
  - The document appears outdated in its market cap figure

## 7. Orphan Drug Definition
- Swiss Re doc says: "rare diseases (<200,000 individuals)" - CORRECT
- Orphan Drug Act: disease affecting fewer than 200,000 persons in the US
