# Sidley Austin 2024 Life Sciences Securities Litigation Report - Detailed Findings

## Source: Sidley Austin GoodLifeSci Blog, April 4, 2025

### Core Framework
"Securities class actions against life sciences companies are mostly second-order problems. The first-order problem is a business or regulatory setback that, when disclosed by the company or a third party, triggers a stock price decline."

### Filing Statistics (2024)
- 44 new class actions against publicly traded life sciences companies in 2024
- Up from 34 in 2023 and 37 in 2022 (upward trend)
- COVID-19 related filings tailed off: only 2 in 2024 (down from 6-7 per year in 2020-2022)

### Geographic Concentration
- 14 new cases in Ninth Circuit (California)
- 9 new cases in Second Circuit (New York)
- 8 new cases in First Circuit (Massachusetts)
- Only 3 in Third Circuit (far fewer than previous years)

### Company Type Analysis (CRITICAL FOR UNDERWRITING)
- 19 of 44 new cases (43%) filed against companies with MATURE PRODUCTS
- Slight decline from 53% in 2023, but higher than earlier years
- Majority of mature product cases arise from setbacks NOT unique to life sciences (principally sales performance)

### Dismissal Rate Data
- Overall: 59% success rate for defendants in 2024 (24 of 41 decisions)
- Consistent with past years: fluctuated in 50-60% range
- Appellate courts: Companies won affirmance in ALL 8 cases with appellate rulings

### PRE-APPROVAL vs. MATURE PRODUCT DISMISSAL RATES (2024)
- Pre-approval companies: 57% dismissal rate (13 of 23)
- Mature product companies: 61% dismissal rate (11 of 18)
- NOTE: This REVERSES the typical trend where pre-approval companies fare BETTER
- Typical trend reflects "plaintiffs' challenges in establishing falsity and scienter in cases where the statements they attack concern inherently unknowable events—the outcome of trials and the FDA approval process"

### KEY SUBSTANTIVE TAKEAWAYS

#### 1. Interim FDA Communications (NDA/BLA Pending)
Five cases involved companies receiving negative feedback while NDA/BLA was pending.
- SAFER GROUND: Discussing clinical trial results, strength of application, approval prospects generally
- DANGEROUS TERRITORY: Directly characterizing the FDA's position, saying FDA is "aligned" with company views
- KEY RULE: "If at all possible, companies should avoid direct characterization of the FDA's position or communications"

#### 2. Forms 483 Disclosure
Six decisions involved Forms 483 (FDA inspection observations).
- No standalone duty to disclose a Form 483
- BUT company faces peril if it does NOT review and revise risk disclosures after receiving a 483
- Must update routine statements about contingent events

#### 3. Product Launch Cases
Three decisions on setbacks at product launch stage (coverage hurdles).
- Mixed results: Companies prevailed in 2 of 3
- Biogen/Aduhelm: Suffered reversal in litigation arising from controversial Alzheimer's drug launch

#### 4. Scienter Analysis (CRITICAL FOR UNDERWRITING)
Three decisions: Courts found adequate falsity allegations BUT dismissed on scienter grounds.
- KEY HOLDING: Awareness of contrary facts is NOT enough for scienter
- "Defendants may be aware of problems with a product but still believe that their optimistic statements about it are true—because they have confidence the problems can be addressed"
- This is a POWERFUL defense for biotech companies

- 2022: 37 filings
- 2020-2022: Elevated by COVID-19 cases (6-7 per year)
