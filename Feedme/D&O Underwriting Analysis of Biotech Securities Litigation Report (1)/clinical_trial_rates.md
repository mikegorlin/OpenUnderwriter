# Clinical Trial Success Rates (BIO/Informa Study 2011-2020)

## Phase Transition Success Rates (from BIO study and other sources)
- Phase I to Phase II: ~52% (varies by therapeutic area)
- Phase II to Phase III: ~28.9% (this is the highest failure point)
- Phase III to NDA/BLA: ~57.8%
- NDA/BLA to Approval: ~90.6%
- Overall LOA (Phase I to Approval): ~7.9% (all indications)
- Overall success rate: ~10-15% (drug development overall)

## Key Facts for D&O Underwriting Context
- ~90% of investigational drugs fail overall
- Phase II is the most critical failure point (~70% failure rate)
- Phase III failures are most costly and most likely to trigger securities litigation
- Oncology has lower success rates than average
- Rare/orphan diseases tend to have higher approval rates
- Biomarker-selected trials have higher success rates

## Typical Timelines
- Preclinical: 3-6 years
- Phase I: 1-2 years, 20-100 participants
- Phase II: 1-3 years, 100-500 participants
- Phase III: 2-4 years, 1,000-5,000+ participants
- FDA Review: 10 months (standard) or 6 months (priority)
- Total from discovery to approval: 10-15 years average
