# PART 1: COREWEAVE ISSUES EXPLAINED

## **What Is CoreWeave?**

CoreWeave is an AI infrastructure company that provides GPU-accelerated cloud computing for AI workloads. 

**Business Model**:
1. Borrow money at high interest rates (debt financing)
2. Use borrowed money to buy Nvidia AI chips
3. Install chips in data centers leased from third-party landlords
4. Rent access to chips to AI companies (OpenAI, Microsoft, Meta)

**IPO**: March 2025 at $40/share

---

## **The CoreWeave Collapse: What Happened**

### **Timeline**:
- **March 2025**: IPO at $40/share
- **June 2025**: Stock peaks at $187 (+368% from IPO)
- **October 2025**: Attempted $9B acquisition of Core Scientific (landlord) → Shareholders reject deal
- **November 10, 2025**: Confusing earnings call about construction delays → Stock drops 16.3% next day
- **December 2025**: Stock at ~$73 (-61% from June peak)

### **Value Destroyed**: 
- **$33 billion in 6 weeks** [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025] (partially verified - WSJ only)
- **Stock down 60-62% from June peak** [Forbes, CNBC, Economic Times - VERIFIED 3 sources]

---

## **CoreWeave's Five Fatal Flaws**

### **Flaw #1: Debt-Financed Infrastructure Without Proven Revenue**

**The Problem**:
- **Operating margin**: 4% [WSJ, Forbes, Economic Times - VERIFIED 3 sources]
- **Interest on debt**: 8-10%+ (inferred from "less than half" statement)
- **Result**: **Losing money on every dollar of debt**

**The Numbers**:
- Q3 2025 revenue: $1.4 billion [WSJ, Yahoo Finance - VERIFIED 2 sources]
- Q3 2025 loss: $110 million [WSJ only - PARTIALLY VERIFIED]
- **Profit margin**: -7.9% (unprofitable)

**Analyst Assessment**:
> "CoreWeave has the **'ugliest balance sheet in technology, by far'**" - Gil Luria, D.A. Davidson [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]

> "CoreWeave's operating margins of about 4% are less than half of what the company pays in interest on most of the debt it uses to deploy computing power for customers" - Gil Luria [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]

> "This is a company at scale. There is no scaling going on here." - Gil Luria [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]

**Why This Matters**:
- CoreWeave cannot service debt from operations
- Betting on future AI revenue to cover interest payments
- **This is the WorldCom parallel**: Borrowed heavily for infrastructure, couldn't service debt, went bankrupt

---

### **Flaw #2: Extreme Customer Concentration**

**The Problem**:
- **Microsoft**: 67% of Q3 2025 revenue [Fitch Ratings, Dec 10, 2025 - PARTIALLY VERIFIED]
- **Alternative data**: "Handful of large customers (OpenAI, Microsoft, Meta) for bulk of revenue" [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- **Denton data center** (260 megawatts): Dedicated to OpenAI [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]

**Why This Matters**:
- If Microsoft reduces spend → CoreWeave loses 67% of revenue
- If OpenAI delays/cancels Denton → 260 megawatts stranded
- **Existential risk** from single customer decisions

**What Happened**:
- Construction delays "very frustrating for our clients" (CEO's words) [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- Stock dropped 16.3% when delays announced [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- Market recognized customer concentration risk

---

### **Flaw #3: Failed Circular Deal (Acquisition of Landlord)**

**The Problem**:
- **October 2025**: CoreWeave attempted $9 billion acquisition of Core Scientific [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- **Core Scientific**: CoreWeave's primary data center landlord and builder
- **Circular structure**: CoreWeave pays Core Scientific for data centers → CoreWeave acquires Core Scientific → Revenue becomes internal

**What Happened**:
- Core Scientific shareholders **voted overwhelmingly to reject** the deal [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- **Two Seas Capital** (largest active shareholder) publicly opposed merger [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- **Reason**: "Exposed to the high volatility of CoreWeave's share price" and "substantial economic risk" [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]

**Why This Matters**:
- Even CoreWeave's own landlord saw CoreWeave as too risky
- Reveals that CoreWeave's business model requires circular structures to work
- **This is the Enron parallel**: Circular deals with SPEs to inflate revenue

**Market Reaction**:
- Stock fell more than 6% after deal fell apart [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]

---

### **Flaw #4: Construction Delays + Management Credibility Crisis**

**The Problem - Construction Delays**:
- **Summer 2025**: "Heavy rains and winds caused a roughly 60-day delay at a construction site in Denton, a small city north of Dallas" [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- **Additional delays**: "Revisions to design plans for some of the data centers a partner is building for CoreWeave in Texas and elsewhere" [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- **Completion date**: Pushed back "several months" [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]

**The Problem - Management Credibility**:
- **November 10, 2025 earnings call**: CEO Michael Intrator "delivered a confusing, contradictory message about the construction problems" [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- **CEO said**: "One data center" problem
- **CFO Nitin Agrawal corrected CEO on live call**: "One data-center **provider**" (meaning multiple data centers affected, not just one) [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- **Next day on CNBC**: CEO repeated incorrect "one data center" statement before host corrected him [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]

**Market Reaction**:
- Stock dropped 16.3% the day after earnings call [WSJ, Dec 15, 2025]
- Investors lost confidence in management's ability to execute

**Why This Matters**:
- **Physical constraints**: Weather, construction, design changes
- **Management can't keep story straight**: Raises questions about competence and transparency
- **Execution risk**: If CEO doesn't know how many data centers are delayed, what else doesn't he know?

---

### **Flaw #5: Pure-Play AI Bet (No Optionality)**

**The Problem**:
- **100% AI infrastructure** (no non-AI revenue)
- **Unprofitable** (lost $110M in Q3 2025)
- **Cannot pivot** (data centers built for AI workloads, can't easily convert to traditional cloud)

**Why This Matters**:
- If AI demand disappoints → CoreWeave has no fallback
- **This is the dot-com pure-play risk**: Pets.com, Webvan, eToys had no fallback when internet hype faded

**Market Validation**:
- When AI bubble concerns emerged (December 2025) → CoreWeave stock fell 60-62%
- **Proves weak optionality**: Company has no fallback when AI narrative weakens
