# Life Sciences Securities Litigation Patterns

## Source
Goodwin Law - Securities Litigation Against Life Sciences 2024 Year in Review

## Key Findings

### Common Allegations in Life Sciences Securities Cases

Securities cases against life sciences companies typically involve:

1. **Drug/Product Development Issues**
   - Announcements of issues related to company's drugs or products
   - Issues may involve negative feedback or actions from FDA
   - Delays, suspensions, or terminations of clinical trials
   - Unfavorable clinical data results
   - Adverse events affecting patients

2. **Product Issues**
   - Product recalls
   - Manufacturing or supply challenges

3. **Disclosure Failures**
   - False or misleading statements
   - Failed to disclose material information
   - Claims under Sections 10(b) and 20(a) of Securities Exchange Act of 1934
   - SEC Rule 10b-5 violations
   - Section 11, 12(a)(2), and 15 of Securities Act of 1933 (for registered securities offerings)

### Industry Context

- Life sciences companies remain the **biggest industry target** for federal securities class actions
- 2024 saw 40% above average filings since 1997
- Court rulings trend favorably for defendants - majority of cases dismissed
- About **50% of life sciences securities class actions are dismissed** by courts (long-term trend)

### Key Legal Distinctions

Courts distinguish between:
- **Pure opinions or predictions of the future** (tend to be nonactionable)
- **Statements of historical fact** (actionable if false/misleading)

### Survival Requirements

To survive challenges, companies and officers need to maintain care when speaking about:
- Ongoing activities (e.g., revenue trends, clinical trial results)
- Active discussions with FDA
- Active manufacturing challenges

### Dismissal Rates

- 15% of 2023 cases dismissed by end of 2024 (77.5% still pending)
- 42.2% of 2022 cases dismissed
- Dismissal rate consistent with long-term trend of ~50%

### Geographic Concentration

Most significant federal circuits for life sciences securities class actions:
- First Circuit (District of Massachusetts)
- Second Circuit (SDNY, EDNY)
- Third Circuit (D. New Jersey, E.D. Pennsylvania)
- Ninth Circuit (California District Courts)
- Second and Ninth circuits account for 61% of all core federal filings

## Underwriting Implications

### High-Risk Scenarios
1. Companies with ongoing clinical trials, especially late-stage trials
2. Companies with active FDA discussions/reviews
3. Companies making statements about ongoing revenue trends
4. Companies with manufacturing operations
5. Products with patient safety implications

### Moderate Risk Indicators
- Recent product launches
- Regulatory approval processes
- Supply chain dependencies
- Public statements about future performance

### Lower Risk (More Likely Dismissal)
- Pure forward-looking statements properly qualified
- Opinion statements about future outcomes
- Properly disclosed risks and uncertainties
