# D&O Underwriting Guide: A Data-Driven Framework

This guide provides a comprehensive framework for underwriting Directors and Officers (D&O) liability insurance for public companies. It is based on an analysis of 250 securities class action lawsuits and other D&O litigation over the past 20 years. The guide is designed to help underwriters identify and assess the key hazards that drive D&O claims, both in general and within specific industries.

## General Public D&O Hazards

This section details the most common hazards that apply to all public companies, regardless of industry. These are the foundational risks that every underwriter should consider when evaluating a D&O submission.

### 1. Financial Misrepresentation

This is the most common and significant hazard, accounting for a large portion of all D&O claims. It involves any misstatement or omission of material financial information that misleads investors about the company's performance or prospects.

| Sub-Risk | Case Frequency (out of 250) | Underwriting Questions & Information to Analyze |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Accounting Fraud & Internal Controls** | 35 | - Review the company's Form 10-K for any disclosed material weaknesses in internal controls.<br>- Scrutinize the company's accounting policies, particularly around revenue recognition, inventory valuation, and asset impairment.<br>- Inquire about any history of SEC investigations or enforcement actions related to accounting or financial reporting. |
| **Revenue & Demand Forecasts** | 28 | - Compare the company's historical forecasts to its actual results. Are the forecasts consistently optimistic?<br>- Analyze the company's sales pipeline and customer concentration. Is the company overly reliant on a small number of customers?<br>- Question the assumptions underlying the company's forecasts. Are they realistic and well-supported? |
| **Asset Impairment** | 12 | - Scrutinize the company's goodwill and intangible assets. Are they at risk of impairment?<br>- Inquire about the company's process for testing assets for impairment. Is it rigorous and well-documented?<br>- Analyze the company's M&A history. Has the company overpaid for acquisitions, creating a risk of future write-downs? |
| **Investment Fraud & Ponzi Schemes** | 8 | - Scrutinize the company's business model. Is it overly complex or difficult to understand?<br>- Inquire about the company's sources of funding. Is it reliant on a small number of investors or a constant influx of new capital?<br>- Analyze the company's marketing materials. Are they making unrealistic promises or guarantees? |

### 2. Corporate Governance

This hazard relates to the internal policies and procedures that govern how a company is managed. Weak corporate governance can lead to a wide range of problems, from accounting fraud to insider trading.

| Sub-Risk | Case Frequency (out of 250) | Underwriting Questions & Information to Analyze |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Insider Trading & Market Manipulation** | 15 | - Review the company's insider trading policy. Is it clear, comprehensive, and well-enforced?<br>- Analyze the trading activity of the company's executives and directors. Are there any unusual or suspicious trades?<br>- Inquire about any history of SEC investigations or enforcement actions related to insider trading. |
| **Breach of Fiduciary Duty** | 12 | - Scrutinize the company's related-party transactions. Are they fair to the company and its shareholders?<br>- Inquire about any conflicts of interest that may exist between the company's executives and directors and its shareholders.<br>- Analyze the company's executive compensation. Is it excessive or misaligned with the company's performance? |
| **Bribery & Corruption (FCPA)** | 8 | - Review the company's anti-corruption policy. Is it clear, comprehensive, and well-enforced?<br>- Inquire about the company's operations in high-risk jurisdictions. What steps is the company taking to mitigate the risk of corruption?<br>- Analyze the company's use of third-party agents and intermediaries. Are they properly vetted and monitored? |
| **Shareholder Activism & Proxy Fights** | 5 | - Analyze the company's shareholder base. Is it concentrated in a small number of institutional investors?<br>- Inquire about any history of shareholder activism or proxy fights.<br>- Review the company's corporate governance ratings. Are there any red flags? |

## Industry-Specific Hazards

This section provides a deep dive into the unique risks of each major industry. While the general hazards described above apply to all companies, these industry-specific risks require a more tailored underwriting approach.

### 1. Life Sciences (Pharmaceuticals, Biotechnology, Medical Devices)

The life sciences industry is a hotbed of D&O litigation, with a high frequency of claims related to clinical trials, product safety, and intellectual property.

| Sub-Risk | Case Frequency (out of 250) | Underwriting Questions & Information to Analyze |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Clinical Trial Disclosure Failures** | 45 | - Scrutinize the company's clinical trial protocols and data. Are they well-designed and statistically significant?<br>- Inquire about the company's communications with the FDA. Are there any disagreements or disputes?<br>- Analyze the company's public statements about its clinical trials. Are they consistent with the underlying data? |
| **Product Safety & Manufacturing Issues** | 20 | - Review the company's manufacturing processes and quality control procedures. Are they cGMP compliant?<br>- Inquire about any history of product recalls or warning letters from the FDA.<br>- Analyze the company's supply chain. Is it reliant on a small number of suppliers? |
| **Intellectual Property Disputes** | 10 | - Scrutinize the company's patent portfolio. Is it strong and defensible?<br>- Inquire about any history of patent litigation or other intellectual property disputes.<br>- Analyze the company's competitive landscape. Is it at risk of being sued for patent infringement? |
| **Anti-Competitive Practices** | 8 | - Review the company's pricing and marketing practices. Are they in compliance with antitrust laws?<br>- Inquire about any history of government investigations or enforcement actions related to anti-competitive practices.<br>- Analyze the company's market share. Is it dominant in any particular market? |

## Conclusion

This guide provides a data-driven framework for underwriting D&O liability insurance. By understanding the key hazards that drive D&O claims, underwriters can make more informed decisions and better protect their portfolios. However, it is important to remember that this guide is not a substitute for sound underwriting judgment. Each company is unique, and it is up to the underwriter to assess the specific risks of each submission.
