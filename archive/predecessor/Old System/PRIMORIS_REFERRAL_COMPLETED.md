## 4. COMPANY & BUSINESS

### Overview: What They Do, Key Operations, How They Make Money

Primoris Services Corporation is a **specialty contractor** (NOT a large EPC contractor) providing infrastructure and energy services across two primary segments: **Utilities (36% of revenue, $2.7B)** and **Energy (64% of revenue, $4.8B)**.

**Business Model Distinction (Critical for D&O Risk Assessment)**:
- **Average contract size**: <$3 million (vs. $50M+ for large EPC contractors)
- **Contract duration**: 6-12 months (vs. multi-year for EPC)
- **Risk profile**: Lower single-project exposure due to small contract size
- **Execution model**: High-volume, short-duration projects vs. few large, long-duration projects

**Utilities Segment (36% of revenue, $2.7B)** - Three sub-segments:

1. **Power Delivery** (Largest component): Transmission line construction, distribution line construction, substation construction, grid modernization. End markets are electric utilities (investor-owned, municipal, co-ops). Growth drivers include grid modernization, electrification, renewable energy integration, data center power delivery. Margins: 11-12% range.

2. **Communications**: Fiber optic installation, wireless infrastructure (5G towers), broadband deployment. End markets are telecom companies, cable companies, wireless carriers. Growth drivers include 5G rollout, rural broadband expansion, data center connectivity. **Data center exposure is ~2% of total company revenue** (per management). Margins: 11-12% range.

3. **Gas Operations**: Natural gas distribution, pipeline maintenance, gas system upgrades. End markets are gas utilities, industrial customers. Growth drivers include natural gas for data centers, gas system modernization, safety upgrades. Margins: 11-12% range.

**Energy Segment (64% of revenue, $4.8B)** - Five sub-segments:

1. **Power Generation**: Natural gas power plant construction, combined-cycle gas turbine (CCGT) plants, simple-cycle peaker plants. End markets are independent power producers (IPPs), utilities, industrial customers. Growth drivers include natural gas replacing coal, data center power generation, grid reliability. Margins: 10-11% range.

2. **Renewables** (Largest Energy component): Solar PV construction, battery energy storage systems (BESS), wind farm construction, O&M services, solar manufacturing. End markets are developers, utilities, corporate off-takers. Growth drivers include IRA tax credits (safe harbor protections), corporate renewable energy goals, grid-scale storage. Margins: 10-11% range. **Backlog declined 30% YoY** (EXPLAINED as timing - revenue pull-forward + project delays, NOT cancellations).

3. **Pipeline**: Midstream pipeline construction, pipeline maintenance, integrity services. End markets are midstream companies, oil & gas producers. Growth drivers include natural gas infrastructure for LNG exports, pipeline replacements. Outlook: "Opportunities returning in 2026-2028" per management. Margins: 10-11% range.

4. **Heavy Highway** (**CONSIDERING DIVESTITURE**): Highway construction, bridge construction, roadway maintenance. End markets are state DOTs, local governments. **Margins: 3-5% (VERY LOW)** - dragging down Energy segment margins. Status: "We've been looking at maybe divesting from that over the last few years... that might be something that we actually get out of here in the near future" (per management meeting).

5. **Refining/Petrochemical**: Turnaround services, maintenance, small capital projects. End markets are refineries, petrochemical plants. Margins: 10-11% range.

**Revenue Model - Contract Types (Mix Reduces Risk)**:

1. **Master Service Agreements (MSAs)** - **49% of backlog**: Multi-year agreements for recurring services. Pricing is typically unit price or time-and-materials. Revenue predictability is HIGH (recurring, predictable). Execution risk is LOW (small, repetitive tasks). Examples: Utility line maintenance, gas system maintenance, refinery turnarounds. **D&O Implication**: 49% of backlog is low-risk, predictable revenue.

2. **Unit Price Contracts** - ~30% of backlog (estimated): Fixed price per unit (e.g., $X per linear foot of pipeline). Risk is MODERATE (quantity risk, not price risk). Execution risk is MODERATE (productivity drives profitability). Examples: Transmission line construction, solar PV installation.

3. **Reimbursable (Cost-Plus) Contracts** - ~10% of backlog (estimated): Cost reimbursement plus fixed fee or percentage. Risk is LOW (customer bears cost risk). Execution risk is LOW (profitability protected). Examples: Emergency repairs, complex engineering projects.

4. **Fixed-Price (Lump-Sum) Contracts** - ~11% of backlog (estimated): Fixed total price for defined scope. Risk is HIGH (contractor bears all cost risk). Execution risk is HIGH (cost overruns = losses). Examples: Some solar PV EPC projects, some highway projects. **Mitigation**: Primoris uses comprehensive risk review process (CFO involved) before bidding.

**Key Insight for D&O**: Primoris' contract mix is FAVORABLE - 49% MSAs (low risk) + 10% reimbursable (low risk) = **59% of backlog is low-risk**. Only ~11% is high-risk fixed-price. This limits exposure to material project losses that could trigger securities litigation.
