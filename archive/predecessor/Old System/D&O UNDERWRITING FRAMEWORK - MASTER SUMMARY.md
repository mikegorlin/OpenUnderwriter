- **~507 Total Checks**: Comprehensive enumerated checklist

- **Standard Analysis** (0 Quick Screen red flags): 2-4 hours
- **High Risk Analysis** (1-2 Quick Screen red flags): 3-5 hours

## COLOR CODING SYSTEM

### 🟢 **GREEN** = Pass / Low Risk / Compliant
- Indicator of low D&O litigation risk
- Company meets or exceeds best practices
- No action required

### 🟡 **YELLOW** = Caution / Moderate Risk / Needs Monitoring
- Indicator of moderate D&O litigation risk
- Company has some concerns but not critical
- Monitor for deterioration

### 🔴 **RED** = Fail / High Risk / Critical Issue
- Indicator of high D&O litigation risk
- Company has serious problems
- May trigger auto-decline if multiple red flags

### 🟣 **PURPLE** = Unknown / Insufficient Data / Requires Manual Research
- Unable to verify with available data
- Requires additional research or manual investigation
- Track as research gap

## VERIFICATION STANDARDS

### Primary Sources (Required for Hard Data)
- **SEC Filings**: 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, DEF 14A, Form 4, Schedule 13D/13G
- **Court Records**: PACER, Stanford Securities Clearinghouse
- **Government Databases**: FDA, EPA, OSHA, CFPB, NHTSA
- **Stock Data**: Yahoo Finance, Bloomberg (authoritative)
- **Credit Ratings**: S&P, Moody's, Fitch

### Secondary Sources (Require 2+ for Verification)
- **News**: WSJ, Bloomberg, Reuters, NYT (tier 1 outlets)
- **Social Media**: Twitter/X, Reddit, LinkedIn
- **Employee Reviews**: Glassdoor, Blind, Indeed
- **Customer Reviews**: Amazon, App Store, BBB, Yelp
- **Short Seller Reports**: Muddy Waters, Citron, Hindenburg
- **Industry Publications**: Trade journals, industry reports

### Verification Requirements by Data Type
| Data Type | Minimum Sources | Preferred Sources |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| Financial Data | 1 (SEC filing) | 1 (authoritative) |
| Litigation | 1 (court record or 8-K) | 1 (authoritative) |
| Stock Data | 1 (Yahoo/Bloomberg) | 2 (cross-check) |
| Insider Trading | 1 (Form 4) | 1 (authoritative) |
| Social Media Allegations | 2 independent | 3+ (social + news + employee) |
| Employee Reviews | 3+ similar reviews | 5+ reviews + corroboration |
| Customer Complaints | 10+ similar complaints | Complaints + regulatory action |
| News Reports | 1 (tier 1 outlet) | 2+ outlets |
| Short Seller Reports | 1 (report) + company response + stock reaction | All three |

### Module-Specific Auto-Decline Triggers

**Module 1**:
- Active securities litigation + settlement >$50M + weak defense
- SEC formal investigation + accounting fraud allegations
- Financial restatement + auditor resignation + going concern

**Module 2**:
- Negative operating cash flow + <12 months runway + no revenue + failed financing
- Debt covenant breach + going concern warning
- Stock down >70% + multiple event drops >20% + no recovery

**Module 3**:
- Core product failure (FDA rejection) + no alternatives + revenue down >50%
- Lost customer >50% of revenue + no replacement
- Failed M&A (>50% market cap) + impairment >30% + customer losses >25%

**Module 4**:
- CEO + CFO departures + accounting issues + insider selling
- Board independence <50% + related party transactions + failed say-on-pay
- Insider trading violations + criminal charges

**Module 5**:
- Credible short seller fraud report + stock down >50% + no recovery + SEC investigation
- All analysts dropped coverage OR all Sell ratings + targets cut >60%
- Institutional ownership down >25% in 2 quarters + top holders exiting

**Module 6**:
- Viral fraud allegations (>1M views) + employee whistleblower + investigative journalism + no rebuttal
- Glassdoor <2.5 + >30% turnover + fraud reviews + C-suite departures
- WSJ/Bloomberg exposé + fraud allegations + company silence + stock down >40%

## UNDERWRITING DECISION MATRIX

### Risk Scoring
Calculate across all modules analyzed:
- **RED %** = (Red flags / Total checks) × 100
- **YELLOW %** = (Yellow flags / Total checks) × 100
- **GREEN %** = (Green flags / Total checks) × 100
- **PURPLE %** = (Purple flags / Total checks) × 100

### Decision Thresholds

| RED % | YELLOW % | Recommendation | Premium | Retention |
|-------|----------|----------------|---------|-----------|
| >30% | Any | **DECLINE** | N/A | N/A |
| 20-30% | >15% | **DECLINE** | N/A | N/A |
| 20-30% | <15% | **DECLINE or EXTREME** | 5-10x | $10M+ |
| 10-20% | <10% | **CAUTION - High Premium** | 2-3x | $5M |
| <10% | <10% | **STANDARD** | 1x | $2-3M |
| <5% | <5% | **FAVORABLE** | <1x | $1-2M |

## WORKFLOW SUMMARY

### Step 1: Quick Screen (5-10 min)
1. Pull basic data (10-K, 10-Q, 8-Ks, stock data)
2. Run 40 Quick Screen checks
3. Count red flags
4. **Decision**:
   - 3+ red flags → DECLINE (stop)
   - 1-2 red flags → Continue with HIGH RISK flag
   - 0 red flags → Continue standard

### Step 2: Module 1 - Core Litigation Risk (30-45 min)
- Run all 112 checks
- **Stop if**: >30 red flags (>25% fail rate)

### Step 3: Module 2 - Financial Health (45-60 min)
- Run all 95 checks
- **Critical**: Stock attribution analysis for all declines >20%
- **Stop if**: Cash crisis, debt distress, or stock catastrophe

### Step 4: Module 3 - Business Model (30-45 min)
- Run all 70 checks
- **Stop if**: Business model collapse, major customer loss, failed M&A

### Step 5: Module 4 - Leadership & Governance (30-45 min)
- Run all 75 checks
- **Critical**: Insider trading analysis 30-90 days before events
- **Stop if**: Executive crisis, governance failure, insider trading violations

### Step 6: Module 5 - Market Dynamics (30-45 min)
- Run all 65 checks
- **Stop if**: Short seller fraud report, analyst abandonment, institutional exodus

### Step 7: Module 6 - Alternative Data (2-3 hours OR 15-20 min quick screen)
- **Option A**: Quick screen top 20 checks (15-20 min)
- **Option B**: Full 90 checks (2-3 hours)
- **Verification**: Require 2+ sources for all soft signals
- **Stop if**: Viral fraud allegations, employee exodus + fraud claims, investigative exposé

### Step 8: Aggregate & Recommend (15-30 min)
1. Calculate risk scores (RED %, YELLOW %, GREEN %, PURPLE %)
2. Apply decision matrix
3. Document recommendation with specific terms
4. List top 10 critical red flags
5. Identify research gaps (purple items)
6. Complete sources & methodology section

## KEY DIFFERENTIATORS OF THIS FRAMEWORK

### 1. **Enumerated Checklist Approach**
- Every check is numbered and documented
- Systematic coverage prevents blind spots
- Reproducible and auditable

### 2. **Primary Source Verification**
- All claims verified with SEC filings, court records, or government databases
- 2+ source requirement for soft signals
- Specific citations with dates and document names

### 3. **Adaptive Depth**
- Stop early if auto-decline triggered (saves time)
- Don't waste hours analyzing obviously uninsurable companies
- Focus deep dive on borderline cases

### 4. **Stock Attribution Analysis**
- Don't just note stock declined - explain WHY
- Identify specific trigger events with dates
- Compare to sector and peers to determine if company-specific or macro

### 5. **Alternative Data / Blind Spot Analysis**
- Captures "soft signals" before they appear in SEC filings
- Social media, employee reviews, news patterns, regulatory databases
- Often provides 3-12 month early warning

### 6. **Color Coding Discipline**
- Consistent use of Green/Yellow/Red/Purple
- Visual risk assessment at a glance
- Quantifiable risk scoring

### 7. **Industry-Specific Modules**
- Tailored checks for life sciences, tech, financial services, etc.
- Industry-specific databases and metrics
- Relevant benchmarks by sector

## BEST PRACTICES

### 1. Always Run Quick Screen First
- Never skip Quick Screen - it only takes 10 minutes
- Can save hours by identifying auto-decline cases early

### 2. Verify All Red Flags with Primary Sources
- Don't rely on secondary sources for critical findings
- Always cite specific SEC filings, court documents, or government databases

### 3. Require 2+ Sources for Soft Signals
- Social media, employee reviews, news can be unreliable
- Corroborate before flagging as red

### 4. Explain Stock Movements
- Don't just note "stock down 40%" - explain WHY
- Identify specific trigger events with dates
- Compare to sector and peers

### 5. Focus on Module 6 for Early Warnings
- Alternative data often predicts problems 3-12 months early
- Glassdoor, social media, news patterns are leading indicators

### 6. Document Research Gaps
- Track purple items (unknowns)
- Estimate impact on recommendation if gaps were filled
- Prioritize high-impact gaps for additional research

### 7. Be Decisive
- If it's a DECLINE, say so clearly
- Don't hedge on obvious red flags
- Provide specific underwriting terms for insurable risks

## LIMITATIONS & DISCLAIMERS

### Data Availability
- Framework assumes access to SEC filings, court records, stock data, and alternative data sources
- Some checks may be impossible to complete without paid data subscriptions (Bloomberg, Audit Integrity, etc.)
- Purple (unknown) items should be tracked as research gaps

### Time Constraints
- Full framework (all 507 checks) can take 6-8 hours
- Quick Screen + selective module analysis is acceptable for time-constrained situations
- Module 6 (Alternative Data) is most time-intensive but highest value

### Industry Expertise
- Some industry-specific checks require domain expertise (e.g., clinical trial analysis for biotech)
- Consider consulting industry experts for complex cases

### Judgment Required
- Framework provides systematic checklist but requires professional judgment
- Borderline cases (15-20% red flags) require nuanced decision-making
- Context matters - not all red flags are equal

### Not Legal Advice
- This framework is for underwriting risk assessment only
- Does not constitute legal advice or prediction of litigation outcomes
- Consult legal counsel for legal questions
