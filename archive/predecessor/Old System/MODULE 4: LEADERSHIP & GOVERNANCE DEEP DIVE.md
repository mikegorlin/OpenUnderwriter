# MODULE 4: LEADERSHIP & GOVERNANCE DEEP DIVE

**Purpose**: Assess quality of executive leadership, board effectiveness, governance practices, compensation alignment, insider behavior, and activist investor exposure.

**Total Checks**: ~75 enumerated checks across 6 categories

**When to Use**:
- Run after Module 3 passes (no business model collapse)
- Critical for assessing management credibility and governance quality
- Often reveals "soft" red flags that predict future problems

**Auto-Decline Triggers in This Module**:
- CEO/CFO simultaneous departures + accounting issues + insider selling
- Board independence <50% + related party transactions + weak audit committee
- Insider trading violations + SEC enforcement + criminal charges
- Activist campaign + proxy contest + management entrenchment + poor performance

---

## CATEGORY 1: EXECUTIVE QUALITY & TRACK RECORD (15 Checks)

### 1.1 CEO Assessment

**1.1.1 CEO Tenure**
- **Check**: Length of time as CEO
- **Pass** 🟢: 3-10 years (experienced but not entrenched)
- **Caution** 🟡: <3 years (new) or >10 years (entrenched)
- **Fail** 🔴: <1 year + company in crisis OR >15 years + poor performance
- **Sources**: Proxy statement (DEF 14A), company website
- **Context**: New CEOs have higher litigation risk during transition

**1.1.2 CEO Prior Experience**
- **Check**: Relevant industry and leadership experience
- **Pass** 🟢: Extensive industry experience + prior CEO/COO role
- **Caution** 🟡: Industry experience but first-time CEO
- **Fail** 🔴: No industry experience + first-time CEO + complex company
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, LinkedIn, executive bio
- **Red Flag**: Inexperienced CEO in crisis = high risk

**1.1.3 CEO Track Record**
- **Check**: Performance at prior companies
- **Pass** 🟢: Strong track record of value creation
- **Caution** 🟡: Mixed track record
- **Fail** 🔴: History of failures, bankruptcies, or litigation at prior companies
- **Sources**: Public company records, news, litigation databases
- **Red Flag**: Serial failure pattern = repeat risk

**1.1.4 CEO Credibility**
- **Check**: History of meeting commitments and guidance
- **Pass** 🟢: Consistent track record of meeting/beating guidance
- **Caution** 🟡: Occasional misses with explanations
- **Fail** 🔴: Pattern of overpromising and underdelivering
- **Sources**: Historical guidance vs. results, earnings calls
- **Red Flag**: Lost credibility = market doesn't trust CEO

**1.1.5 CEO Communication Style**
- **Check**: Transparency and clarity in communications
- **Pass** 🟢: Clear, transparent, addresses tough questions
- **Caution** 🟡: Standard corporate communications
- **Fail** 🔴: Evasive, promotional, attacks critics, blocks questions
- **Sources**: Earnings calls, interviews, shareholder letters
- **Red Flag**: Defensive/evasive CEO = hiding problems

**1.1.6 CEO Litigation History**
- **Check**: Personal involvement in prior litigation
- **Pass** 🟢: No material litigation history
- **Caution** 🟡: Defendant in routine business litigation
- **Fail** 🔴: History of fraud allegations, SEC enforcement, criminal charges
- **Sources**: Litigation databases (PACER), SEC enforcement, news
- **Red Flag**: Prior fraud = repeat risk

---

### 1.2 CFO Assessment

**1.2.1 CFO Tenure**
- **Check**: Length of time as CFO
- **Pass** 🟢: 2-8 years
- **Caution** 🟡: <2 years (new) or >8 years
- **Fail** 🔴: <6 months + accounting issues OR frequent CFO turnover
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, Form 8-K
- **Red Flag**: CFO turnover = accounting problems

**1.2.2 CFO Credentials**
- **Check**: Accounting credentials and experience
- **Pass** 🟢: CPA + Big 4 experience + prior CFO role
- **Caution** 🟡: Accounting degree + relevant experience
- **Fail** 🔴: No accounting background or weak credentials
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, LinkedIn, executive bio
- **Red Flag**: Unqualified CFO = accounting risk

**1.2.3 CFO Track Record**
- **Check**: History at prior companies
- **Pass** 🟢: Clean track record, no restatements or issues
- **Caution** 🟡: No prior CFO experience
- **Fail** 🔴: History of restatements, SEC issues, or departures under pressure
- **Sources**: Public company records, SEC filings
- **Red Flag**: Prior accounting problems = repeat risk

**1.2.4 CFO Departure Circumstances**
- **Check**: If CFO departed, circumstances of departure
- **Pass** 🟢: No recent CFO departure OR retirement after long tenure
- **Caution** 🟡: CFO departed for "personal reasons" or "pursue other opportunities"
- **Fail** 🔴: CFO departed abruptly + accounting issues + no explanation
- **N/A**: No recent CFO departure
- **Sources**: Form 8-K, press releases
- **Red Flag**: Sudden CFO departure = accounting problems

---

### 1.3 Executive Team Assessment

**1.3.1 Executive Team Stability**
- **Check**: Turnover in C-suite (CEO, CFO, COO, CTO, etc.)
- **Pass** 🟢: <10% annual executive turnover
- **Caution** 🟡: 10-25% turnover
- **Fail** 🔴: >25% turnover or multiple simultaneous departures
- **Sources**: Form 8-K Item 5.02, press releases
- **Red Flag**: Executive exodus = crisis or toxic culture

**1.3.2 Executive Team Experience**
- **Check**: Depth of management bench
- **Pass** 🟢: Experienced team with industry expertise
- **Caution** 🟡: Mix of experienced and new executives
- **Fail** 🔴: Inexperienced team or multiple first-time roles
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, executive bios
- **Red Flag**: Weak bench = execution risk

**1.3.3 Succession Planning**
- **Check**: Disclosed succession plans for key roles
- **Pass** 🟢: Clear succession plans disclosed
- **Caution** 🟡: No formal disclosure but internal candidates exist
- **Fail** 🔴: No succession plan + aging CEO + no internal candidates
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, governance disclosures
- **Red Flag**: No succession plan = key person risk

**1.3.4 Executive Litigation History**
- **Check**: Executives involved in prior litigation
- **Pass** 🟢: No material litigation involving executives
- **Caution** 🟡: Routine business litigation
- **Fail** 🔴: Multiple executives with fraud/misconduct history
- **Sources**: Litigation databases, SEC enforcement
- **Red Flag**: Pattern of problematic executives = governance failure

**1.3.5 Executive Reputation**
- **Check**: Industry reputation and references
- **Pass** 🟢: Well-regarded in industry
- **Caution** 🟡: Unknown or mixed reputation
- **Fail** 🔴: Negative reputation, industry concerns
- **Sources**: Industry publications, news, references
- **Red Flag**: Poor reputation = credibility issues

## CATEGORY 2: BOARD OF DIRECTORS QUALITY (15 Checks)

### 2.1 Board Independence

**2.1.1 Board Independence Percentage**
- **Check**: Percentage of independent directors
- **Pass** 🟢: >75% independent directors
- **Caution** 🟡: 50-75% independent
- **Fail** 🔴: <50% independent (majority not independent)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement (DEF 14A)
- **Benchmark**: NYSE/NASDAQ require majority independent

**2.1.2 Audit Committee Independence**
- **Check**: Audit committee 100% independent
- **Pass** 🟢: All audit committee members independent
- **Caution** 🟡: N/A (should be 100%)
- **Fail** 🔴: Any non-independent audit committee members
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Non-independent audit committee = governance failure

**2.1.3 Compensation Committee Independence**
- **Check**: Compensation committee 100% independent
- **Pass** 🟢: All comp committee members independent
- **Caution** 🟡: N/A
- **Fail** 🔴: Any non-independent comp committee members
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Non-independent comp committee = pay abuse risk

**2.1.4 Related Party Transactions**
- **Check**: Transactions between company and directors/executives
- **Pass** 🟢: No related party transactions
- **Caution** 🟡: Immaterial related party transactions with disclosure
- **Fail** 🔴: Material related party transactions or pattern of self-dealing
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, 10-K related party note
- **Red Flag**: Self-dealing = governance failure

---

### 2.2 Board Composition & Expertise

**2.2.1 Board Size**
- **Check**: Number of directors
- **Pass** 🟢: 7-11 directors (optimal size)
- **Caution** 🟡: 5-6 or 12-13 directors
- **Fail** 🔴: <5 directors (too small) or >13 (too large)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Context**: Too small = lack of oversight, too large = ineffective

**2.2.2 Industry Expertise**
- **Check**: Directors with relevant industry experience
- **Pass** 🟢: >50% of directors have industry expertise
- **Caution** 🟡: 25-50% with industry expertise
- **Fail** 🔴: <25% with industry expertise
- **Sources**: Proxy statement director bios
- **Red Flag**: No industry expertise = ineffective oversight

**2.2.3 Financial Expertise**
- **Check**: Directors with financial/accounting expertise
- **Pass** 🟢: Multiple directors with financial expertise
- **Caution** 🟡: One financial expert (minimum requirement)
- **Fail** 🔴: No designated financial expert
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: No financial expertise = weak audit oversight

**2.2.4 Board Diversity**
- **Check**: Gender, ethnic, and background diversity
- **Pass** 🟢: >30% diverse directors (gender, ethnicity)
- **Caution** 🟡: 10-30% diverse
- **Fail** 🔴: <10% diverse or all-male board
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Context**: Diversity correlates with better governance

**2.2.5 Director Age Profile**
- **Check**: Average age and age range of directors
- **Pass** 🟢: Average age 55-65 with mix of ages
- **Caution** 🟡: Average age 65-70
- **Fail** 🔴: Average age >70 or all directors >75
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Aging board = lack of refreshment

---

### 2.3 Board Effectiveness

**2.3.1 Director Attendance**
- **Check**: Board and committee meeting attendance
- **Pass** 🟢: All directors >90% attendance
- **Caution** 🟡: One director with <90% attendance
- **Fail** 🔴: Multiple directors with <75% attendance
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Poor attendance = lack of engagement

**2.3.2 Overboarding**
- **Check**: Directors serving on >4 public boards
- **Pass** 🟢: No directors on >4 boards
- **Caution** 🟡: One director on 5 boards
- **Fail** 🔴: Multiple directors on >4 boards or CEO on >2 outside boards
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, public board records
- **Red Flag**: Overboarding = lack of time/attention

**2.3.3 Director Tenure**
- **Check**: Average tenure and tenure distribution
- **Pass** 🟢: Average tenure 5-10 years with mix of tenures
- **Caution** 🟡: Average tenure >10 years
- **Fail** 🔴: All directors >15 years tenure (stale board)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Entrenched board = lack of fresh perspectives

**2.3.4 Board Refreshment**
- **Check**: New directors added in past 3 years
- **Pass** 🟢: 2+ new directors in past 3 years
- **Caution** 🟡: 1 new director in past 3 years
- **Fail** 🔴: No new directors in 3+ years
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: No refreshment = stale board

**2.3.5 Director Litigation History**
- **Check**: Directors involved in prior litigation/governance failures
- **Pass** 🟢: No directors with litigation history
- **Caution** 🟡: Directors involved in routine business litigation
- **Fail** 🔴: Directors with history of fraud, bankruptcy, or governance failures
- **Sources**: Litigation databases, public records
- **Red Flag**: Problem directors = governance risk

**2.3.6 Lead Independent Director**
- **Check**: Designated lead independent director (if CEO is chairman)
- **Pass** 🟢: Strong lead independent director OR independent chairman
- **Caution** 🟡: Lead independent director with limited powers
- **Fail** 🔴: No lead independent director + CEO is chairman
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: CEO-chairman with no counterbalance = weak governance

## CATEGORY 3: COMPENSATION ALIGNMENT (12 Checks)

### 3.1 Executive Compensation Structure

**3.1.1 CEO Total Compensation Level**
- **Check**: CEO pay vs. peers
- **Pass** 🟢: CEO pay at or below peer median
- **Caution** 🟡: CEO pay 10-25% above peer median
- **Fail** 🔴: CEO pay >25% above peers OR >100x median employee
- **Sources**: Proxy statement Summary Compensation Table
- **Benchmark**: Excessive pay = governance concern

**3.1.2 Pay Mix (Fixed vs. Variable)**
- **Check**: Percentage of pay at risk (performance-based)
- **Pass** 🟢: >60% of pay at risk (equity, bonuses)
- **Caution** 🟡: 40-60% at risk
- **Fail** 🔴: <40% at risk (mostly salary)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement compensation discussion
- **Benchmark**: High fixed pay = poor alignment

**3.1.3 Equity Vesting Terms**
- **Check**: Vesting schedule for equity awards
- **Pass** 🟢: 3-4 year vesting with performance conditions
- **Caution** 🟡: 3-4 year time-based vesting
- **Fail** 🔴: <2 year vesting or immediate vesting
- **Sources**: Proxy statement equity award disclosures
- **Red Flag**: Short vesting = short-term focus

**3.1.4 Performance Metrics**
- **Check**: Metrics used for bonus/equity vesting
- **Pass** 🟢: Multiple metrics including TSR, ROIC, strategic goals
- **Caution** 🟡: Single metric (e.g., EPS only)
- **Fail** 🔴: Easily manipulated metrics or no performance conditions
- **Sources**: Proxy statement CD&A
- **Red Flag**: Weak metrics = gaming risk

**3.1.5 Clawback Policy**
- **Check**: Ability to recoup compensation for misconduct/restatement
- **Pass** 🟢: Robust clawback policy covering all executives
- **Caution** 🟡: Basic clawback policy (minimum required)
- **Fail** 🔴: No clawback policy or weak policy
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Benchmark**: NYSE/NASDAQ now require clawback policies

---

### 3.2 Pay-for-Performance Alignment

**3.2.1 Pay vs. TSR Alignment**
- **Check**: CEO pay changes correlated with stock performance
- **Pass** 🟢: Pay moves with TSR (positive correlation)
- **Caution** 🟡: Weak correlation
- **Fail** 🔴: Pay increases while TSR negative (inverse correlation)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement pay vs. performance disclosure
- **Red Flag**: Pay up, stock down = misalignment

**3.2.2 Say-on-Pay Vote Results**
- **Check**: Shareholder approval of executive compensation
- **Pass** 🟢: >90% approval on say-on-pay
- **Caution** 🟡: 70-90% approval
- **Fail** 🔴: <70% approval or failed say-on-pay vote
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, Form 8-K
- **Red Flag**: Failed vote = shareholder revolt

**3.2.3 Bonus Payout History**
- **Check**: Actual bonus payouts vs. target
- **Pass** 🟢: Bonuses vary with performance (some below, some above target)
- **Caution** 🟡: Bonuses consistently at target
- **Fail** 🔴: Bonuses always at maximum regardless of performance
- **Sources**: Proxy statement compensation tables
- **Red Flag**: Always max payout = rigged metrics

**3.2.4 Equity Grant Timing**
- **Check**: Timing of equity grants relative to news/earnings
- **Pass** 🟢: Grants on regular schedule (e.g., annual grant date)
- **Caution** 🟡: Grants clustered around earnings
- **Fail** 🔴: Grants before positive news or after negative news (spring-loading/backdating)
- **Sources**: Form 4 filings, grant date analysis
- **Red Flag**: Suspicious timing = manipulation

---

### 3.3 Severance & Change-in-Control

**3.3.1 Severance Multiples**
- **Check**: Severance payments upon termination
- **Pass** 🟢: ≤2x salary + bonus
- **Caution** 🟡: 2-3x salary + bonus
- **Fail** 🔴: >3x salary + bonus (golden parachute)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, employment agreements
- **Red Flag**: Excessive severance = pay for failure

**3.3.2 Change-in-Control Provisions**
- **Check**: Payments triggered by acquisition
- **Pass** 🟢: Double-trigger (termination + change-in-control)
- **Caution** 🟡: Single-trigger for some benefits
- **Fail** 🔴: Single-trigger for all benefits (paid just for acquisition)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement CIC disclosures
- **Red Flag**: Single-trigger = windfall without job loss

**3.3.3 Excise Tax Gross-Ups**
- **Check**: Company pays executives' excise taxes
- **Pass** 🟢: No tax gross-ups
- **Caution** 🟡: Limited gross-ups for legacy agreements
- **Fail** 🔴: Broad tax gross-ups (company pays executives' taxes)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Gross-ups = excessive executive protection

## CATEGORY 4: INSIDER OWNERSHIP & TRADING (15 Checks)

### 4.1 Insider Ownership

**4.1.1 CEO Ownership Level**
- **Check**: CEO ownership as % of shares outstanding
- **Pass** 🟢: CEO owns >1% of shares
- **Caution** 🟡: CEO owns 0.25-1%
- **Fail** 🔴: CEO owns <0.25% (minimal skin in game)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement beneficial ownership table
- **Benchmark**: Higher ownership = better alignment

**4.1.2 Executive Team Ownership**
- **Check**: Total executive ownership
- **Pass** 🟢: Executives collectively own >5%
- **Caution** 🟡: Executives own 2-5%
- **Fail** 🔴: Executives own <2%
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Low ownership = weak alignment

**4.1.3 Director Ownership**
- **Check**: Non-executive director ownership
- **Pass** 🟢: Directors own meaningful stakes (>$500K each)
- **Caution** 🟡: Directors own modest stakes
- **Fail** 🔴: Directors own minimal/no stock
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Context**: Director ownership = alignment with shareholders

**4.1.4 Insider Ownership Trend**
- **Check**: Insider ownership increasing or decreasing
- **Pass** 🟢: Insider ownership increasing (buying)
- **Caution** 🟡: Insider ownership stable
- **Fail** 🔴: Insider ownership declining (selling)
- **Sources**: Multi-year proxy statements
- **Red Flag**: Insiders selling = lack of confidence

---

### 4.2 Insider Trading Patterns

**4.2.1 Insider Buying Activity**
- **Check**: Open market purchases by insiders in past 12 months
- **Pass** 🟢: Multiple insiders buying in open market
- **Caution** 🟡: Some insider buying
- **Fail** 🔴: No insider buying despite low stock price
- **Sources**: Form 4 filings
- **Context**: Insider buying = confidence signal

**4.2.2 Insider Selling Volume**
- **Check**: Shares sold by insiders in past 12 months
- **Pass** 🟢: Minimal selling (<10% of holdings)
- **Caution** 🟡: Moderate selling (10-30% of holdings)
- **Fail** 🔴: Heavy selling (>30% of holdings) or selling >50% of position
- **Sources**: Form 4 filings
- **Red Flag**: Heavy selling = lack of confidence

**4.2.3 Insider Selling Pattern**
- **Check**: Pattern and timing of insider sales
- **Pass** 🟢: Regular 10b5-1 plan sales on schedule
- **Caution** 🟡: Mix of planned and discretionary sales
- **Fail** 🔴: Clustered discretionary sales before bad news
- **Sources**: Form 4 filings, 10b5-1 plan disclosures
- **Red Flag**: Selling before bad news = insider trading risk

**4.2.4 10b5-1 Plan Adoption Timing**
- **Check**: When 10b5-1 trading plans adopted
- **Pass** 🟢: Plans adopted >90 days before sales
- **Caution** 🟡: Plans adopted 30-90 days before sales
- **Fail** 🔴: Plans adopted <30 days before sales (especially before bad news)
- **Sources**: Form 4 footnotes, company disclosures
- **Red Flag**: Quick adoption before bad news = manipulation

**4.2.5 Clustered Insider Selling**
- **Check**: Multiple insiders selling in same time period
- **Pass** 🟢: No clustered selling
- **Caution** 🟡: 2 insiders selling in same month
- **Fail** 🔴: 3+ insiders selling in same week/month
- **Sources**: Form 4 filings
- **Red Flag**: Clustered selling = coordinated exit

**4.2.6 Insider Selling Before Events**
- **Check**: Selling 30-90 days before material negative events
- **Pass** 🟢: No suspicious pre-event selling
- **Caution** 🟡: Some selling before events but explainable
- **Fail** 🔴: Pattern of selling before bad news (FDA rejection, earnings miss, etc.)
- **Sources**: Form 4 filings, event dates
- **Red Flag**: Pre-event selling = smoking gun for litigation

**4.2.7 Form 4 Filing Compliance**
- **Check**: Timely filing of insider trading reports
- **Pass** 🟢: All Form 4s filed within 2 business days
- **Caution** 🟡: Occasional late filings with explanations
- **Fail** 🔴: Pattern of late filings or missing Form 4s
- **Sources**: Form 4 filing dates, late filing notices
- **Red Flag**: Late filings = compliance problems

---

### 4.3 Stock Pledging & Hedging

**4.3.1 Share Pledging**
- **Check**: Insiders pledging shares as collateral for loans
- **Pass** 🟢: No share pledging
- **Caution** 🟡: <25% of holdings pledged
- **Fail** 🔴: >25% of holdings pledged or CEO pledging shares
- **Sources**: Proxy statement beneficial ownership footnotes
- **Red Flag**: Pledging = forced sale risk if stock drops

**4.3.2 Hedging Transactions**
- **Check**: Insiders hedging stock exposure (collars, puts, etc.)
- **Pass** 🟢: No hedging OR hedging prohibited by policy
- **Caution** 🟡: Limited hedging allowed
- **Fail** 🔴: Significant hedging activity by executives
- **Sources**: Proxy statement, Form 4 derivative disclosures
- **Red Flag**: Hedging = reducing alignment with shareholders

**4.3.3 Anti-Hedging/Pledging Policy**
- **Check**: Company policy prohibiting hedging and pledging
- **Pass** 🟢: Strong policy prohibiting hedging and pledging
- **Caution** 🟡: Policy allows limited hedging/pledging
- **Fail** 🔴: No policy or weak policy
- **Sources**: Proxy statement governance disclosures
- **Benchmark**: Best practice is prohibition

## CATEGORY 5: SHAREHOLDER RIGHTS & ACTIVISM (10 Checks)

### 5.1 Shareholder Rights

**5.1.1 Dual-Class Share Structure**
- **Check**: Multiple classes of stock with different voting rights
- **Pass** 🟢: One class, one vote (no dual-class)
- **Caution** 🟡: Dual-class with sunset provision
- **Fail** 🔴: Perpetual dual-class structure with founder control
- **Sources**: 10-K, proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Dual-class = entrenched management

**5.1.2 Supermajority Voting Requirements**
- **Check**: Charter/bylaws requiring >50% vote for actions
- **Pass** 🟢: Majority voting for all actions
- **Caution** 🟡: Supermajority for limited actions (mergers)
- **Fail** 🔴: Supermajority for routine actions (director election, bylaw amendments)
- **Sources**: Charter, bylaws, proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Supermajority = anti-takeover defense

**5.1.3 Poison Pill (Shareholder Rights Plan)**
- **Check**: Existence of poison pill
- **Pass** 🟢: No poison pill
- **Caution** 🟡: Poison pill adopted in response to specific threat with expiration
- **Fail** 🔴: Perpetual poison pill without shareholder approval
- **Sources**: Form 8-K, proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Poison pill = anti-takeover defense

**5.1.4 Classified Board**
- **Check**: Staggered board elections (directors elected for multi-year terms)
- **Pass** 🟢: Annual election of all directors
- **Caution** 🟡: Moving to annual elections
- **Fail** 🔴: Classified board (only 1/3 elected each year)
- **Sources**: Charter, proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: Classified board = entrenchment

**5.1.5 Shareholder Right to Call Special Meeting**
- **Check**: Shareholders can call special meetings
- **Pass** 🟢: Shareholders with ≥10% can call special meeting
- **Caution** 🟡: Shareholders with 25% can call meeting
- **Fail** 🔴: No shareholder right to call special meeting
- **Sources**: Bylaws, proxy statement
- **Benchmark**: Best practice is ≤15% threshold

**5.1.6 Shareholder Right to Act by Written Consent**
- **Check**: Shareholders can act without meeting
- **Pass** 🟢: Shareholders can act by written consent
- **Caution** 🟡: Limited written consent rights
- **Fail** 🔴: No written consent rights
- **Sources**: Charter, bylaws
- **Context**: Written consent allows faster shareholder action

- **Caution** 🟡: Proxy contest >3 years ago, resolved
- **Fail** 🔴: Active proxy contest or recent contest (<2 years)
- **Sources**: Proxy statements, Form 8-K
- **Red Flag**: Proxy contest = shareholder dissatisfaction
