## PART 1: QUESTIONS ANSWERED BY EARNINGS CALL

### ✅ **ANSWERED #1: Energy Segment Backlog Decline** (CRITICAL - Your Gap #1)

**Your Question**:
> "Your Energy segment backlog declined 30% from $6.3B in 2024 to $4.5B in Q3 2025. Can you explain this decline? Is this timing or market softness?"

**Earnings Call Answer** (David King, CEO):
> "The ramp-up in revenue, combined with the delay of a couple of larger dollar value projects, led to a higher-than-anticipated backlog burn rate in the Energy segment. The timing of when projects are signed and placed into backlog can [be due to] a number of factors, including changes in scope and design and the shifting of supply chain schedules."

> "I want to emphasize that our lower than forecasted bookings in Q3 were **not a result of projects being canceled or awarded to other service providers**, but are instead being impacted by other circumstances that can affect the timing of executed contracts."

> "Because of this, we remain highly confident that we will sign several high-value Energy segment projects in the coming quarters that will set us up for another successful year in 2026."

**Ken Dodgen, CFO** (additional detail):
> "The pull forward on revenue was at least $100 million... Revenue for Q4, I've got a ballpark number of about $1.2 billion for Energy in Q4."

> "For the Energy segment alone, we've already booked over $600 million [in Q4]. We have another $600 million that should book within the next 30 days. And for the Energy segment, we're expecting a book-to-bill well north of 1 for Q4, maybe as high as 1.2 or 1.3."

**Assessment**:
- ✅ **TIMING, NOT MARKET SOFTNESS**: Management explicitly states projects were NOT canceled or lost to competitors
- ✅ **REVENUE PULL-FORWARD**: $100M+ revenue pulled forward from Q4/2026 (strong execution)
- ✅ **Q4 RECOVERY**: $600M already booked, $600M expected within 30 days = $1.2B total
- ✅ **2026 CONFIDENCE**: Management "highly confident" about 2026 setup

**D&O Implication**: **LOW RISK** - Backlog decline is timing-driven, not structural. Q4 book-to-bill 1.2-1.3x indicates recovery.

**Your Action**: ✅ **DO NOT ASK** - Fully answered. Update your analysis to reflect timing explanation.

### ✅ **ANSWERED #5: Fixed-Price Contract Risk** (Your Question #3)

**Your Question**:
> "What percentage of your current backlog is fixed-price vs. cost-plus or T&M? Have you experienced any significant cost overruns or margin compression on recent fixed-price projects?"

**Earnings Call Answer** (Ken Dodgen):
> "Gross margins in the [Energy] segment were 10.1%, down from 11% in the prior year. The decrease in margin was driven by fewer project closeouts in 2025 compared to the prior year. Pipeline margins were also a drag on margins during the quarter due to lower revenue and gross profit compared to Q3 of 2024."

> "As those [pipeline] projects finish in Q4 and burn-off, we may have a little bit of margin drag in Q4, but that should be it."

**David King**:
> "The kind of pipeline projects we're looking at now are really down the fairway for us through the larger diameter pipeline projects. So I feel like we'll perform better on those in the future than what we've been struggling with on some of the work that we've seen over this last year or so."

**Assessment**:
- ✅ **MARGIN COMPRESSION**: Energy margins 10.1% (vs. 11% prior year) due to fewer closeouts + pipeline drag
- ✅ **PIPELINE ISSUES**: Temporary (Q4 burn-off), NOT systemic
- ✅ **FUTURE OUTLOOK**: "Down the fairway" projects (larger diameter) = better margins

**D&O Implication**: **LOW-MODERATE RISK** - Margin compression is temporary (pipeline drag + fewer closeouts). 2026 outlook is positive.

**Your Action**: ⚠️ **PARTIALLY ANSWERED** - Margin trend explained, but **specific fixed-price % NOT disclosed**. Consider asking: "Can you confirm the 49% fixed-price by revenue figure from your presentation? Any changes expected?"
