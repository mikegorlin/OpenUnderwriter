## QUESTION 1: UNDISCLOSED LITIGATION (REQUIRED)

### **The Question**:

> "Are you aware of any securities investigations, subpoenas, government inquiries, shareholder demands, potential derivative claims, or threatened litigation that have not been publicly disclosed? This includes informal inquiries from regulators, demand letters from shareholders or their counsel, or internal investigations that could lead to claims against directors or officers."

### **Why This Matters**:

- **Standard Required Question**: Must be asked for all D&O underwriting
- **Timing**: Often, threatened claims emerge 6-12 months before public disclosure
- **Scope**: Includes SEC informal inquiries, DOJ contacts, state AG investigations, shareholder demands
- **D&O Trigger**: Any "yes" answer requires immediate deep-dive and potential declination

### **What to Listen For**:

**RED FLAGS** (Immediate concern):
- ❌ "We've received an informal SEC inquiry about..."
- ❌ "A shareholder law firm sent a demand letter regarding..."
- ❌ "We're conducting an internal investigation into..."
- ❌ Hesitation, vague answers, or "I'll have to get back to you"

**GREEN FLAGS** (Positive):
- ✅ "No, we are not aware of any such matters"
- ✅ Immediate, confident, unequivocal answer
- ✅ General Counsel present and confirms

### **UPGRADE/DOWNGRADE Criteria**:

**MAINTAIN 3-5/100** (No change):
- ✅ Clear "No" with no hesitation
- ✅ General Counsel confirms

**INCREASE to 15-25/100** (Material concern):
- ❌ SEC informal inquiry (even if "informal")
- ❌ Shareholder demand letter received
- ❌ Internal investigation ongoing
- ❌ Any threatened litigation disclosed

**AUTOMATIC DECLINE**:
- ❌ Formal SEC investigation
- ❌ DOJ criminal inquiry
- ❌ Securities class action filed
- ❌ Derivative lawsuit filed

### **Follow-Up Questions** (if any concerns):

1. "When did you first become aware of this matter?"
2. "Have you notified your current D&O carrier?"
3. "What is the nature of the allegations or inquiry?"
4. "Have you retained outside counsel to investigate?"

## QUESTION 2: BOARD-MANAGEMENT ALIGNMENT & GOVERNANCE

### **The Question**:

> "Can you discuss the Board's strategic alignment with management, particularly around the CEO transition from Tom McCormick to Koti Vadlamudi? How has the Board's focus evolved over the past few years, and are there any areas where the Board and management have had different views on company strategy, capital allocation, or risk management?"

### **Why This Matters**:

- **CEO Transition Risk**: New CEO (Koti Vadlamudi) started recently; Board-CEO alignment is critical
- **Governance Quality**: Board-management tension is a leading indicator of D&O claims
- **Strategic Disagreements**: Divergent views on capital allocation, M&A, or risk appetite create litigation risk
- **Earnings Call Gap**: CEO transition mentioned but Board alignment NOT discussed

### **What to Listen For**:

**RED FLAGS** (Governance concern):
- ❌ "The Board had concerns about the pace of growth..."
- ❌ "There were different views on capital allocation..."
- ❌ "The Board pushed for more conservative guidance..."
- ❌ Vague answers about "healthy debate" without specifics
- ❌ Defensive tone when discussing Board oversight

**GREEN FLAGS** (Strong governance):
- ✅ "The Board and management are fully aligned on strategy"
- ✅ Specific examples of Board support for management initiatives
- ✅ Board proactively supported CEO transition and succession planning
- ✅ Regular Board-management strategy sessions with documented alignment
- ✅ Board has relevant industry expertise (construction, energy, utilities)

### **UPGRADE/DOWNGRADE Criteria**:

**DECREASE to 2-3/100** (Upgrade):
- ✅ Board and management fully aligned on strategy
- ✅ Board has strong industry expertise (construction, energy, utilities)
- ✅ CEO transition was planned and supported by Board
- ✅ No strategic disagreements in past 3 years

**MAINTAIN 3-5/100** (No change):
- ✅ Board-management alignment is strong
- ⚠️ Minor differences on timing or pace, but no fundamental disagreements

**INCREASE to 10-15/100** (Moderate concern):
- ❌ Board-management tension on capital allocation or M&A strategy
- ❌ Board pushed for more conservative guidance (suggests management was aggressive)
- ❌ CEO transition was "unexpected" or "unplanned"

**INCREASE to 20-30/100** (Material concern):
- ❌ Significant Board-management disagreements on strategy
- ❌ Board forced CEO change due to performance concerns
- ❌ Board lacks industry expertise or independence

### **Follow-Up Questions**:

1. "How often does the Board meet with management to review strategy?" (Quarterly minimum is standard)
2. "Does the Board have a Risk Committee? Who chairs it?" (Should exist for public company)
3. "What industry expertise does the Board bring?" (Construction, energy, utilities experience critical)
4. "How was Koti Vadlamudi selected? Was it a competitive search?" (Planned succession vs. reactive hire)

## QUESTION 3: NON-GAAP MEASURES & SEC COMMENT RISK

### **The Question**:

> "Your investor presentation and earnings materials use multiple non-GAAP measures including EBITDA, Adjusted EBITDA, Adjusted Net Income, and Adjusted EPS. Have you received any SEC comment letters regarding your non-GAAP disclosures in the past three years? How do you ensure that your non-GAAP adjustments are conservative, consistent year-over-year, and clearly reconciled to GAAP measures?"

### **Why This Matters**:

- **Securities Litigation Trigger**: Aggressive non-GAAP measures are a common basis for securities class actions
- **SEC Scrutiny**: SEC has increased focus on non-GAAP metrics (C&DI updates in 2016, 2022, 2024)
- **Disclosure Risk**: If adjustments are unclear or inconsistent, creates "misleading disclosure" claim
- **Earnings Call Gap**: Non-GAAP measures used extensively but quality NOT discussed

### **What to Listen For**:

**RED FLAGS** (Disclosure concern):
- ❌ "We received an SEC comment letter about non-GAAP measures in [year]"
- ❌ "We changed our non-GAAP methodology in 2024/2025"
- ❌ "Adjustments vary depending on the quarter"
- ❌ Defensive tone or vague answers about "standard industry practice"
- ❌ Unable to clearly explain what's being adjusted and why

**GREEN FLAGS** (Strong disclosure):
- ✅ "No SEC comment letters on non-GAAP measures in past 3 years"
- ✅ "Our non-GAAP adjustments are consistent year-over-year"
- ✅ "We follow SEC C&DI guidance and GAAP reconciliation requirements"
- ✅ Clear explanation of what's adjusted (e.g., "one-time restructuring charges, acquisition costs")
- ✅ Conservative approach (adjustments are infrequent and clearly non-recurring)

### **UPGRADE/DOWNGRADE Criteria**:

**DECREASE to 2-3/100** (Upgrade):
- ✅ No SEC comment letters on non-GAAP in past 3 years
- ✅ Adjustments are consistent year-over-year
- ✅ Conservative approach (few adjustments, clearly non-recurring)
- ✅ Clear GAAP reconciliations in all materials

**MAINTAIN 3-5/100** (No change):
- ✅ No SEC comment letters
- ⚠️ Adjustments are standard (stock-based comp, acquisition costs, restructuring)

**INCREASE to 10-15/100** (Moderate concern):
- ❌ SEC comment letter received in past 3 years (even if resolved)
- ❌ Non-GAAP methodology changed recently
- ❌ Adjustments are frequent or unclear

**INCREASE to 20-30/100** (Material concern):
- ❌ Multiple SEC comment letters on non-GAAP
- ❌ Adjustments are aggressive (e.g., adjusting for "normal" operating expenses)
- ❌ Non-GAAP metrics are more prominent than GAAP in earnings materials

### **Follow-Up Questions**:

1. "What specific items do you adjust for in Adjusted EBITDA?" (Should be non-recurring: restructuring, acquisition costs, impairments)
2. "Have your adjustments been consistent over the past 3 years?" (Consistency is key)
3. "Do you have a formal policy for determining what's 'non-recurring'?" (Should have documented policy)
4. "How do you ensure compliance with SEC C&DI guidance?" (Should reference specific guidance)

## QUESTION 4: ENERGY VS. UTILITIES MARGIN DIFFERENTIAL

### **The Question**:

> "Your Q3 2025 results show Energy segment gross margin at 10.1% versus Utilities at 11.7%, but Energy is 64% of your revenue. What's driving this margin differential? Given that Energy is your larger segment, what's your outlook for Energy margins in 2026, and are there any structural reasons why Energy margins would remain below Utilities?"

### **Why This Matters**:

- **Margin Compression Risk**: Lower-margin segment (Energy) is larger (64% of revenue)
- **Guidance Risk**: If Energy margins compress further, overall profitability at risk → guidance miss → securities litigation
- **Earnings Call Gap**: Margins discussed but differential NOT explained
- **Structural vs. Temporary**: Need to understand if 10.1% is temporary (pipeline drag, fewer closeouts) or structural

### **What to Listen For**:

**RED FLAGS** (Margin concern):
- ❌ "Energy margins are structurally lower due to competitive dynamics"
- ❌ "We expect Energy margins to remain in the 9-10% range"
- ❌ "Pipeline margins will continue to be a drag in 2026"
- ❌ "Fixed-price contracts in Energy are more competitive"
- ❌ Vague answers or inability to explain differential

**GREEN FLAGS** (Margin improvement):
- ✅ "Energy margins are temporarily compressed due to pipeline drag and fewer project closeouts in Q3"
- ✅ "We expect Energy margins to improve to 11-12% in 2026 as pipeline recovers and renewables projects close out"
- ✅ "Utilities margins are lower due to lack of storm work in Q3 (one-time factor)"
- ✅ "Energy and Utilities margins should converge to 11-12% range in 2026"
- ✅ Specific explanation of temporary factors (pipeline drag, fewer closeouts)

### **UPGRADE/DOWNGRADE Criteria**:

**DECREASE to 2-3/100** (Upgrade):
- ✅ Energy margins expected to improve to 11-12% in 2026
- ✅ Q3 compression is temporary (pipeline drag + fewer closeouts)
- ✅ Structural margin profile is 11-12% for both segments

**MAINTAIN 3-5/100** (No change):
- ✅ Energy margins expected to remain 10-11% (stable)
- ⚠️ Slight differential is normal due to business mix

**INCREASE to 10-15/100** (Moderate concern):
- ❌ Energy margins expected to remain 9-10% (below Utilities)
- ❌ Structural reasons for lower Energy margins (competitive dynamics, fixed-price pressure)
- ❌ No clear path to margin improvement in 2026

**INCREASE to 20-30/100** (Material concern):
- ❌ Energy margins expected to decline further (below 9%)
- ❌ Management unable to explain differential
- ❌ Guidance at risk if Energy margins compress

### **Follow-Up Questions**:

1. "What was Energy margin in Q1 and Q2 2025?" (Trend analysis - is Q3 an outlier or a trend?)
2. "What percentage of Energy revenue is pipeline?" (Quantify pipeline drag)
3. "How many project closeouts do you expect in Q4 and 2026?" (Closeouts boost margins)
4. "What's your long-term target margin for Energy?" (Should be 11-12% like Utilities)

## QUESTION 5: BACKLOG QUALITY & CANCELLATION RISK

### **The Question**:

> "Your total backlog is at a record $11.9 billion. What's the average project duration across your backlog? What percentage of your backlog is subject to customer cancellation without penalty? And specifically, for the Energy segment, how much of the $4.5 billion backlog is dependent on projects that haven't reached financial close or secured final permits?"

### **Why This Matters**:

- **Revenue Visibility**: Backlog is forward-looking indicator of revenue; cancellation risk affects guidance reliability
- **Energy Segment Risk**: Energy backlog declined 30% (Q3 2025 vs. 2024); need to understand quality of remaining $4.5B
- **Financial Close Risk**: Renewables projects often in backlog before financial close; if financing falls through, backlog evaporates
- **Permit Risk**: Projects without final permits can be delayed or canceled (regulatory risk)
- **Earnings Call Gap**: Backlog growth discussed but cancellation risk NOT addressed

### **What to Listen For**:

**RED FLAGS** (Backlog quality concern):
- ❌ "Most of our backlog is cancellable with 30-60 days notice"
- ❌ "A significant portion of Energy backlog hasn't reached financial close"
- ❌ "Some projects are still awaiting final permits"
- ❌ "Average project duration is 3-5 years" (long duration = higher cancellation risk)
- ❌ Vague answers or inability to quantify cancellation risk

**GREEN FLAGS** (Strong backlog quality):
- ✅ "Less than 10% of backlog is cancellable without penalty"
- ✅ "Over 90% of Energy backlog has reached financial close"
- ✅ "All projects in backlog have final permits or are under construction"
- ✅ "Average project duration is 12-18 months" (short duration = lower cancellation risk)
- ✅ "MSA work (49% of backlog) is highly stable with long-term contracts"

### **UPGRADE/DOWNGRADE Criteria**:

**DECREASE to 2-3/100** (Upgrade):
- ✅ <10% of backlog is cancellable without penalty
- ✅ >90% of Energy backlog has reached financial close
- ✅ All projects have final permits
- ✅ Average project duration <18 months

**MAINTAIN 3-5/100** (No change):
- ✅ 10-20% of backlog is cancellable (moderate risk)
- ✅ 80-90% of Energy backlog has reached financial close
- ⚠️ Some projects awaiting permits but low risk

**INCREASE to 10-15/100** (Moderate concern):
- ❌ 20-30% of backlog is cancellable without penalty
- ❌ 70-80% of Energy backlog has reached financial close (20-30% at risk)
- ❌ Material portion of projects awaiting final permits

**INCREASE to 20-30/100** (Material concern):
- ❌ >30% of backlog is cancellable
- ❌ <70% of Energy backlog has reached financial close
- ❌ Significant permit risk or regulatory uncertainty

### **Follow-Up Questions**:

1. "What percentage of your backlog is MSA work vs. project work?" (MSA = more stable)
2. "For renewables projects in backlog, what percentage have reached financial close?" (Critical for revenue visibility)
3. "Have you experienced any material project cancellations in the past 2 years?" (Historical pattern)
4. "What's your typical backlog-to-revenue conversion rate?" (Should be 80-90% annually for 12-18 month projects)

## QUESTION 6: CEO TRANSITION - KOTI VADLAMUDI BACKGROUND & STRATEGY

### **The Question**:

> "Mr. Vadlamudi, welcome to Primoris. Can you share what attracted you to Primoris, and how does your experience at Jacobs Engineering prepare you for the specific challenges here? Specifically, how do you view the balance between growth and profitability, and are there any strategic priorities you're bringing from Jacobs that you'd like to implement at Primoris?"

### **Why This Matters**:

- **CEO Transition Risk**: New CEO = governance risk; need to assess strategic alignment and experience
- **Jacobs Background**: Jacobs is a larger, more diversified engineering firm; need to understand if Koti's approach fits Primoris
- **Growth vs. Profitability**: Critical to understand if new CEO will push for aggressive growth (higher risk) or disciplined profitability
- **Strategic Changes**: Any major strategy shifts increase execution risk and potential for guidance misses
- **Earnings Call Gap**: CEO transition mentioned but Koti's background and strategy NOT discussed

### **What to Listen For**:

**RED FLAGS** (Strategy concern):
- ❌ "I want to accelerate growth significantly..."
- ❌ "We need to be more aggressive on M&A..."
- ❌ "Jacobs' approach to [X] is different, and I plan to change that here..."
- ❌ Emphasis on "transformation" or "major changes"
- ❌ Lack of specific industry knowledge (construction, energy, utilities)

**GREEN FLAGS** (Strong fit):
- ✅ "Primoris' focus on infrastructure and energy aligns perfectly with my experience at Jacobs"
- ✅ "I'm impressed by Primoris' disciplined approach to project selection and margin management"
- ✅ "My priority is to build on the strong foundation that's already here"
- ✅ Specific examples of relevant experience (energy, utilities, construction)
- ✅ Emphasis on continuity and incremental improvement (not transformation)

### **UPGRADE/DOWNGRADE Criteria**:

**DECREASE to 2-3/100** (Upgrade):
- ✅ Strong relevant experience (energy, utilities, construction at Jacobs)
- ✅ Emphasis on continuity and building on existing strengths
- ✅ Balanced view on growth vs. profitability (disciplined growth)
- ✅ No major strategic changes planned

**MAINTAIN 3-5/100** (No change):
- ✅ Relevant experience but some differences in approach
- ⚠️ Some strategic adjustments planned but incremental

**INCREASE to 10-15/100** (Moderate concern):
- ❌ Limited relevant experience (Jacobs is more diversified, less construction-focused)
- ❌ Emphasis on aggressive growth or transformation
- ❌ Major strategic changes planned (M&A, new markets, new services)

**INCREASE to 20-30/100** (Material concern):
- ❌ No relevant industry experience
- ❌ Emphasis on "transformation" or "major changes"
- ❌ Board-CEO misalignment on strategy (see Question 2)
- ❌ Aggressive growth targets that could compromise margins

### **Follow-Up Questions**:

1. "What specific aspects of your Jacobs experience are most relevant to Primoris?" (Should mention energy, utilities, construction)
2. "How do you view the current balance between growth and profitability at Primoris?" (Should say "appropriate" or "disciplined")
3. "Are there any strategic priorities from Jacobs you'd like to bring to Primoris?" (Should be incremental, not transformational)
4. "How long do you plan to stay in this role?" (Commitment signal - should be "long-term" or "5+ years")
