# Telecommunications Sector Securities Litigation Patterns

## Source Data
Cornerstone Research reports, recent case filings, and industry analysis

## Overview Statistics

From Cornerstone Research (2014-2023):
- **Number of Settlements**: 28 cases
- **Median Settlement**: $11.4 million
- **Median Simplified Tiered Damages**: $381.0 million
- **Settlement as % of Damages**: 4.4%

**Key Characteristics**:
- Fewer cases than tech or pharma but significant damages when they occur
- High damages relative to settlement amounts suggests strong defenses
- Cases often event-driven rather than financial reporting focused

## Major Hazard Categories

### 1. Network Performance and Coverage Claims

**Description**: Misrepresentations about network quality, coverage, speed, or reliability.

**Common Scenarios**:
- Overstating network coverage or geographic reach
- Exaggerating network speed or performance (5G claims)
- Misleading statements about network capacity
- Failure to disclose network congestion or performance issues
- Overpromising on network deployment timelines
- Misrepresenting competitive position vs. rivals

**Underwriting Red Flags**:
- Aggressive marketing claims about network superiority
- Rapid 5G deployment claims without infrastructure investment
- Customer complaints about service quality
- Network outages or service disruptions
- Gap between advertised and actual speeds
- Competitive pressure to match rival claims
- Limited third-party network testing validation

### 2. Subscriber Growth and Churn Issues

**Description**: Misrepresenting subscriber trends, customer acquisition costs, or retention rates.

**Common Scenarios**:
- Overstating subscriber growth projections
- Hiding increasing churn rates
- Failing to disclose impact of competitive threats
- Misrepresenting customer acquisition cost trends
- Concealing subsidy program impacts on subscriber base
- Misleading statements about customer lifetime value

**Recent Case Example**:
- **Charter Communications**: Multiple securities suits filed in 2025 over customer decline and failure to disclose information about Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) impact on subscriber base; stock price dropped when information revealed

**Underwriting Red Flags**:
- Dependence on government subsidy programs (ACP, Lifeline)
- Rising customer acquisition costs
- Increasing churn rates
- Competitive market entry or aggressive pricing by rivals
- Subscriber growth slowing without disclosure
- Promotional pricing masking underlying trends
- Geographic concentration of subscriber base

### 3. Cybersecurity Breaches and Data Protection

**Description**: Failure to disclose data breaches, inadequate cybersecurity measures, or misrepresenting security capabilities.

**Common Scenarios**:
- Concealing data breaches from investors
- Delayed disclosure of cybersecurity incidents
- Misrepresenting adequacy of security measures
- Failure to implement security controls
- Downplaying scope or impact of breaches
- Not disclosing vulnerability to cyberattacks

**Recent Case Example**:
- **Dish Network**: Investor fraud suit over concealment of cybersecurity data breach; unlawfully concealed breach from investors

**Underwriting Red Flags**:
- History of data breaches
- Large customer database with personal information
- Payment card data storage
- Limited cybersecurity investment
- No CISO or inadequate security leadership
- Delayed breach notifications
- Regulatory investigations into data security
- Customer data monetization business model

### 4. Technology Deployment and Capital Expenditure

**Description**: Misrepresentations about technology deployment progress, capital requirements, or return on investment.

**Common Scenarios**:
- Overstating 5G deployment progress
- Underestimating capital expenditure requirements
- Misrepresenting ROI on network investments
- Hiding technology deployment delays
- Overpromising on fiber buildout or infrastructure projects
- Concealing obsolescence of existing infrastructure

**Underwriting Red Flags**:
- Aggressive deployment timelines
- Capital expenditure significantly below industry norms
- Technology transition periods (4G to 5G)
- Spectrum acquisition debt burden
- Infrastructure sharing agreements with unclear economics
- Regulatory requirements for network buildout
- Geographic buildout obligations from spectrum auctions

### 5. Regulatory and Spectrum Issues

**Description**: Failure to disclose regulatory risks, spectrum auction results, or compliance challenges.

**Common Scenarios**:
- Concealing regulatory investigations
- Misrepresenting spectrum auction strategies or results
- Failing to disclose compliance violations
- Hiding risks from regulatory changes
- Overstating value of spectrum holdings
- Not disclosing interconnection disputes

**Underwriting Red Flags**:
- FCC investigations or enforcement actions
- Spectrum license renewal risks
- Buildout obligations not being met
- Net neutrality regulatory uncertainty
- State-level regulatory challenges
- Universal service fund obligations
- Interconnection disputes with other carriers

### 6. Merger Integration and Competitive Positioning

**Description**: Misrepresentations about merger synergies, integration progress, or competitive threats.

**Common Scenarios**:
- Overstating merger synergies
- Hiding integration challenges
- Concealing customer losses during integration
- Misrepresenting competitive position post-merger
- Failing to disclose network integration costs
- Understating subscriber churn from merger

**Underwriting Red Flags**:
- Recent major mergers or acquisitions
- Network integration complexity
- Brand consolidation challenges
- Overlapping coverage areas requiring rationalization
- Workforce reductions and service quality impacts
- System integration delays
- Customer confusion during transition

### 7. Content and Media Business Risks (Cable/Satellite)

**Description**: For companies with content businesses, misrepresentations about cord-cutting, content costs, or streaming competition.

**Common Scenarios**:
- Downplaying cord-cutting trends
- Concealing rising content programming costs
- Misrepresenting streaming service performance
- Failing to disclose competitive threats from streaming
- Overstating ability to retain video subscribers
- Hiding margin pressure from content costs

**Underwriting Red Flags**:
- Declining video subscriber base
- Rising programming costs
- Disputes with content providers
- Streaming service losses
- Shift from high-margin video to lower-margin broadband
- Sports programming cost escalation
- Cord-cutting acceleration

## Recent Litigation Trends

### Charter Communications (2025)
Multiple securities class actions filed alleging:
- Failure to disclose customer decline
- Concealment of Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP) impact
- Misleading guidance about subscriber trends
- Stock price decline when information revealed

### Dish Network (2023-2024)
Securities suit over:
- Concealment of cybersecurity data breach
- Failure to timely disclose breach to investors
- Inadequate cybersecurity measures

## Key Underwriting Insights

### Highest Risk Profiles
- Heavy dependence on government subsidy programs
- History of data breaches or weak cybersecurity
- Aggressive 5G deployment claims without capital investment
- Declining subscriber base without adequate disclosure
- Recent major mergers with integration challenges
- Cord-cutting acceleration in video business
- Regulatory investigations or compliance issues

### Moderate Risk Profiles
- Established carriers with stable subscriber base
- Adequate capital investment in network infrastructure
- Transparent disclosure of competitive challenges
- Moderate cybersecurity posture with some incidents
- Standard regulatory compliance issues

### Lower Risk Profiles
- Conservative public statements about network capabilities
- Strong cybersecurity programs and incident response
- Transparent disclosure of subscriber trends
- Adequate capital investment relative to deployment claims
- Diversified revenue streams
- Proactive regulatory compliance
- Realistic competitive positioning statements

## Critical Disclosure Areas

1. **Subscriber Metrics**: Transparent reporting of gross adds, churn, net adds, and ARPU trends
2. **Network Performance**: Honest assessment of coverage, speed, and quality vs. competitors
3. **Cybersecurity**: Timely disclosure of breaches and adequate security measures
4. **Capital Requirements**: Realistic capex guidance for network deployment
5. **Regulatory Risks**: Full disclosure of investigations, compliance issues, and regulatory changes
6. **Competitive Threats**: Honest assessment of competitive position and market share trends
7. **Subsidy Programs**: Clear disclosure of dependence on government programs and impact of changes

## Emerging Risks

1. **5G Overpromising**: Gap between 5G marketing and actual network capabilities
2. **Subsidy Program Dependency**: Risk from expiration or changes to government broadband subsidies
3. **Cybersecurity**: Increasing sophistication of attacks on telecom infrastructure
4. **Cord-Cutting Acceleration**: Faster than expected decline in traditional video subscribers
5. **Spectrum Costs**: Rising costs for spectrum in auctions creating financial pressure
6. **Infrastructure Requirements**: Massive capital needs for fiber and 5G deployment
7. **Competition from Tech Giants**: Entry of big tech into connectivity markets
