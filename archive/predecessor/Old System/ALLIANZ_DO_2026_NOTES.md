## KEY THEMES (Pages 2-5)

### **1. Geopolitical and Emerging Risk Trends**

**Main Message**: Senior managers navigating challenging environment in 2026 with:
- Geopolitical and macroeconomic uncertainty
- Artificial intelligence (AI)
- Cyber risks
- Forever chemicals
- Elevating D&O liability

### **2. Litigation Landscape**

**Securities Class Actions (SCA)**:
- Peaked 2017-2019
- On course to fall just short of 2024 (229 total)
- **BUT severity has increased**: Average settlement value for first 6 months of 2025 rose 27% to US$56m

**Key Quote**: "Political and social shifts have emerged as a significant source of potential liability"

### **3. Shareholder Activism & Derivative Litigation**

**Trend**: Surge in derivative litigation
- Investors bring claims against directors on behalf of company
- Typically follows shareholder class actions (breach of fiduciary duty)
- **13 of 18 shareholder derivative settlements** over $100m settled in last 5 years
- Increasing in severity

### **4. Non-Accounting and Event-Driven Claims**

**Key Drivers**:
- Mergers and acquisitions
- Regulatory enforcement
- Consumer or employee litigation

**Frequency**: More than doubled in past 10 years

**Environmental and Product Safety**:
- Forever chemicals emerging as particularly challenging risk
- Costly litigation and large settlements

### **5. Technology and AI Risks**

**Growing Source of D&O Liability**:
- Data security and cyber-related incidents
- Ransomware attacks and outages = major frequency driver
- **AI = next frontier**: Significant uptick in AI-related filings in first half of 2025 (50+ lawsuits in past 5 years)

**Two AI Risk Types**:
1. **AI-washing**: Management overstated benefits to companies
2. **Understated risks**: Management understated AI risks to profitability

**Quote from Jarrod Schlesinger, Global Head of Financial Lines and Cyber, Allianz Commercial**:

> "D&Os are coming under increasing scrutiny from shareholders and regulators in what is a challenging geopolitical and economic environment. ### **Pages 7-10: CYBER AS MAJOR D&O RISK**

#### **Key Finding**: "Cyber has emerged as a major cause of claims against D&Os"

**Cyber Risk Ranking**:
- #1 risk in Allianz Risk Barometer 2025 (fourth consecutive year)
- Persistent threat evidenced by recent ransomware attacks (retailers, airlines, auto manufacturers in US and Europe)

**Quote from Alfred Mora, Chief Underwriter, Financial Lines, Germany and Switzerland, Allianz Commercial**:
> "More and more we see companies and their investors holding board members responsible for cyber incidents. The willingness to seek damages in court is increasing, partly out of fear of recourse claims by shareholders or supervisory authorities. The threshold for asserting internal liability is falling while the number of cyber-attacks with a systemic impact has increased"

---

#### **Regulatory Drivers**

**EU Regulations**:
1. **GDPR** (General Data Protection Regulation)
2. **California Privacy Rights Act**
3. **DORA** (Digital Operational Resilience Act)
4. **NIS2** (Network and Information Security directive) - establishes cyber security standards for critical sectors

**NIS2 Impact**:
- Extends robust cyber security and reporting to more companies and supply chains
- Failure to comply: Fines up to €10mn or 2% of global turnover
- **Increases personal accountability** for D&Os
- D&Os directly responsible for overseeing cyber security, risk management, incident response

**Example - Illumina Inc.**:
- US life sciences company
- US$9.8mn settlement with US Department of Justice
- Failed to incorporate adequate cyber security in genomic sequencing systems sold to federal agencies

**Quote from David De Greef, Head of Financial Lines and Cyber, Benelux, Allianz Commercial**:
> "Following a cyber incident, investors will ask whether the company could have been better prepared and protected, and they will look to the directors to see if they did enough to give the business the best chance of withstanding an attack. This is a real threat for directors. With ongoing cyber-attacks and increasing regulation it will be easier to go after them. And while they might not be at fault, they can still face significant defense and investigation costs"

---

#### **D&O Liability from Cyber**

**How D&O Exposure Arises**:
- Duty to oversee organization's cyber security posture
- If cyber incident results in financial loss → Legal claims from shareholders, customers, suppliers (and sometimes company itself)
- Board failed to implement adequate cyber risk controls or business continuity plans
- Shareholder litigation for perceived lack of cyber insurance coverage

**Numerous shareholder lawsuits** filed following ransomware attacks and data breach incidents in US

---

#### **Growing Source of D&O Claims**

**Quote from De Greef**:
> "Ten years ago, this was not really a boardroom topic, and we would not have seen claims against directors and officers for lack of attention or investment in cyber security. But with increasing media and regulatory interest, we are seeing an increase in frequency of claims related to cyber"

---

#### **Cyber Claims Analysis (Allianz Data)**

**2025 (First 6 Months) - Large Claims >€1mn**:
- **41%**: Attack-driven losses (with data exfiltration)
- **19%**: Attack-driven losses (without data exfiltration)
- **15%**: Contingent business interruption (CBI)/supply chain
- **25%**: Other categories

**2024 - Large Claims >€1mn**:
- **25%**: Attack-driven losses (with data exfiltration)
- **20%**: Attack-driven losses (without data exfiltration)
- **6%**: Contingent business interruption (CBI)/supply chain
- **10%**: Business interruption due to technical failure
- **18%**: Non-attack data breaches (wrongful collection/processing)
- **21%**: Other categories

**Key Trends**:
1. **Ransomware = 60% of value** of large cyber insurance claims (>€1mn) in first half 2025 (Allianz Cyber Security Resilience Outlook)
2. **Data exfiltration doubled**: 40% of value of large cyber claims in first half 2025 included data theft (up from 25% in all of 2024)
3. **Non-attack incidents = 28%** of large cyber claims by value in 2024 (wrongful collection/processing of data, business interruption from technical failure)
4. **CBI/supply chain = 15%** of large cyber claims in first half 2025 (up from 6% in 2024)

**Risk Landscape Expanding**:
- Attackers targeting smaller firms (typically less resilient)
- Shift from purely extraction-based ransomware to double extortion (including data exfiltration)
- Non-attack incidents (wrongful data collection/processing, technical failures) = significant portion
- Supply chain/IT partner breaches harder to control

## PAGES 11-15: PFAS, PRIVATE COMPANIES, BANKRUPTCY RISK

### **Pages 11-12: PFAS (Forever Chemicals) - Emerging D&O Risk**

**What are PFAS**:
- Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (developed 1940s)
- Used in nonstick cookware, waterproof/stain resistant fabric, food packaging, firefighting foams
- Linked to serious health issues: Increased cancer risk, reduced fertility
- Do not easily degrade → Widely distributed and persistent
- 23,000 sites in Europe alone contaminated by forever chemicals (Forever Pollution Project)

**Billion-Dollar Litigation**:
- Mounting litigation in US (also civil proceedings in Europe, Canada, Australia)
- As of year-end 2024: **30 US states initiated litigation** against PFAS manufacturers (per Safer States coalition)

**Major Settlements**:
1. **3M**: US$10.3bn to settle water contamination claims in 2023
2. **AFFF Firefighting Foam MDL**: 12,000+ pending lawsuits consolidated into multidistrict litigation (PFAS-related claims from firefighters, municipalities, utilities)
3. **Verisk estimate**: PFAS water contamination and environmental litigation in US could eventually cost as much as **$165bn** (depending on regulatory/legal trends)

**D&O Exposure**:
- Corporate and securities litigation
- Shareholders allege directors failed to identify potential PFAS liability exposures
- Failed to adequately disclose risks to investors
- "When stock price drops, investors may point finger at D&Os and say that was a risk you should have been aware of and disclosed"

**Quote from Dan Holloway, Head of Management Liability Commercial and Professional Indemnity, Allianz Commercial**:
> "Like other event-driven directors and officers (D&O) losses, we are seeing securities litigation where shareholders allege that the directors failed to identify potential PFAS liability exposures and did not adequately disclose those risks to investors. When the stock price drops, investors may point the finger at D&Os and say that was a risk you should have been aware of and disclosed"

**Key Quote**:
> "The duty of a director now is greater than ever before. They need to be able to look forward, as well as back, when it comes to assessing risks to their company"

**Horizon Scanning**:
- D&O liability for emerging risks like PFAS is in how such risks are identified and disclosed
- Unlike forever chemicals, artificial intelligence, geopolitical risks, climate change or cyber security → Directors need to seek guidance when it comes to disclosure
- Traditionally risk management = historical view, foreseeable risks
- Increasingly directors expected to anticipate horizon scanning and disclose risks that haven't yet occurred but may happen

**Quote from Holloway**:
> "Emerging risks shine a light on how the directors and officers run a company. The duty of a director now is greater than ever before. They need to be able to look forward, as well as back, when it comes to assessing risks to their company"

**D&O Insurance Coverage**:
- Securities actions related to forever chemicals have not been successful to date
- However, PFAS liability is still developing
- Likelihood of further securities litigation cannot be discounted
- Unlike general liability and product liability claims, D&O policies do not require firm scientific evidence of damage to respond

**Quote from Holloway**:
> "D&O insurance is very much a defense costs policy. While the link between forever chemicals and human health may not have been 100% proven, directors can still find themselves in the firing line through securities litigation and regulatory investigations, and these legal costs would be covered"

**Underwriting Challenges**:
- For D&O insurers, underwriting emerging exposures like PFAS is challenging, especially when risks not well established
- Science around impact of PFAS on environment and human health not conclusive
- While studies have linked exposure with adverse health effects, research still ongoing to determine how different levels of PFAS exposure can lead to variety of health effects (per US EPA)
- As yet, D&O insurers not applying restrictive language for PFAS-related exposures
- Considerable uncertainty around potential liability remains

**Quote from Holloway**:
> "PFAS exposure does not automatically mean a D&O claim. But this issue isn't going away. These chemicals are everywhere and take a long time to degrade naturally. So how boards identify, monitor and disclose these risks will be critical going forward"

**Task Force Approach**:
- Allianz Commercial uses insights from emerging risks task force to consider how they might materialize as D&O exposures
- Many companies would do well to adopt similar approach

**Quote from Holloway**:
> "A task force is the best way to identify potential emerging risks. By having a task force you bring in different disciplines from the business and different perspectives that can help you identify potential trends, like a social or demographic shift or a new technology, and explore how they might impact the business. That is how directors will be expected to operate going forward"

### **Pages 13-14: Private Companies - Trade Risks Challenge D&Os**

**Key Message**: D&O liabilities for privately held companies can differ from publicly traded firms. While listed companies may have higher exposure to securities litigation, directors of private companies still face significant risks.

**Top Sources of Private D&O Claims**:
- Bankruptcy and regulatory enforcement actions
- Breach of fiduciary duty (misleading or inadequate disclosure or negligence)

**Current Environment**:
- Challenging business environment (tariffs, weak demand, rising costs)
- Increasing risks of claims against directors of private companies

**Quote from Peter Carazza, Regional Head for Private Company Business, Management Liability, North America, Allianz Commercial**:
> "Directors of private companies are having to navigate their organizations through some very choppy waters. At a time when margins are already being challenged, geopolitical instability and technology transformation are bringing additional issues. Directors can expect additional scrutiny from stakeholders, placing even greater emphasis on good governance and the need for robust D&O insurance coverage"

---

#### **Trade Tariffs Risk**

**Allianz Trade Global Survey (May 2025)**:
- 42% of exporting companies expect turnover to decline by -2% to as much as -10% over next 12 months
- Almost 2/3 of companies plan to find new markets for exports and supply
- Ongoing trade tensions will continue to weigh on world economy
- Growth in global trade expected to slow to +0.6% in 2026 from +2% in 2025 (Allianz Trade forecasts)

**Financial and Regulatory Implications**:
- Tariffs will have financial and regulatory implications for many private businesses
- Under pressure from relatively low growth and higher interest rates
- Without capital and resources of large corporates, many private mid-sized companies will find it harder to pivot and navigate changing trade and tariff environment
- Tariffs will affect sectors differently with winners and losers
- **Cyclical, capital intensive, and protectionist sectors most at risk**: Automotive, retail, manufacturing, construction

**D&O Liability from Tariffs**:
> "Trade tariffs can bring an additional risk of regulatory investigation and enforcement actions, and litigation for private companies"

**Quote from Sarah Geraghty, Global Underwriting and Mid-Corp Product Lead, Financial Lines, Allianz Commercial**:
- Shareholders may allege directors/officers breached fiduciary duties by not taking adequate precautionary measures or not sufficiently adapting to changing conditions
- Consumer class actions could arise if company perceived to have misrepresented tariff impacts in marketing or passed on costs in allegedly deceptive or unfair ways

**Compliance Challenges**:
- Companies and directors could potentially face investigations and enforcement actions from regulators or customs authorities or civil litigants
- Allegations of tariff evasion or misleading disclosures
- US authorities can use variety of tools to enforce tariff compliance, including False Claims Act (FCA)
- **Example**: US government recently filed FCA complaint against South Carolina furniture company alleging firm falsified documents and underreported tariffs

**Quote from Carazza**:
> "For D&Os, mitigating tariff-related risks comes down to robust governance and staying informed about the evolving political and trade landscape. Scenario planning and risk assessments focusing on potential tariff outcomes and supply chain dependencies can help boards anticipate potential financial and operational implications, as well as support timely and accurate disclosure"

### **Page 15: Elevated Bankruptcy Risk**

**Insolvency Trends**:
- According to Allianz Trade: Global business insolvencies expected to rise by +6% in 2025 and +5% in 2026
- Next year will mark **five consecutive years of increases** to reach record high number of bankruptcies
- **+24% above pre-pandemic average**

**US "Mega Bankruptcies"**:
- Rise in "mega bankruptcies" (companies with over US$1bn in reported assets)
- First half of 2025: 17 such bankruptcies
- Highest number of any half-year period since Covid-19 pandemic
- Over past 12 months: 32 mega bankruptcies (well above 2005-2024 historical average of 23)

**Sectors at Risk**:
- Insolvency risks particularly **concentrated in automotive, construction, retail and consumer goods sectors**

**D&O Heightened Liability**:
- D&Os' liabilities heightened during and following bankruptcy proceedings
- Even suggestion that business may be in financial distress can increase risk of litigation
- Lenders, investors, other parties look to protect their interests
- In tough and volatile trading environment, insolvency can also affect companies in relatively good health
- Unexpected failure of large company can have domino effect of insolvencies among suppliers

**Quote from David Ackerman, Global Practice Leader, Commercial Management Liability and Financial Lines Claims, Allianz Commercial**:
> "The conduct of directors during the period a company approached insolvency will fall under increasing scrutiny by stakeholders once bankruptcy is declared. This is true whether the insolvent company is large or small, or public or private"

**Quote from Peter Carazza**:
> "In the event of a company failure, creditors and bankruptcy trustees may look to recover funds by targeting directors and officers, exposing D&Os to potentially large defense costs"

**Quote from Sarah Geraghty**:
> "It's important that directors understand their expanded fiduciary duties in the event of an insolvency, seek expert advice and keep detailed records of all key decisions and supporting information. Such information will prove critical if D&Os face claims of mismanagement or allegations of conflicts of interest"

## PAGES 16-20: MARKET DYNAMICS & REGIONAL OUTLOOKS

### **Page 16: Market Dynamics - State of D&O Insurance Sector**

#### **US Market**

**Overall Trend**:
- Financial lines commercial business showing signs of stabilizing
- Segments remain more competitive (private D&O)
- Broadly little change in insurance buying behavior
- Some insureds in better position to purchase additional limits

**Claims Frequency**:
- Expecting slight reduction in frequency of securities class action claims in 2025
- 216 down from 229 (based on first-half activity, per NERA)

**Claims Severity**:
- Average settlement value increased significantly in H1 2025 to **US$56mn**
- Up from inflation adjusted $44mn in 2024
- Up from $38mn average of prior nine years

**Quote from Eric Wedin, Head of Financial Lines, North America, Allianz Commercial**

---

#### **Asia Market**

**Overall Trend**:
- Heightened competition from abundance of capacity globally eyeing Asia-domiciled risks
- Overall commercial D&O market size shrinking due to rate erosion and limited new business

**Client Behavior**:
- More clients cutting insurance spending
- Being more cautious about costs
- Driving tenders and remarketing by clients seeking more economical solutions
- Driving intense broker competition and rate pressure

**Terms and Conditions**:
- Starting to move with trends of lower deductibles and wider coverages provided by market

**Claims**:
- Claims frequency remains low (whether local claims or US securities claims)
- Higher severity in terms of settlement amounts for prior year claims being resolved

**Market Opportunity**:
- Good time for new potential buyers to consider purchasing D&O insurance because of solutions and choices available

**Quote from Josephine Tam, Head of Financial Lines and Cyber, Asia, Allianz Commercial**
