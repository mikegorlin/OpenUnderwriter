## CORE RULES

### Rule 1: Complete All Checks
- Work through ALL ~120 checks in Part 2
- Do NOT skip checks or categories
- Document findings for each check (even if "LOW RISK" or "NOT APPLICABLE")

### Rule 2: Systematic Approach
- Follow checklist order: Categories A → B → C → D → E → F
- Complete each category before moving to next
- Use hierarchical check numbering (e.g., A.1.1, B.3.2, F.7.4)

### Rule 3: Evidence-Based Analysis
- Every finding MUST be supported by specific evidence
- Cite sources for all claims
- Include dates, numbers, and specific details

### Rule 4: Verification Standards
- **Hard data** (financial, regulatory): 1 authoritative source required (SEC filings, regulatory databases)
- **Soft signals** (social media, reviews, news): 2+ independent sources required
- **Critical/High findings**: Must verify with primary sources before flagging

### Rule 5: Tiered Severity Assessment
For each check, assign ONE severity level:
- 🔴 **CRITICAL**: Most severe findings requiring immediate attention
- 🔴 **HIGH**: Significant risk findings
- ⚠️ **MODERATE**: Caution items worth noting
- ✅ **LOW**: Pass / low risk / no concerns
- 🟣 **UNKNOWN**: Insufficient data to assess

### Rule 6: Stock Decline Attribution (REQUIRED)
- For ANY stock decline >10% in past 12 months:
  - Identify trigger event with specific date
  - Document stock reaction magnitude
  - Compare to peer/sector performance
  - Attribute cause (company-specific vs. sector-wide vs. macro)
- This is Check B.5.1 - do NOT skip

### Rule 7: No Pricing or Underwriting Recommendations
- Do NOT suggest specific premium multiples
- Do NOT recommend retention amounts
- Do NOT specify coverage limits
- Do NOT provide detailed policy terms
- ONLY present findings and suggest areas for underwriter review

### Rule 8: Objective Presentation
- Present findings objectively, not opinions
- Use professional, formal business writing
- Avoid speculation or assumptions
- Clearly mark when data is unavailable (🟣 UNKNOWN)

### Rule 9: Alternative Data Verification
- Category F (Alternative Data) findings require 2+ independent sources
- Cross-verify soft signals (Glassdoor + LinkedIn + Form 8-K, for example)
- Do NOT flag based on single unreliable source
- Document verification method

### Rule 10: Comprehensive Output
- Generate complete referral document using Part 3 template
- Include all sections (Executive Summary through Appendix)
- Provide top 10 critical concerns ranked by severity
- Suggest targeted underwriting questions based on findings
- List all sources consulted

## SEVERITY CRITERIA GUIDANCE

### 🔴 CRITICAL (Immediate Red Flags)
- Active securities class action + settlement >$50M
- Criminal charges filed (DOJ)
- Financial restatement + SEC investigation
- Auditor resigned citing fraud concerns
- Bankruptcy filing (Chapter 7 or 11)
- Going concern + cash runway <6 months
- Stock down >50% + company-specific fraud/regulatory trigger
- CEO + CFO departed within 90 days (unplanned)
- Multiple derivative suits + Caremark claims
- Material data breach + >10M records + regulatory penalties >$50M

### 🔴 HIGH (Significant Risk)
- Active securities litigation + settlement $10-50M
- SEC enforcement settlement <24 months
- Material weakness in internal controls (not remediated)
- Auditor change with disagreement
- Cash runway 6-12 months + negative OCF
- Stock down 30-50% + company-specific trigger
- >30% C-suite departed <12 months (unplanned)
- Activist campaign with demands for changes
- Short interest >20% + credible short seller report

### ⚠️ MODERATE (Caution Items)
- Securities litigation dismissed <12 months
- SEC comment letter (resolved)
- Significant deficiency in controls (remediated)
- Cash runway 12-18 months
- Stock down 10-30% + company-specific trigger
- 10-30% C-suite departed (mix planned/unplanned)
- Some shareholder proposals with <30% support

### ✅ LOW (Pass / No Concerns)
- No securities litigation in 5+ years
- Clean regulatory record
- Effective internal controls
- Stable Big 4 auditor >5 years
- Cash runway >24 months + positive OCF
- Stock performance in line with peers/sector
- <10% C-suite turnover + planned transitions
- Strong governance practices

### 🟣 UNKNOWN (Insufficient Data)
- Data not publicly available
- Source not accessible
- Conflicting information requiring clarification
- Requires manual research beyond automated sources

# SECTION 1: ANALYSIS INSTRUCTIONS

## CATEGORY A: LITIGATION & REGULATORY RISK (~20 checks)

### A.1: COMPANY PROFILE & CLASSIFICATION

**A.1.1: Basic Company Information**
- **Risk Area**: Foundational company data for risk assessment
- **Sources**: 10-K, company website, Yahoo Finance
- **Assess**: Company name, ticker, industry/sector, headquarters, fiscal year end, market cap, public company tenure
- **Severity**: 🟣 UNKNOWN if key data missing | ✅ LOW if complete profile obtained

**A.1.2: Market Capitalization & Life Stage**
- **Risk Area**: Company size and maturity affecting D&O risk profile
- **Sources**: Yahoo Finance, 10-K financial history
- **Assess**: Current market cap, market cap tier (mega/large/mid/small/micro), life stage (mature/growth/pre-revenue/distressed), years profitable
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Micro-cap (<$300M) + pre-revenue + distressed
  - 🔴 HIGH: Small-cap + pre-revenue OR distressed
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Mid-cap + growth stage
  - ✅ LOW: Large/mega-cap + mature + profitable 5+ years

**A.1.3: SPAC Status & Performance**
- **Risk Area**: De-SPAC companies with elevated litigation risk
- **Sources**: 8-K, merger documents, stock performance
- **Assess**: SPAC merger date, projections vs. actuals, stock performance post-merger, litigation filed
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: De-SPAC <24mo + projections missed >50% + stock down >60% + litigation filed
  - 🔴 HIGH: De-SPAC <24mo + projections missed 30-50% + stock down 40-60%
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: De-SPAC <24mo + projections missed <30%
  - ✅ LOW: Not a SPAC OR de-SPAC >24mo + met projections

---

### A.2: SECURITIES LITIGATION

**A.2.1: Securities Class Action Lawsuits**
- **Risk Area**: Active or recent securities fraud litigation
- **Sources**: Stanford Securities Clearinghouse, 8-K, 10-K/10-Q Legal Proceedings, PACER
- **Assess**: Active lawsuits, dismissed <24mo, class period, allegations (10b-5, Section 11/12), estimated settlement, parallel derivative suits
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Active lawsuit + estimated settlement >$50M OR >10% market cap
  - 🔴 HIGH: Active lawsuit + settlement $10-50M OR multiple active lawsuits (2+) OR accounting fraud allegations
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Lawsuit dismissed <12mo (appeal risk) OR settlement <$10M
  - ✅ LOW: No securities litigation in 5+ years

**A.2.2: Derivative Lawsuits**
- **Risk Area**: Shareholder derivative suits alleging breach of fiduciary duty
- **Sources**: Delaware Court of Chancery, PACER, company disclosures
- **Assess**: Active derivative suits, Caremark duty of oversight claims, demand refused/excused, parallel securities action
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: 2+ active derivative suits + Caremark claims + parallel securities action
  - 🔴 HIGH: 1 active derivative suit + Caremark claims OR demand refused
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Derivative suit dismissed <12mo
  - ✅ LOW: No derivative litigation in 5+ years

---

### A.3: REGULATORY ENFORCEMENT

**A.3.1: SEC Enforcement Actions**
- **Risk Area**: SEC civil enforcement for accounting/disclosure violations
- **Sources**: SEC.gov Litigation Releases, 8-K, company disclosures
- **Assess**: Active enforcement, settlement <24mo, violations alleged, penalties, officer/director bars, disgorgement
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Active enforcement OR settlement <12mo + penalties >$25M OR officer bars
  - 🔴 HIGH: Settlement 12-24mo + penalties $5-25M OR disgorgement required
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Settlement >24mo + penalties <$5M
  - ✅ LOW: No SEC enforcement in 5+ years

**A.3.2: DOJ Criminal Investigations**
- **Risk Area**: Criminal charges against company or executives
- **Sources**: DOJ press releases, 8-K, news, court records
- **Assess**: Criminal investigation disclosed, charges filed, target letters, plea agreements, deferred prosecution agreements (DPA)
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Criminal charges filed against company or executives
  - 🔴 HIGH: Target letter disclosed OR investigation disclosed + subpoenas issued
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: DPA or NPA entered <36mo
  - ✅ LOW: No criminal investigations disclosed

**A.3.3: FCPA Enforcement**
- **Risk Area**: Foreign Corrupt Practices Act violations
- **Sources**: DOJ/SEC FCPA database, 8-K, 10-K risk factors
- **Assess**: FCPA investigation, criminal/civil charges, penalties, compliance monitor, internal controls remediation
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Criminal FCPA charges OR penalty >$100M
  - 🔴 HIGH: Active FCPA investigation OR settlement <36mo + penalty $25-100M
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Settlement >36mo OR penalty <$25M
  - ✅ LOW: No FCPA issues OR strong compliance program

**A.3.4: Antitrust Enforcement**
- **Risk Area**: DOJ/FTC antitrust investigation or enforcement
- **Sources**: DOJ/FTC press releases, 8-K, news, court filings
- **Assess**: Antitrust investigation, civil/criminal charges, merger blocked, consent decree, penalties
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Criminal antitrust charges OR merger blocked + litigation
  - 🔴 HIGH: Active DOJ/FTC investigation OR settlement <24mo
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Consent decree OR settlement >24mo
  - ✅ LOW: No antitrust issues

**A.3.5: Industry-Specific Regulatory Actions**
- **Risk Area**: FDA, EPA, CFPB, state AG, or other regulator enforcement
- **Sources**: FDA.gov, EPA.gov, CFPB.gov, state AG websites, 8-K
- **Assess**: Warning letters, consent decrees, enforcement actions, penalties, product recalls, facility shutdowns
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: FDA CRL for lead product OR >10 FDA warnings <24mo OR EPA criminal referral OR CFPB penalty >$100M
  - 🔴 HIGH: 5-10 FDA warnings OR EPA consent decree OR CFPB complaints up >300% YoY
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: 1-4 FDA warnings OR minor EPA violations
  - ✅ LOW: Clean regulatory record

### A.4: FINANCIAL STATEMENT RED FLAGS

**A.4.1: Financial Restatements**
- **Risk Area**: Accounting errors requiring restatement
- **Sources**: 8-K Item 4.02, 10-K/A filings, auditor communications
- **Assess**: Restatement announced, magnitude (% revenue/net income), reason, SEC investigation, auditor involvement
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Restatement + SEC investigation + >5% revenue OR >10% net income
  - 🔴 HIGH: Restatement >5% revenue OR >10% net income (no investigation)
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Restatement <5% revenue AND <10% net income
  - ✅ LOW: No restatements in 5+ years

**A.4.2: Auditor Changes & Resignations**
- **Risk Area**: Auditor departure indicating problems
- **Sources**: 8-K Item 4.01, auditor letters
- **Assess**: Auditor change, resigned vs. dismissed, disagreements cited, fraud concerns, going concern
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Auditor resigned + cited disagreement OR fraud concerns
  - 🔴 HIGH: Auditor resigned (no specific reason) OR dismissed + disagreement
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Auditor dismissed (no disagreement) OR changed to smaller firm
  - ✅ LOW: Stable Big 4 auditor >5 years

**A.4.3: Internal Controls Deficiencies**
- **Risk Area**: Material weaknesses enabling fraud or error
- **Sources**: 10-K Item 9A, auditor reports
- **Assess**: Material weakness disclosed, significant deficiency, area affected (revenue recognition, fraud risk, IT controls), remediation status
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Material weakness + revenue recognition OR fraud risk area + not remediated
  - 🔴 HIGH: Material weakness + other areas + not remediated
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Significant deficiency OR material weakness remediated
  - ✅ LOW: Effective internal controls

**A.4.4: Revenue Recognition Issues**
- **Risk Area**: Aggressive or questionable revenue recognition
- **Sources**: 10-K revenue footnote, SEC comment letters, MD&A
- **Assess**: Revenue recognition policy, SEC comment letters, changes required, unbilled receivables, deferred revenue trends
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: SEC comment letter + changes required + revenue restated
  - 🔴 HIGH: Aggressive revenue recognition + high unbilled receivables (>20% AR)
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: SEC comment letter resolved OR complex revenue model
  - ✅ LOW: Conservative revenue recognition + clean SEC review

**A.4.5: Asset Impairments**
- **Risk Area**: Major write-downs indicating overpayment or failure
- **Sources**: 10-Q/10-K impairment disclosures, goodwill footnote
- **Assess**: Impairment as % total assets, goodwill impairment, frequency, trigger events
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Impairment >25% total assets <12mo + goodwill impairment >50%
  - 🔴 HIGH: Impairment 10-25% total assets OR goodwill impairment 25-50%
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Impairment 5-10% total assets OR goodwill impairment <25%
  - ✅ LOW: No material impairments

- **Sources**: 10-K related party footnote, proxy DEF 14A
- **Assess**: Related party transactions as % revenue/assets, arm's length determination, board approval, disclosure quality
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Related party >10% revenue/assets + not arm's length + inadequate disclosure
  - 🔴 HIGH: Related party 5-10% revenue/assets + questions about arm's length
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Related party <5% OR arm's length + proper disclosure
  - ✅ LOW: No material related party transactions

**A.5.2: Whistleblower Complaints**
- **Risk Area**: Internal fraud allegations going public
- **Sources**: News, 8-K, SEC/DOJ filings, court records
- **Assess**: Public whistleblower allegations, investigation opened, retaliation claims, settlement
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Public whistleblower fraud allegations + SEC/DOJ investigation opened
  - 🔴 HIGH: Whistleblower allegations + internal investigation + retaliation claims
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Whistleblower allegations resolved OR no investigation
  - ✅ LOW: No whistleblower issues

**A.5.3: Disclosure Quality & Timeliness**
- **Risk Area**: Late or inadequate SEC filings
- **Sources**: SEC EDGAR, NT 10-K/10-Q filings, 8-K history
- **Assess**: Late filings (NT 10-K/Q), 8-K timeliness, MD&A quality, risk factor disclosure, legal proceedings disclosure
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Late 10-K/10-Q + inadequate disclosure + SEC comment letter
  - 🔴 HIGH: Late filings OR inadequate disclosure of material events
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Occasional late 8-K OR boilerplate risk factors
  - ✅ LOW: Timely, comprehensive disclosure

## CATEGORY B: FINANCIAL HEALTH & QUANTITATIVE ANALYSIS (~30 checks)

### B.1: LIQUIDITY & SOLVENCY

**B.1.1: Cash Position & Runway**
- **Risk Area**: Ability to meet short-term obligations
- **Sources**: 10-Q balance sheet, cash flow statement, MD&A
- **Assess**: Cash & equivalents, restricted cash, cash runway (months), burn rate, financing plan
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Negative cash OR cash runway <6 months + negative OCF + no financing
  - 🔴 HIGH: Cash runway 6-12 months + negative OCF
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Cash runway 12-18 months OR positive OCF but low cash
  - ✅ LOW: Cash runway >24 months + positive OCF

**B.1.2: Working Capital & Current Ratio**
- **Risk Area**: Short-term liquidity health
- **Sources**: 10-Q balance sheet
- **Assess**: Working capital (current assets - current liabilities), current ratio, quick ratio, trends
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Negative working capital + current ratio <0.8 + declining
  - 🔴 HIGH: Current ratio <1.0 OR quick ratio <0.5
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Current ratio 1.0-1.5 OR quick ratio 0.5-1.0
  - ✅ LOW: Current ratio >1.5 + quick ratio >1.0 + stable/improving

**B.1.3: Accounts Receivable Quality**
- **Risk Area**: Collectability of customer receivables
- **Sources**: 10-Q balance sheet, AR footnote
- **Assess**: Days sales outstanding (DSO), AR aging, allowance for doubtful accounts, write-offs, customer concentration
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: DSO >120 days + AR allowance <2% + write-offs increasing + customer concentration >50%
  - 🔴 HIGH: DSO 90-120 days OR AR allowance inadequate
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: DSO 60-90 days OR some collection concerns
  - ✅ LOW: DSO <60 days + adequate allowance + low write-offs

**B.1.4: Inventory Management**
- **Risk Area**: Inventory obsolescence and turnover
- **Sources**: 10-Q balance sheet, inventory footnote
- **Assess**: Inventory turnover, days inventory outstanding (DIO), obsolete inventory reserve, slow-moving inventory
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Inventory turnover <2x + obsolete reserve <5% + slow-moving >30%
  - 🔴 HIGH: Inventory turnover 2-4x OR obsolete reserve inadequate
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Inventory turnover 4-6x OR some slow-moving inventory
  - ✅ LOW: Inventory turnover >6x + adequate reserves OR not applicable (services)

---

### B.2: DEBT & LEVERAGE

**B.2.1: Debt Levels & Structure**
- **Risk Area**: Total debt burden and maturity profile
- **Sources**: 10-Q balance sheet, debt footnote
- **Assess**: Total debt, net debt (debt - cash), debt-to-equity, debt-to-EBITDA, maturity schedule, secured vs. unsecured
- **Severity**:
  - 🔴 CRITICAL: Net debt >5x EBITDA + debt matures <12mo + no refinancing plan
  - 🔴 HIGH: Net debt 3-5x EBITDA OR debt matures 12-24mo
  - ⚠️ MODERATE: Net debt 2-3x EBITDA OR some near-term maturities
  - ✅ LOW: Net debt <2x EBITDA + well-laddered maturities
