# AI COMPANY BUCKET STRATEGY FOR D&O UNDERWRITING (REVISED)

**Purpose**: Classification framework for AI-exposed companies to guide D&O underwriting decisions  
**Based on**: Howard Marks Oaktree Memo "Is It a Bubble?" (December 9, 2025)  
**Version**: 2.0 - Directional Guidance  
**Date**: December 2025

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The AI sector presents a paradox for D&O underwriters. While AI represents potentially transformative technology comparable to railroads or electricity ("inflection bubble"), current investment behavior shows concerning speculative characteristics including circular deals, excessive leverage, lottery-ticket valuations, and unpredictable demand trajectories.

**Key Insight**: This is fundamentally an **equity story, not a debt story**. AI will likely produce winner-takes-all outcomes. Companies financing AI investments with significant debt face asymmetric risk - if they lose the competitive race, debt holders face impairment while equity in the winner generates massive returns. D&O litigation risk follows this pattern.

**Strategic Recommendation**: **Selective participation** - target established cash-generative companies making prudent AI investments; avoid pre-revenue AI pure-plays and debt-financed infrastructure plays; approach with extreme caution any company exhibiting circular deal patterns or lottery-ticket financing.

## BUCKET CLASSIFICATION OVERVIEW

| Bucket | Definition | Portfolio Target | Pricing Approach | Risk Level |
|--------|------------|------------------|------------------|------------|
| **Bucket 1: TARGET** | Established, profitable companies integrating AI to enhance existing business | 70-80% of AI premium | Standard + modest load | **Low-Moderate** |
| **Bucket 2: CAUTION** | Significant AI exposure with mixed risk profile - requires case-by-case judgment | 20-25% of AI premium | Elevated to high premiums | **Moderate-High** |
| **Bucket 3: AVOID** | High-risk bubble characteristics - speculative financing, circular deals, unproven models | 0-5% of AI premium | Decline or extreme premiums | **Very High** |

## BUCKET 1: TARGET (Favorable for D&O Coverage)

### Quick Reference Table

| Criterion | Directional Guidance | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------|
| **Company Size** | Large, established companies with significant market presence | Scale provides financial flexibility and diversification |
| **Profitability** | Consistently profitable with multi-year track record | Demonstrates sustainable business model |
| **Cash Flow** | Strong operating cash flow; AI investments funded primarily from operations | Reduces dependence on external financing and associated risks |
| **Leverage** | Conservative debt levels; minimal AI-specific borrowing | Lower financial distress risk |
| **Credit Quality** | Investment-grade or near-investment-grade | Indicates financial strength and discipline |
| **AI Role** | AI enhances existing business; not core dependency | Diversification reduces "all-in" bet risk |
| **AI Investment** | Disciplined, phased capital deployment with clear ROI visibility | Reduces execution risk |
| **Revenue Traction** | AI products already generating revenue | Proves market demand and monetization ability |
| **Business Diversification** | Multiple revenue streams; AI is meaningful but not dominant | Reduces concentration risk |
| **Management** | Experienced team with proven track record | Reduces execution and credibility risk |
| **Governance** | Strong board with relevant expertise | Enhances oversight and risk management |
| **Disclosure** | Transparent, conservative financial guidance practices | Reduces securities litigation risk |

### Detailed Characteristics

**Financial Profile**:
- **Size**: Large-cap or mega-cap companies with substantial market capitalization
- **Profitability**: Multi-year history of consistent profitability
- **Cash Generation**: Strong, predictable operating cash flow
- **AI Financing**: AI investments represent modest portion of annual operating cash flow
- **Debt Structure**: Conservative overall leverage; AI-specific debt is minimal or absent
- **Credit Profile**: Investment-grade credit rating or equivalent financial strength

**AI Investment Profile**:
- **Strategic Role**: AI enhances existing products/services rather than replacing entire business model
- **Capital Discipline**: Phased, measured deployment with clear milestones
- **ROI Visibility**: Demonstrable returns or clear path to monetization
- **Spending Proportion**: AI represents meaningful but not dominant share of total capital expenditures
- **Revenue Evidence**: AI products/features already contributing to revenue

**Business Model**:
- **Diversification**: Multiple revenue streams across products, geographies, or customer segments
- **Customer Base**: Existing customers adopting AI features (not purely speculative new market)
- **Track Record**: Proven ability to successfully monetize technology investments historically
- **Competitive Position**: Not dependent on "winner-takes-all" outcome in AI race

**Governance & Management**:
- **Management Tenure**: Experienced leadership team with substantial tenure
- **Board Composition**: Directors with relevant technology, financial, and risk management expertise
- **Guidance Practices**: History of conservative, achievable financial guidance
- **Execution Track Record**: Consistent history of meeting or exceeding guidance
- **Disclosure Quality**: Transparent, comprehensive AI investment disclosures

**Examples** (Illustrative):
- Established hyperscalers with diversified businesses (Microsoft, Amazon, Google)
- Enterprise software leaders adding AI features (Salesforce, Adobe, ServiceNow)
- Financial institutions using AI for internal efficiency (major banks for operational use)

### Underwriting Approach

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Appetite** | Strong - actively seek these opportunities |
| **Pricing** | Standard rates with modest load for AI exposure |
| **Retention** | Standard retention levels appropriate for company size |
| **Coverage** | Broad form acceptable with standard terms |
| **Key Focus** | Verify AI disclosures are adequate; confirm no circular deal relationships; assess management credibility |

## BUCKET 2: CAUTION (Selective - Enhanced Due Diligence Required)

### Sub-Bucket Comparison Table

| Sub-Bucket | Profile | Risk Level | Pricing Load | Key Concerns |
|------------|---------|------------|--------------|--------------|
| **2A: Established AI Product Companies** | AI-native with revenue traction | Moderate | +30-50% | Competitive position, path to profitability, customer concentration |
| **2B: AI Infrastructure / Picks-and-Shovels** | Selling AI infrastructure (chips, data centers, cloud) | Moderate-High | +50-75% | Demand uncertainty, debt levels, asset obsolescence, customer concentration |
| **2C: Traditional Companies with Heavy AI Pivot** | Legacy business pivoting heavily to AI | High | +75-100% | Execution risk, legacy decline, management credibility, financial flexibility |

---

### Sub-Bucket 2A: Established AI Product Companies

**Profile**: Mid-cap companies with AI-native business models BUT demonstrable revenue traction

**Directional Characteristics**:
- **Size**: Mid-cap companies (smaller than Bucket 1 but substantial)
- **Business Model**: AI is core to business model, not just enhancement
- **Revenue**: Meaningful revenue generation from AI products/services
- **Profitability**: Profitable OR clear, credible path to profitability visible within reasonable timeframe
- **Leverage**: Some debt present but manageable relative to earnings/cash flow
- **Competitive Position**: Uncertain but defensible market position
- **Examples**: Palantir, C3.ai, UiPath (companies with AI products generating substantial revenue)

**Underwriting Approach**:

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Appetite** | Moderate - selective participation based on specific risk profile |
| **Pricing** | Elevated premiums (material load vs. non-AI tech companies) |
| **Retention** | Higher than Bucket 1 |
| **Coverage** | Consider sublimits on forward-looking statement coverage |
| **Key Focus** | Revenue quality and sustainability, customer concentration, competitive threats, realistic path to profitability, management execution capability |

**Move to Bucket 3 (Avoid) if**:
- Excessive leverage relative to cash generation
- Extreme customer concentration (business depends on small number of customers)
- Evidence of circular deal arrangements
- Significant vendor financing as percentage of revenue
- Thin or negative cash flow coverage ratios

---

### Sub-Bucket 2B: AI Infrastructure / Picks-and-Shovels

**Profile**: Companies selling infrastructure that enables AI (chips, data centers, cloud services)

**Directional Characteristics**:
- **Business Model**: Benefiting from AI boom but exposed to demand uncertainty
- **Capital Intensity**: May have significant debt to finance infrastructure buildout
- **Demand Visibility**: Revenue tied to unpredictable AI adoption trajectory
- **Asset Risk**: Infrastructure assets may face obsolescence from next-generation technology
- **Examples**: Data center REITs, specialized AI chip designers, cloud infrastructure providers

**Underwriting Approach**:

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Appetite** | Cautious - limited, selective participation |
| **Pricing** | Significantly elevated premiums |
| **Retention** | High retentions |
| **Coverage** | Restrictive terms - exclude coverage for debt financing representations |
| **Key Focus** | Debt maturity profile vs. asset useful life assumptions, demand visibility and contract backlog, customer concentration, competitive dynamics |

**Red Flags** (Move to Bucket 3 - Avoid):
- **Excessive leverage**: Debt levels high relative to cash generation capacity
- **Maturity mismatch**: Significant debt maturing well before expected asset payback period
- **Concentration risk**: Business highly dependent on small number of customers
- **Circular deals**: Evidence of circular revenue arrangements
- **Vendor financing**: Heavy reliance on vendor financing to customers
- **Thin coverage**: Cash flow insufficient to comfortably service debt obligations

---

### Sub-Bucket 2C: Traditional Companies with Heavy AI Pivot

**Profile**: Legacy companies attempting significant transformation to AI-centric business model

**Directional Characteristics**:
- **Legacy Business**: Established company with traditional business model
- **AI Investment**: AI investments represent substantial portion of capital expenditures
- **Financing**: May be issuing debt or equity to finance AI transformation
- **Competitive Pressure**: Competitive position in legacy business may be eroding
- **Execution Risk**: Transformation success highly uncertain
- **Examples**: Traditional software companies racing to add AI capabilities, legacy tech companies reinventing themselves

**Underwriting Approach**:

| Element | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| **Appetite** | Very cautious - minimal participation, highly selective |
| **Pricing** | Highly elevated premiums |
| **Retention** | Very high retentions |
| **Coverage** | Highly restrictive - exclude AI-related projections and forward-looking statements |
| **Key Focus** | Execution risk assessment, management credibility and track record, financial flexibility to sustain transformation, disclosure quality regarding transformation risks |

**Red Flags** (Move to Bucket 3 - Avoid):
- **Legacy collapse**: Core legacy business declining rapidly
- **Aggressive projections**: Management making highly optimistic AI revenue projections without supporting evidence
- **Debt-financed pivot**: Issuing significant debt specifically for AI investments
- **Guidance misses**: History of failing to meet financial guidance
- **Management turnover**: Recent changes in key leadership during critical transformation period

## BUCKET 3: AVOID (Decline Coverage)

### Sub-Bucket Overview Table

| Sub-Bucket | Profile | Primary Risk | Recommendation |
|------------|---------|--------------|----------------|
| **3A: Pre-Revenue AI Pure-Plays** | No meaningful revenue, extreme valuations | Valuation entirely speculative; high litigation probability | **DECLINE** |
| **3B: Circular Deal Participants** | Revenue from parties who are also investors | Revenue quality suspect; unsustainable arrangements | **DECLINE** |
| **3C: Debt-Financed AI Infrastructure** | Heavy debt for AI assets with uncertain useful life | Asset obsolescence risk; bankruptcy probability | **DECLINE** |
| **3D: Lottery-Ticket Financings** | Extreme valuations based on speculative outcomes | Down-round or failure highly probable | **DECLINE** |

---

### Sub-Bucket 3A: Pre-Revenue AI Pure-Plays

**Profile**: Companies with minimal or no revenue despite substantial funding and extreme valuations

**Characteristics**:
- **Revenue**: Minimal revenue (early-stage or pre-revenue)
- **Valuation**: Extreme valuation (multi-billion dollar valuations without revenue to support)
- **Funding**: Raised substantial capital despite lack of product or clear business model
- **Business Model**: Cannot or will not clearly explain path to monetization
- **Examples**: Thinking Machines Lab ($2B raised, $10B valuation, no product), Safe Superintelligence ($32B valuation, no released product)

**Rationale for Decline**:
- Valuation entirely speculative with no financial metrics to assess
- High probability of securities litigation when/if bubble deflates
- Management credibility unknown or unproven
- "Building airplane while in flight" - outcome purely conjectural
- No basis for rational D&O underwriting assessment

---

### Sub-Bucket 3B: Circular Deal Participants

**Profile**: Companies with material revenue derived from parties who are simultaneously investors or investment partners

**Characteristics**:
- **Revenue Pattern**: Significant revenue from companies that are also investors in the business
- **Investment Commitments**: Large investment commitments to industry counterparties
- **Vendor Financing**: Heavy reliance on vendor financing arrangements
- **Sustainability**: "Perpetual motion machine" dynamics where money circulates among participants
- **Examples**: Companies with OpenAI-style arrangements (receiving billions from tech companies while spending billions with same parties)

**Rationale for Decline**:
- Revenue quality highly suspect - may not represent true economic activity
- Potential for accounting manipulation or aggressive revenue recognition
- Arrangements likely unsustainable over medium term
- High securities litigation risk when circular arrangements unwind
- Resembles telecom bust circular deal patterns from 2000

---

### Sub-Bucket 3C: Debt-Financed AI Infrastructure with Thin Coverage

**Profile**: Companies that have issued substantial debt specifically for AI infrastructure investments with uncertain payback

**Characteristics**:
- **Debt Issuance**: Issued substantial debt specifically for AI-related investments
- **Maturity Mismatch**: Debt maturity significantly shorter than uncertain AI asset useful life
- **Coverage**: Cash flow coverage of debt obligations is thin or inadequate
- **Leverage**: Overall debt levels high relative to earnings capacity
- **Structure**: May use SPV structures to hide debt off primary balance sheet
- **Customer Risk**: Building infrastructure for other startups, both parties burning cash

**Rationale for Decline**:
- "Minsky moment" risk - credit expansion exhausting good projects, moving to marginal deals
- Asset obsolescence risk (next-generation technology may obsolete current infrastructure)
- Lender incentives misaligned (originate loans without long-term exposure)
- High bankruptcy probability if AI demand disappoints or technology shifts
- D&O litigation virtually certain if company fails to service debt obligations

**Specific Red Flags**:
- Long-dated bonds (e.g., 30-year) issued for technology with highly uncertain useful life
- Bond yields only modestly above Treasuries despite substantial technological uncertainty
- Startups borrowing to build infrastructure for other startups (both losing cash)
- No clear visibility into whether investments will generate adequate returns

---

### Sub-Bucket 3D: Lottery-Ticket Financings / Extreme Valuations

**Profile**: Companies receiving massive funding rounds at extreme valuations based purely on speculative potential

**Characteristics**:
- **Funding Size**: Seed or early-stage funding rounds of extraordinary size
- **Valuation**: Valuation implies need for extreme multiples to justify investment
- **Product Status**: Founders may refuse to explain product, business model, or path to market
- **Pitch**: Emphasis on "revolutionary" potential or "best AI talent" without concrete plans
- **Investor Rationale**: Lottery-ticket thinking - small probability of extreme outcome justifies investment

**Rationale for Decline**:
- No reasonable basis for valuation assessment
- High probability of down-round, failure, or extended period without liquidity
- Securities litigation inevitable if company fails to achieve speculative outcome
- Management accountability unclear when outcome is purely conjectural
- Represents purest form of bubble speculation

## CONCLUSION & STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATION

### Key Takeaways

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Participate Selectively** | AI will transform the economy; complete avoidance is wrong, but indiscriminate participation is dangerous |
| **Distinguish Equity from Debt Stories** | Favor companies financing AI with equity/cash flow over debt; debt-financed AI faces asymmetric litigation risk |
| **Avoid Lottery Tickets** | Decline pre-revenue pure-plays, circular deal participants, and extreme valuations |
| **Demand Transparency** | Only underwrite companies that clearly explain AI business model, risks, and path to profitability |
| **Monitor Constantly** | Bubble dynamics can shift rapidly; early warning indicators are critical |
| **Maintain Discipline** | Resist competitive pressure to relax standards as market heats up |

---

### The Bottom Line

This bucket strategy provides a framework for **intelligent participation in AI without catastrophic exposure to bubble collapse**. By concentrating in Bucket 1 (established, cash-generative companies) and being highly selective in Bucket 2 (proven AI companies with manageable leverage), underwriters can capture AI-driven premium growth while avoiding the securities litigation tsunami that will inevitably follow if/when speculative excess unwinds.

**As Howard Marks concludes**: "Intelligent investment in data centers, and thus in AI – like everything else – requires sober, insightful judgment and skillful implementation."

**The same applies to D&O underwriting of AI companies.**

## APPENDIX: HOWARD MARKS KEY QUOTES

> "Most technological advances develop into winner-takes-all or winner-takes-most competitions. The 'right' way to play this dynamic is through equity, not debt."

> "Of course, if you can't identify the pool of companies from which the winner will emerge, the difference between debt and equity is irrelevant – you're a zero either way."

> "There is no doubt that investors are applying exuberance with regard to AI. The question is whether it's irrational. Given the vast potential of AI but also the large number of enormous unknowns, I think virtually no one can say for sure."

> "No one should go all-in without acknowledging that they face the risk of ruin if things go badly. But by the same token, no one should stay all-out and risk missing out on one of the great technological steps forward."

> "A moderate position, applied with selectivity and prudence, seems like the best approach."
