# PRIMORIS UNDERWRITING MEETING - QUESTION #7: EPS FORECASTING PROCESS

## QUESTION 7: EPS FORECASTING PROCESS DIFFICULTY & GUIDANCE QUALITY

### **The Question**:

> "Can you walk us through your EPS forecasting process? How difficult is it for you to forecast EPS with confidence, and what are the biggest sources of variability or uncertainty in your forecasts? Specifically, how far in advance can you predict quarterly EPS within a 5-10% range, and what factors most commonly cause you to revise guidance?"

### **Why This Matters**:

**Securities Litigation Risk**: The #1 trigger for securities class actions is **guidance miss** (missed EPS or revenue guidance). Understanding management's forecasting process helps assess the likelihood of future guidance misses.

**Key D&O Risk Factors**:
- **Forecasting difficulty**: If management struggles to forecast EPS, guidance is unreliable → higher miss risk
- **Variability sources**: High variability (weather, project timing, material costs) = higher forecast error = higher litigation risk
- **Guidance revision frequency**: Frequent revisions signal poor forecasting process or external volatility
- **Visibility horizon**: Short visibility (<1 quarter) = reactive guidance = higher miss risk

**Primoris Context**:
- **2025 Performance**: Beat-and-raise pattern (Q3 EPS $1.88 vs. consensus $1.35 = 39% beat)
- **Positive Signal**: Conservative guidance with consistent beats is GOOD for D&O
- **Question Purpose**: Confirm this is intentional (conservative philosophy) vs. poor forecasting ability

### **What to Listen For**:

#### **RED FLAGS** (Forecasting difficulty concern):

**Poor Visibility**:
- ❌ "It's very difficult to forecast EPS beyond the current quarter"
- ❌ "We can only predict EPS within 5-10% range for the next 30-60 days"
- ❌ "Project timing is highly unpredictable"
- ❌ "Weather significantly impacts our ability to forecast"

**High Variability**:
- ❌ "Material costs are volatile and hard to predict"
- ❌ "Customer decisions on project timing are unpredictable"
- ❌ "Project closeout timing creates significant EPS variability quarter-to-quarter"
- ❌ "We've had to revise guidance 3-4 times in the past 2 years"

**Reactive Process**:
- ❌ "We update our forecast monthly based on project performance"
- ❌ "It's hard to predict which projects will close out in a given quarter"
- ❌ "We rely heavily on real-time project data to forecast"
- ❌ Vague answers or inability to articulate a clear forecasting process

**Guidance Philosophy Concern**:
- ❌ "We set aggressive targets to motivate the organization"
- ❌ "We prefer to set stretch goals rather than conservative guidance"
- ❌ "We've missed guidance 1-2 times in the past 3 years"

#### **GREEN FLAGS** (Strong forecasting process):

**Good Visibility**:
- ✅ "We can forecast EPS within 5-10% range for the next 2-3 quarters with high confidence"
- ✅ "Our backlog provides 12-18 months of revenue visibility"
- ✅ "MSA work (49% of revenue) is highly predictable"
- ✅ "Project timing is generally within our control"

**Low Variability**:
- ✅ "Material costs are largely locked in through supplier contracts"
- ✅ "Weather impact is minimal due to geographic diversification"
- ✅ "Project closeout timing is predictable based on milestones"
- ✅ "We've only revised guidance once in the past 2 years (upward)"

**Proactive Process**:
- ✅ "We have a formal quarterly forecasting process with input from all segments"
- ✅ "We use rolling 12-month forecasts updated quarterly"
- ✅ "We stress-test our forecasts for downside scenarios"
- ✅ "We have a formal policy for setting guidance (e.g., 80% confidence level)"

**Conservative Philosophy**:
- ✅ "We prefer to set conservative guidance and beat it consistently"
- ✅ "We've beaten guidance for the past 8-10 quarters"
- ✅ "We set guidance at a level we're 80-90% confident we can achieve"
- ✅ "We'd rather surprise positively than disappoint"

### **Follow-Up Questions**:

#### **If Forecasting Difficulty is Mentioned**:

1. **"What percentage of your revenue is MSA vs. project-based?"**
   - **Why**: MSA = predictable, project-based = variable
   - **Good Answer**: "49% MSA, 51% project-based" (from presentation)
   - **Concern**: "Mostly project-based" (higher variability)

2. **"How much of your backlog converts to revenue in the next 12 months?"**
   - **Why**: Measures visibility horizon
   - **Good Answer**: "70-80% of backlog converts within 12 months"
   - **Concern**: "<50% converts within 12 months" (long-duration projects = higher timing risk)

3. **"What's your typical forecast error (actual vs. guidance) over the past 8 quarters?"**
   - **Why**: Quantifies forecasting accuracy
   - **Good Answer**: "Within 5% for 7 out of 8 quarters"
   - **Concern**: "Within 10-15% variability" or "We've missed 2-3 times"

#### **If Variability Sources are Mentioned**:

4. **"How do you manage material cost volatility in your forecasts?"**
   - **Why**: Material costs are a common source of margin compression
   - **Good Answer**: "We lock in material costs through supplier contracts" or "We use escalation clauses in fixed-price contracts"
   - **Concern**: "Material costs are passed through to customers" (suggests reimbursable work, which is good) OR "We absorb material cost increases" (margin risk)

5. **"How does weather impact your ability to forecast, and how do you mitigate that?"**
   - **Why**: Weather is a common excuse for guidance misses
   - **Good Answer**: "Geographic diversification limits weather impact" or "Weather impact is <5% of quarterly variability"
   - **Concern**: "Weather significantly impacts Q1 and Q4 results" (suggests poor forecasting or high exposure)

6. **"How predictable is project closeout timing?"**
   - **Why**: Project closeouts boost margins; unpredictable timing = EPS variability
   - **Good Answer**: "Project closeouts are milestone-based and predictable within 30-60 days"
   - **Concern**: "Closeout timing is highly variable and depends on customer acceptance"

#### **If Guidance Philosophy is Unclear**:

7. **"What's your philosophy on setting guidance - do you prefer conservative starts with upside, or hitting targets consistently?"**
   - **Why**: This is the KEY question for assessing securities litigation risk
   - **Good Answer**: "We prefer conservative guidance that we can beat consistently" (beat-and-raise pattern)
   - **Concern**: "We set stretch goals to motivate the organization" (aggressive guidance = higher miss risk)

8. **"How many times have you revised guidance in the past 2 years, and were those revisions upward or downward?"**
   - **Why**: Frequent revisions = poor forecasting; downward revisions = red flag
   - **Good Answer**: "1-2 revisions, all upward" (conservative guidance)
   - **Concern**: "3-4 revisions, mix of up and down" (poor forecasting or volatility)

9. **"Have you missed guidance in the past 3 years?"**
   - **Why**: History of misses is the #1 predictor of future misses
   - **Good Answer**: "No, we've beaten or met guidance for the past 10+ quarters"
   - **Concern**: "Yes, we missed once in 2023 due to [reason]" (even one miss is a red flag)
