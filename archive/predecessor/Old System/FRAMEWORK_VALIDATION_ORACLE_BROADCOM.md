## PART 4: FRAMEWORK ADJUSTMENTS NEEDED

### What Needs to Change?

Based on Oracle and Broadcom case studies, here are recommended framework adjustments:

---

### **Adjustment 1: Add "Margin Pressure" as Explicit Factor**

**Issue**: Broadcom declined on margin pressure, but our framework doesn't explicitly assess gross margin trends

**Current Framework**: Factor 7 (Optionality) implicitly considers profitability

**Proposed Change**: Add explicit margin assessment to Factor 7

| Optionality Assessment | Current | Proposed Addition |
|----------------------|---------|------------------|
| **Profitability** | "Does company have profitable non-AI business?" | **Add**: "Are AI gross margins stable or improving?" |
| **Scoring** | 5-star = profitable core | **Add**: 5-star = stable/improving AI margins; 3-star = margin pressure; 1-star = negative margins |

**Example**:
- **Microsoft**: Stable AI margins → ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
- **Broadcom**: Margin pressure warning → ⭐⭐⭐ (downgrade from ⭐⭐⭐⭐)
- **C3.ai**: Negative margins → ⭐⭐

---

### **Adjustment 2: Strengthen "Physical Infrastructure" Risk Assessment**

**Issue**: Oracle's data center delays highlight "world of atoms" constraints

**Current Framework**: Optionality Score considers execution risk generally

**Proposed Change**: Add explicit "Physical Infrastructure Risk" sub-factor

| Company Type | Physical Infrastructure Risk | Assessment |
|--------------|----------------------------|------------|
| **Hyperscalers building data centers** (Oracle, Meta) | HIGH | Long lead times; labor/material shortages; permitting delays |
| **Chip manufacturers** (Nvidia, Broadcom) | MODERATE | Supply chain constraints; fab capacity limits |
| **Software/SaaS** (Salesforce, Adobe) | LOW | Digital delivery; no physical constraints |

**Scoring Impact**:
- **Oracle**: Physical infrastructure risk = HIGH → Optionality Score reduced to ⭐⭐ (from ⭐⭐⭐)
- **Microsoft/Amazon/Google**: Already have data centers → MODERATE risk
- **Salesforce/Adobe**: Software only → LOW risk

---

### **Adjustment 3: Add "Single Customer Concentration" Red Flag**

**Issue**: Oracle's dependence on OpenAI ($300B deal) is existential risk

**Current Framework**: Business Model (Category C) assesses customer concentration generally

**Proposed Change**: Add to Absolute No-Go list

**New Absolute No-Go Category 11**:
- **Single Customer >40% of Revenue or Capex**
- Example: Oracle ($300B OpenAI deal represents majority of AI capex)
- Rationale: Customer delays/cancellations = existential risk

---

### **Adjustment 4: Strengthen "Credit Market" Monitoring**

**Issue**: Oracle's bond yields rising to "junk" levels is early warning signal

**Current Framework**: Factor 3 (Debt) assesses debt levels and coverage

**Proposed Change**: Add explicit credit market monitoring

| Credit Indicator | Threshold | Action |
|-----------------|-----------|--------|
| **Bond yields** | Rising to "junk" levels (>7-8%) | Immediate review; consider non-renewal |
| **Credit rating downgrade** | Downgrade below BBB- | Decline new business; review existing |
| **Credit-risk gauge** | Highest in 10+ years | Enhanced monitoring; elevated pricing |
| **Credit spread widening** | >200bps vs. peers | Investigate; potential decline |

**Example**:
- **Oracle**: Bond yields at junk levels + credit-risk gauge highest since 2009 → **DECLINE** (would have triggered immediate review)

---

### **Adjustment 5: Add "Market Sentiment Shift" Trigger**

**Issue**: Both Oracle and Broadcom declined as market shifted from growth to profitability focus

**Current Framework**: Bull/Bear Case Triggers monitor specific metrics

**Proposed Change**: Add "Market Sentiment Shift" as Bear Case Trigger

**New Bear Case Trigger**:
- **"Profitability Scrutiny" Trigger**: 3+ AI companies decline >10% on profitability concerns (not revenue misses) within 30 days
- **Action**: Reduce Bucket 2B and Bucket 3 allocations; increase focus on profitable companies (Bucket 1A, 2A)
- **Example**: Oracle (-15% on debt/margins) + Broadcom (-11% on margins) = 2 of 3 → Monitor closely
