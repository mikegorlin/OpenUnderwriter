# AI Company D&O Underwriting Framework
## **Simplified 4-Lens Approach**

**Date**: December 2025  
**Purpose**: Practical framework for underwriters - 30-60 minute assessment per company

---

## THE CORE QUESTION

> **"Does this company survive if AI fails?"**

**If YES** → Consider underwriting  
**If NO** → Decline

---

## FRAMEWORK OVERVIEW

**4 Lenses → Simple Assessment → 3 Buckets**

| Lens | Weight | Question | Options |
|------|--------|----------|---------|
| **1. Optionality** | **40%** | Do they survive if AI fails? | STRONG / MODERATE / WEAK |
| **2. Financial** | **30%** | Can they afford AI? | YES / TIGHT / NO |
| **3. Execution** | **20%** | Can they deliver? | PROVEN / UNCERTAIN / UNLIKELY |
| **4. Red Flags** | **10%** | Any deal-breakers? | CLEAN / CONCERNS / DISQUALIFYING |

**Buckets**: 🟢 GREEN (target) / 🟡 YELLOW (selective) / 🔴 RED (decline)

---

## LENS 1: OPTIONALITY (40% Weight)

### **The Question**: "Do they survive if AI fails?"

### **3 Options**:

#### **🟢 STRONG OPTIONALITY**
- Profitable non-AI core business (AI <30% of revenue)
- Multiple revenue streams ($1B+ each)
- Can pivot away from AI if needed
- **Examples**: Microsoft, Salesforce, Apple, Adobe
- **D&O Risk**: LOW - Survives in both scenarios

#### **🟡 MODERATE OPTIONALITY**
- Some non-AI revenue but AI-dependent (AI 30-60% of revenue)
- Limited revenue diversification
- Difficult but possible to pivot
- **Examples**: Amazon, Google, Nvidia
- **D&O Risk**: MODERATE - Elevated pricing needed

#### **🔴 WEAK OPTIONALITY**
- No profitable non-AI core (AI >60% of revenue)
- Pure-play or near-pure-play AI
- Cannot pivot; existential AI bet
- **Examples**: C3.ai, most AI startups, Oracle (due to debt)
- **D&O Risk**: EXTREME - Decline

---

### **How to Assess**:

**Step 1**: What % of revenue is AI?
- <30% → STRONG
- 30-60% → MODERATE
- >60% → WEAK

**Step 2**: Is non-AI business profitable?
- Highly profitable → STRONG
- Break-even → MODERATE
- Unprofitable → WEAK

**Step 3**: Can they pivot away from AI?
- Yes, easily → STRONG
- Difficult but possible → MODERATE
- No, existential bet → WEAK

**Final Assessment**: Take the WORST of the three answers

---

### **Why Optionality Matters Most**:

**Historical Evidence**:
- **Dot-com (2000)**: Pure-play internet companies (Pets.com) → Bankruptcy; Diversified companies (Microsoft, Amazon) → Survived
- **Telecom (2002)**: Pure-play fiber companies (WorldCom) → Bankruptcy; Diversified telecoms → Survived
- **Lesson**: Companies with optionality survive bubbles; pure-plays don't

**Howard Marks' Framework**:
- AI could be **inflection** (transformative) OR **mean-reversion** (bubble)
- We don't know which
- **Solution**: Underwrite companies that succeed in EITHER scenario
- **Optionality = Lowest D&O risk**

---

## LENS 2: FINANCIAL HEALTH (30% Weight)

### **The Question**: "Can they afford AI from operating cash flow?"

### **3 Options**:

#### **🟢 YES - Can Afford**
- AI capex <40% of operating cash flow
- No AI-specific debt OR manageable debt (<2x EBITDA)
- Positive free cash flow after AI investments
- **Examples**: Microsoft, Apple, Salesforce
- **D&O Risk**: LOW - Sustainable AI investment

#### **🟡 TIGHT - Strained but Manageable**
- AI capex 40-80% of operating cash flow
- Some AI debt but manageable (2-4x EBITDA)
- Slightly positive or near-zero free cash flow
- **Examples**: Amazon, Meta, Google
- **D&O Risk**: MODERATE - Elevated pricing needed

#### **🔴 NO - Cannot Afford**
- AI capex >80% of operating cash flow
- Heavy AI debt (>4x EBITDA) OR bonds at junk levels
- Negative free cash flow; betting on AI revenue
- **Examples**: Oracle ($100B debt, 320% capex/OCF)
- **D&O Risk**: EXTREME - Decline

---

### **How to Assess**:

**Step 1**: Calculate AI Capex / Operating Cash Flow ratio
- <40% → YES
- 40-80% → TIGHT
- >80% → NO

**Step 2**: Check AI-specific debt
- None or minimal → YES
- Moderate (2-4x EBITDA) → TIGHT
- Heavy (>4x EBITDA) or junk bonds → NO

**Step 3**: Is free cash flow positive after AI?
- Strongly positive → YES
- Slightly positive or near-zero → TIGHT
- Negative → NO

**Final Assessment**: Take the WORST of the three answers

---

### **Why Financial Health Matters**:

**Oracle Case Study (Dec 2025)**:
- $100B AI debt + $48B annual AI capex
- $15B operating cash flow
- **Ratio**: 320% capex/OCF = Cannot afford
- Stock declined -15%; bonds at junk levels
- **Lesson**: Companies that can't afford AI from OCF face debt crisis → D&O exposure

**Telecom Bubble Parallel (2000-2002)**:
- Telecom companies borrowed $1 trillion for fiber networks
- Couldn't service debt from operations
- WorldCom → Bankruptcy → $6B D&O settlement
- **Lesson**: Debt-financed infrastructure without revenue = disaster

## LENS 3: EXECUTION CAPABILITY (20% Weight)

### **The Question**: "Can they actually deliver on AI strategy?"

### **3 Options**:

#### **🟢 PROVEN**
- Strong track record of delivering (Azure, Office 365, AWS)
- Minimal physical constraints (software/SaaS or existing infrastructure)
- Strong governance (independent board, good oversight)
- **Examples**: Microsoft, Apple, Salesforce, Amazon
- **D&O Risk**: LOW - Can execute

#### **🟡 UNCERTAIN**
- Mixed track record OR significant physical constraints
- Building new infrastructure (data centers, chips)
- Adequate governance but some concerns
- **Examples**: Oracle (data center delays), Meta (massive buildout), Broadcom (supply chain)
- **D&O Risk**: MODERATE - Execution risk

#### **🔴 UNLIKELY**
- History of failures/delays (Tesla FSD, Oracle cloud transition)
- Severe physical constraints (greenfield infrastructure)
- Weak governance (rubber stamp board, CEO dominance)
- **Examples**: Tesla (FSD delays), companies with no track record
- **D&O Risk**: HIGH - Decline

---

### **How to Assess**:

**Step 1**: Track record?
- Excellent (proven at scale) → PROVEN
- Mixed or limited → UNCERTAIN
- Poor (history of delays/failures) → UNLIKELY

**Step 2**: Physical constraints?
- None (software) or low (existing infrastructure) → PROVEN
- Moderate (new data centers, chips) → UNCERTAIN
- Severe (greenfield, long lead times) → UNLIKELY

**Step 3**: Governance quality?
- Strong (independent board) → PROVEN
- Adequate → UNCERTAIN
- Weak (rubber stamp, CEO dominance) → UNLIKELY

**Final Assessment**: Take the WORST of the three answers

---

### **Why Execution Matters**:

**Oracle Example**:
- Announced data center delays (2027 → 2028)
- Reason: "Labor and material shortages"
- Stock declined -15%
- **Lesson**: Physical infrastructure = execution risk

**"World of Atoms" vs. "World of Bits"**:
- **Software/SaaS** (Salesforce, Adobe): Digital delivery; no physical constraints → LOW RISK
- **Data centers** (Oracle, Meta): Physical construction; labor/material/permit constraints → HIGH RISK
- **Chips** (Nvidia, Broadcom): Supply chain constraints; fab capacity limits → MODERATE RISK

## LENS 4: RED FLAGS (10% Weight, but DISQUALIFYING)

### **The Question**: "Are there any automatic deal-breakers?"

### **3 Options**:

#### **🟢 CLEAN**
- No circular deals
- No single customer concentration (>40%)
- No AI washing
- No fraud history
- No product liability issues
- **Examples**: Microsoft, Salesforce, Apple, Adobe
- **Action**: Proceed to bucket determination

#### **🟡 CONCERNS**
- Minor circular deals (<20%)
- Some customer concentration (20-40%)
- Marketing hype but real products
- No major issues
- **Examples**: Microsoft/OpenAI (10-15% circular), Nvidia (elevated valuation)
- **Action**: Proceed with elevated pricing

#### **🔴 DISQUALIFYING**
- Circular deals >20% of revenue
- Single customer >40% of revenue/capex
- AI washing / fraudulent claims
- Fraud history / SEC enforcement
- Product liability (death, serious harm to minors)
- **Examples**: Oracle/OpenAI (40% concentration), Character.AI (2 suicides), C3.ai (pure-play)
- **Action**: **AUTOMATIC DECLINE**

---

### **7 Automatic DECLINE Triggers** (Any ONE = Decline):

1. ❌ **Circular deals >20%** (Oracle/OpenAI: 40%)
2. ❌ **Single customer >40%** (Oracle/OpenAI, Palantir/US Govt)
3. ❌ **AI washing** (fraudulent AI claims; SEC enforcement)
4. ❌ **Extreme valuation** (>100x revenue for pre-revenue companies)
5. ❌ **Fraud history** (restatements, SEC enforcement, criminal)
6. ❌ **Mass layoffs** (>30% AI-driven workforce reduction)
7. ❌ **Product liability** (AI causing death, serious harm, exploitation of minors)

**If ANY red flag present → AUTOMATIC DECLINE, regardless of Lenses 1-3**

---

### **Red Flag Examples**:

**Circular Deals**:
- **Oracle/OpenAI**: Oracle building $300B data centers primarily for OpenAI (40% of AI capex)
- **Microsoft/OpenAI**: Microsoft invests $13B → OpenAI commits $300B cloud spend (10-15% circular)
- **Threshold**: >20% = Enron-level revenue quality concerns

**Product Liability**:
- **Character.AI**: 2 teen suicides, 1 self-harm case, 1 sexual abuse case
  - Federal judge allowed wrongful death lawsuit to proceed
  - Rejected Section 230 defense (AI content ≠ third-party content)
  - **Automatic decline**: AI products causing death/harm to minors

**AI Washing**:
- **SEC enforcement**: Delphia, Global Predictions charged with false AI claims
- **Theranos parallel**: Overstated technology → Criminal charges
- **Threshold**: Fraudulent claims = automatic decline

### **Simplified Rules**:

**🟢 GREEN Bucket** (Target These):
- **STRONG optionality** (survive if AI fails)
- **YES or TIGHT financial** (can afford AI)
- **PROVEN or UNCERTAIN execution** (can likely deliver)
- **Pricing**: +10-35% load

**🟡 YELLOW Bucket** (Selective):
- **MODERATE optionality** (some AI dependency)
- **TIGHT financial** (strained but manageable)
- **PROVEN execution** (track record compensates for other concerns)
- **Pricing**: +30-80% load

**🔴 RED Bucket** (Decline):
- **WEAK optionality** (existential AI bet)
- **NO financial** (cannot afford AI from OCF)
- **UNLIKELY execution** (history of failures)
- **ANY red flag** (circular deals, product liability, etc.)
- **Action**: **DECLINE**

## QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE

### **30-Second Assessment**:

**Question 1**: Do they survive if AI fails?
- YES (profitable non-AI core) → Continue
- NO (pure-play AI) → **DECLINE**

**Question 2**: Can they afford AI from operating cash flow?
- YES (capex <40% OCF) → Continue
- NO (capex >80% OCF) → **DECLINE**

**Question 3**: Any red flags?
- Circular deals >20%? → **DECLINE**
- Single customer >40%? → **DECLINE**
- Product liability (death/harm)? → **DECLINE**
- AI washing / fraud? → **DECLINE**
- None → Continue to bucket

**Question 4**: What bucket?
- STRONG optionality + YES financial + PROVEN execution → 🟢 **GREEN**
- MODERATE optionality OR TIGHT financial → 🟡 **YELLOW**
- WEAK optionality OR NO financial → 🔴 **RED**
