## DETAILED FINDINGS (7 Key Areas)

### 1. ENERGY BACKLOG DECLINE (-30%)

**Management Meeting Statement**:
> "Energy backlog decline from $6.3B (2024) to $4.5B (Q3 2025) was due to timing: revenue pull-forward from strong execution + project delays (permits, customer timing). NOT market softness or lost business. Q4 recovery expected with $600M booked + $600M within 30 days."

**10-K Filing (December 31, 2024)**:
- Energy backlog: **$6,344M** ✅ (matches management statement)
- Utilities backlog: **$5,521M** ✅ (matches management statement)
- Total backlog: **$11,865M**

**Q3 2025 Earnings Call (November 4, 2025)**:
- Energy backlog: **$4,470M** ✅ (matches management statement)
- Utilities backlog: **$6,593M** ✅ (matches management statement)
- Management explanation: "Revenue pull-forward from strong execution; some project delays; Q4 book-to-bill 1.2-1.3x expected" ✅ (consistent with meeting)

**10-K Risk Factor Disclosure (Page 944-954)**:
> "Backlog may not be realized or may not result in revenue or profit... contract revenues reflected in our backlog may be realized in different periods from those previously anticipated, which can result from project accelerations or delays due to various reasons, including changes in customer spending priorities, project cancellations, regulatory interruptions, scheduling changes, commercial issues (e.g., permitting), engineering revisions, job site conditions and adverse weather."

**FINDING**: ✅ **NO CONTRADICTION**
- Management's explanation (timing, permits, customer delays) is **consistent with 10-K risk factor disclosures**
- Numbers match exactly between filings and management statements
- Q4 recovery claim is **forward-looking** (not verifiable from 10-K, but consistent with Q3 earnings call guidance)

### 6. MARGIN TRENDS (ENERGY VS. UTILITIES)

**Management Meeting Statement**:
> "Energy margins slightly lower than Utilities due to mix (more fixed-price work in Energy). Energy margins stable, not declining. 2026 outlook: margins expanding across both segments."

**10-K Filing (December 31, 2024)**:
- **Utilities segment margin**: 11.9% (2024)
- **Energy segment margin**: 10.3% (2024)
- **Differential**: 1.6 percentage points (Utilities higher)

**Q3 2025 Earnings Call (November 4, 2025)**:
- Management confirmed "strong margins across both segments"
- No specific segment margin breakdown disclosed in Q3 call
- Management emphasized "margin expansion expected in 2026"

**10-K Risk Factor Disclosure (Page 958)**:
> "We currently generate, and expect to continue to generate, a substantial portion of our revenue from fixed price and unit price contracts. The actual cost of labor and materials may vary from the costs we originally estimated, and we may not be successful in recouping additional costs from our customers. These variations may cause gross profit for a project to differ from those we originally estimated."

**FINDING**: ✅ **NO CONTRADICTION**
- Management's explanation (more fixed-price in Energy → lower margins) is **consistent with 10-K risk factors**
- 1.6 percentage point differential (11.9% vs. 10.3%) is **not material** (both segments profitable)
- Management's "stable" claim is **consistent with 2024 performance** (no significant deterioration)
- **Recommendation**: Accept management's representation; differential is explained and not material

**Management Meeting Statement**:

**10-K Filing (December 31, 2024) - Forward-Looking Statements Section**:

Our actual results may differ materially from those anticipated in these forward-looking statements."

**Q3 2025 Earnings Call (November 4, 2025)**:
- Q3 EPS: **$1.88** vs. consensus **$1.35** = **39% beat** ✅
- Management raised full-year guidance multiple times in 2025
- Pattern of "beat-and-raise" evident from earnings history

**Historical Performance (from public filings)**:
- Q1 2025: Beat consensus
- Q2 2025: Beat consensus
- Q3 2025: Beat consensus (39%)
- **3/3 quarters beat in 2025** = consistent pattern

**FINDING**: ✅ **NO CONTRADICTION**
- Management's "conservative guidance" claim is **validated by beat-and-raise pattern**
- 3/3 quarters beat consensus in 2025 = **consistent execution**
- Management's "1-2 quarter visibility" claim is **reasonable** given:
  - Average contract duration: 6-12 months
  - MSAs provide 49% of backlog (recurring revenue)
  - Short forecasting horizon reduces uncertainty
- **Recommendation**: Accept management's representation; track record validates claims
