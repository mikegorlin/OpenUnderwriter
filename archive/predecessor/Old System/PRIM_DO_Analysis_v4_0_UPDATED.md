### DEEP-DIVE #2: 2018 SEC ENFORCEMENT MATTER

#### Matter Summary

**Date**: September 2018 (conduct from 2014)  
**Type**: Administrative proceeding (not securities fraud)  
**Issue**: Internal Control over Financial Reporting (ICFR) deficiencies  
**Penalty**: $200,000  
**Outcome**: Cease-and-desist order (no admission/denial of findings)

#### Specific Findings

The SEC found that Primoris:
1. Lacked written policies for contingency cost estimation at project start
2. Lacked procedures for adjusting contingency estimates as projects progressed
3. Internal emails referenced "cushion," "cookie jars," and "sandbagging" for contingencies
4. Failed to properly assess whether control deficiencies constituted material weaknesses
5. Incorrectly concluded ICFR was effective as of December 31, 2014

#### Risk Assessment

**Age Mitigation**: The relevant conduct occurred in 2014 (11+ years ago) and the settlement was in 2018 (7+ years ago). Under most underwriting frameworks, SEC matters >5 years old receive substantially reduced weight.

**Severity Mitigation**: The $200,000 penalty is at the lower end of SEC enforcement actions, indicating the Commission viewed this as a controls/process failure rather than intentional misconduct.

**No Restatement**: The financials were not restated, meaning the underlying accounting conclusions were not materially incorrect - only the control environment was deficient.

**Remediation**: As a condition of settlement, Primoris was required to cease and desist from violations. Post-settlement, the company would have implemented required controls.

#### Deep-Dive Conclusion

Historical SEC matter is substantially mitigated by age (>7 years), penalty size ($200K), absence of restatement, and administrative (non-fraud) nature. This should not be a material factor in current underwriting decision.

**Underwriting Meeting Priority**: **LOW** - Confirm specific controls implemented post-2018.
