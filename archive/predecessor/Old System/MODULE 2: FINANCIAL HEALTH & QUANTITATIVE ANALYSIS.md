# MODULE 2: FINANCIAL HEALTH & QUANTITATIVE ANALYSIS

**Purpose**: Deep financial analysis beyond basic red flags to assess accounting quality, cash flow sustainability, balance sheet strength, and stock performance attribution.

**Total Checks**: ~95 enumerated checks across 8 categories

**When to Use**: 
- Run after Module 1 Quick Screen passes (no auto-decline triggers)
- Stop early if critical financial distress indicators found
- Essential for all underwriting decisions

**Auto-Decline Triggers in This Module**:
- Negative operating cash flow + <12 months cash runway + no revenue
- Debt covenant breach + going concern warning
- Restatement announced + SEC investigation
- Accounting fraud allegations + auditor resignation

## CATEGORY 1: REVENUE QUALITY & SUSTAINABILITY (15 Checks)

### 1.1 Revenue Recognition Policies

**1.1.1 Revenue Recognition Method**
- **Check**: Review revenue recognition policy in 10-K/10-Q notes
- **Pass** 🟢: Standard industry practice (point-in-time for products, over-time for services)
- **Caution** 🟡: Complex multi-element arrangements, significant estimates
- **Fail** 🔴: Non-standard policies, frequent changes, aggressive recognition
- **N/A**: Pre-revenue company
- **Sources**: 10-K Note 2 (Significant Accounting Policies), 10-Q revenue note
- **Verification**: Compare to peer companies in same industry

**1.1.2 Percentage of Completion Accounting**
- **Check**: For companies using POC method (construction, aerospace, defense)
- **Pass** 🟢: Conservative estimates, history of accurate completion forecasts
- **Caution** 🟡: Frequent revisions to completion estimates
- **Fail** 🔴: Pattern of over-estimating completion, contract losses
- **N/A**: Not using POC method
- **Sources**: 10-K revenue recognition note, contract disclosures
- **Red Flag**: Change from completed contract to POC method = aggressive

**1.1.3 Bill-and-Hold Arrangements**
- **Check**: Revenue recognized before delivery to customer
- **Pass** 🟢: No bill-and-hold arrangements
- **Caution** 🟡: Limited bill-and-hold with proper documentation
- **Fail** 🔴: Significant bill-and-hold revenue, especially near quarter-end
- **Sources**: Revenue recognition note, MD&A
- **Red Flag**: Classic earnings manipulation technique

**1.1.4 Channel Stuffing Indicators**
- **Check**: Abnormal shipments to distributors/resellers near quarter-end
- **Pass** 🟢: Stable quarterly shipment patterns
- **Caution** 🟡: Q4 spike in shipments (could be seasonal)
- **Fail** 🔴: Consistent pattern of Q-end spikes + high returns in following quarter
- **Sources**: Revenue by quarter, accounts receivable aging, returns reserve
- **Red Flag**: Days sales outstanding (DSO) increasing + inventory at distributors rising

**1.1.5 Side Letter Agreements**
- **Check**: Undisclosed agreements that modify revenue recognition
- **Pass** 🟢: No history of side letter issues
- **Caution** 🟡: Industry where side letters common (software, pharma)
- **Fail** 🔴: Prior restatement due to side letters, allegations of hidden agreements
- **Sources**: Restatement disclosures, litigation allegations, news reports
- **Red Flag**: Often discovered during SEC investigations

### 1.2 Revenue Concentration Risk

**1.2.1 Customer Concentration**
- **Check**: Percentage of revenue from top customer
- **Pass** 🟢: <10% from any single customer
- **Caution** 🟡: 10-25% from single customer
- **Fail** 🔴: >25% from single customer (especially >50%)
- **N/A**: Pre-revenue company
- **Sources**: 10-K Note on Concentrations, MD&A
- **Benchmark**: >25% concentration increases litigation risk by 2.0x

**1.2.2 Top 3 Customer Concentration**
- **Check**: Percentage of revenue from top 3 customers
- **Pass** 🟢: <25% from top 3 customers
- **Caution** 🟡: 25-50% from top 3 customers
- **Fail** 🔴: >50% from top 3 customers
- **Sources**: 10-K concentrations note
- **Red Flag**: If top customer in financial distress

**1.2.3 Geographic Concentration**
- **Check**: Revenue concentration by geography
- **Pass** 🟢: Diversified across multiple regions
- **Caution** 🟡: >40% from single country (other than home country)
- **Fail** 🔴: >60% from single foreign country with political/economic risk
- **Sources**: 10-K segment reporting, geographic revenue disclosure
- **Red Flag**: High China exposure + geopolitical tensions

**1.2.4 Product/Service Concentration**
- **Check**: Revenue from single product or service line
- **Pass** 🟢: Diversified product portfolio
- **Caution** 🟡: 40-60% from single product
- **Fail** 🔴: >60% from single product (especially if patent expiring or competitive threat)
- **Sources**: Segment reporting, product revenue disclosure
- **Red Flag**: Single-product company with generic competition coming

**1.2.5 Contract Renewal Risk**
- **Check**: Percentage of revenue from contracts expiring within 12 months
- **Pass** 🟢: <20% of revenue from expiring contracts
- **Caution** 🟡: 20-40% from expiring contracts
- **Fail** 🔴: >40% from expiring contracts, especially if renewal uncertain
- **Sources**: 10-K contract disclosures, MD&A
- **Red Flag**: Major contract up for renewal + customer in bankruptcy

### 1.3 Revenue Growth Quality

**1.3.1 Organic vs. Inorganic Growth**
- **Check**: Revenue growth from operations vs. acquisitions
- **Pass** 🟢: Primarily organic growth
- **Caution** 🟡: Mix of organic and acquisition growth
- **Fail** 🔴: Growth entirely from acquisitions, organic growth negative
- **Sources**: MD&A revenue analysis, acquisition disclosures
- **Red Flag**: Acquisitions masking declining core business

**1.3.2 Same-Store Sales Growth (Retail/Restaurants)**
- **Check**: Comparable store sales growth
- **Pass** 🟢: Positive comps for past 4 quarters
- **Caution** 🟡: 1-2 quarters of negative comps
- **Fail** 🔴: 3+ quarters of negative comps or accelerating decline
- **N/A**: Not retail/restaurant
- **Sources**: Earnings releases, 10-Q MD&A
- **Benchmark**: Negative comps = leading indicator of revenue miss

**1.3.3 Price vs. Volume Growth**
- **Check**: Revenue growth driven by price increases vs. unit volume
- **Pass** 🟢: Growth from volume increases
- **Caution** 🟡: Growth from mix of price and volume
- **Fail** 🔴: Growth entirely from price increases while volume declining
- **Sources**: MD&A, earnings call transcripts
- **Red Flag**: Price increases + volume declines = losing market share

**1.3.4 Deferred Revenue Trends**
- **Check**: For SaaS/subscription companies, deferred revenue growth
- **Pass** 🟢: Deferred revenue growing faster than recognized revenue
- **Caution** 🟡: Deferred revenue growing in line with revenue
- **Fail** 🔴: Deferred revenue declining or growing slower than revenue
- **N/A**: Not subscription business model
- **Sources**: Balance sheet, MD&A
- **Red Flag**: Declining deferred revenue = bookings slowing

**1.3.5 Revenue Guidance Track Record**
- **Check**: History of meeting/beating revenue guidance
- **Pass** 🟢: Consistently meets or beats guidance (last 4 quarters)
- **Caution** 🟡: Mixed record (2/4 quarters missed)
- **Fail** 🔴: Consistently misses guidance (3+ quarters) or multiple cuts
- **Sources**: Earnings releases, guidance announcements
- **Red Flag**: Pattern of missing guidance = litigation trigger

## CATEGORY 2: PROFITABILITY & MARGIN ANALYSIS (15 Checks)

### 2.1 Gross Margin Analysis

**2.1.1 Gross Margin Level**
- **Check**: Gross margin compared to industry benchmarks
- **Pass** 🟢: At or above industry average
- **Caution** 🟡: 5-10% below industry average
- **Fail** 🔴: >10% below industry average or <20% absolute
- **N/A**: Pre-revenue company
- **Sources**: Income statement, industry reports
- **Benchmarks**: SaaS >70%, Pharma >80%, Retail 30-40%, Manufacturing 25-35%

**2.1.2 Gross Margin Trend**
- **Check**: Gross margin trajectory over past 8 quarters
- **Pass** 🟢: Stable or improving
- **Caution** 🟡: Declining <2% over 8 quarters
- **Fail** 🔴: Declining >2% over 8 quarters or accelerating decline
- **Sources**: Quarterly income statements
- **Red Flag**: Margin compression = pricing pressure or cost inflation

**2.1.3 Gross Margin Volatility**
- **Check**: Quarter-to-quarter gross margin stability
- **Pass** 🟢: Variance <1% between quarters
- **Caution** 🟡: Variance 1-3% between quarters
- **Fail** 🔴: Variance >3% between quarters (excluding seasonal businesses)
- **Sources**: Quarterly financials
- **Red Flag**: High volatility = unpredictable business or accounting manipulation

---

### 2.2 Operating Margin Analysis

**2.2.1 Operating Margin Level**
- **Check**: Operating margin compared to peers
- **Pass** 🟢: At or above peer median
- **Caution** 🟡: Below peer median but positive
- **Fail** 🔴: Negative operating margin for 4+ consecutive quarters (excluding early-stage)
- **Sources**: Income statement, peer comparison
- **Red Flag**: Negative operating margin + no path to profitability

**2.2.2 Operating Leverage**
- **Check**: Operating margin improvement as revenue grows
- **Pass** 🟢: Operating margin expanding with revenue growth
- **Caution** 🟡: Operating margin flat despite revenue growth
- **Fail** 🔴: Operating margin declining despite revenue growth
- **Sources**: Multi-period income statements
- **Red Flag**: No operating leverage = business model problem

**2.2.3 SG&A as % of Revenue**
- **Check**: Selling, general & administrative expense ratio
- **Pass** 🟢: <25% of revenue and stable/declining
- **Caution** 🟡: 25-35% of revenue
- **Fail** 🔴: >35% of revenue or rapidly increasing
- **Sources**: Income statement
- **Red Flag**: Rising SG&A % = inefficiency or growth investments not paying off

**2.2.4 R&D Spending Trends**
- **Check**: R&D as % of revenue trend
- **Pass** 🟢: Stable or increasing (for tech/pharma)
- **Caution** 🟡: Declining slightly
- **Fail** 🔴: Significant decline in R&D spending (>20% cut)
- **N/A**: Non-R&D intensive industry
- **Sources**: Income statement, MD&A
- **Red Flag**: Cutting R&D to meet earnings = mortgaging future

### 2.3 Earnings Quality

**2.3.1 GAAP vs. Non-GAAP Earnings Gap**
- **Check**: Difference between GAAP and adjusted earnings
- **Pass** 🟢: Minimal difference (<10%)
- **Caution** 🟡: 10-25% difference with reasonable adjustments
- **Fail** 🔴: >25% difference or questionable adjustments
- **Sources**: Earnings releases, reconciliation tables
- **Red Flag**: Large gap = aggressive non-GAAP reporting

**2.3.2 One-Time Items Frequency**
- **Check**: How often "one-time" charges occur
- **Pass** 🟢: Truly infrequent (once every 2+ years)
- **Caution** 🟡: Annual one-time items
- **Fail** 🔴: Quarterly "one-time" items (not really one-time)
- **Sources**: Income statement, MD&A
- **Red Flag**: Recurring "one-time" charges = masking operating problems

**2.3.3 Earnings Surprise Pattern**
- **Check**: Pattern of beating/missing analyst estimates
- **Pass** 🟢: Consistent beats or meets
- **Caution** 🟡: Mixed results
- **Fail** 🔴: Consistent misses (3+ quarters) or beats by exactly 1 cent (suspicious)
- **Sources**: Earnings releases, analyst consensus data
- **Red Flag**: Beating by exactly 1 cent every quarter = earnings management

**2.3.4 Accrual Quality**
- **Check**: Cash earnings vs. accrual earnings
- **Pass** 🟢: Operating cash flow > net income
- **Caution** 🟡: Operating cash flow roughly equal to net income
- **Fail** 🔴: Operating cash flow significantly < net income for multiple quarters
- **Sources**: Income statement, cash flow statement
- **Red Flag**: Earnings not converting to cash = low quality earnings

**2.3.5 Tax Rate Analysis**
- **Check**: Effective tax rate compared to statutory rate
- **Pass** 🟢: Effective rate near statutory rate or explained by permanent differences
- **Caution** 🟡: Effective rate 5-10% below statutory
- **Fail** 🔴: Effective rate >10% below statutory with weak explanation or volatile
- **Sources**: Income statement, tax note
- **Red Flag**: Unsustainably low tax rate boosting earnings

**2.3.6 Pension/OPEB Assumptions**
- **Check**: Pension discount rate and return assumptions
- **Pass** 🟢: Conservative assumptions in line with peers
- **Caution** 🟡: Slightly aggressive assumptions
- **Fail** 🔴: Highly aggressive assumptions (high return, high discount rate)
- **N/A**: No defined benefit pension
- **Sources**: Pension note in 10-K
- **Red Flag**: Aggressive assumptions reduce pension expense artificially

## CATEGORY 3: BALANCE SHEET HEALTH (15 Checks)

### 3.1 Liquidity Analysis

**3.1.1 Current Ratio**
- **Check**: Current assets / current liabilities
- **Pass** 🟢: >1.5x
- **Caution** 🟡: 1.0-1.5x
- **Fail** 🔴: <1.0x (working capital deficit)
- **Sources**: Balance sheet
- **Benchmark**: <1.0x = liquidity stress

**3.1.2 Quick Ratio**
- **Check**: (Current assets - inventory) / current liabilities
- **Pass** 🟢: >1.0x
- **Caution** 🟡: 0.75-1.0x
- **Fail** 🔴: <0.75x
- **Sources**: Balance sheet
- **Red Flag**: <0.5x = severe liquidity crisis

**3.1.3 Cash Position**
- **Check**: Cash and equivalents as % of total assets
- **Pass** 🟢: >20% of assets in cash
- **Caution** 🟡: 10-20% in cash
- **Fail** 🔴: <10% in cash (excluding financial institutions)
- **Sources**: Balance sheet
- **Context**: Varies by industry (tech should have more cash)

**3.1.4 Working Capital Trend**
- **Check**: Working capital (current assets - current liabilities) trend
- **Pass** 🟢: Positive and growing
- **Caution** 🟡: Positive but declining
- **Fail** 🔴: Negative or turning negative
- **Sources**: Balance sheet over multiple periods
- **Red Flag**: Deteriorating working capital = cash flow stress

**3.1.5 Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)**
- **Check**: (Accounts receivable / revenue) × 365
- **Pass** 🟢: <45 days and stable
- **Caution** 🟡: 45-60 days or increasing slowly
- **Fail** 🔴: >60 days or rapidly increasing
- **N/A**: Pre-revenue or no A/R
- **Sources**: Balance sheet, income statement
- **Red Flag**: Rising DSO = collection problems or channel stuffing

---

### 3.2 Debt & Leverage Analysis

**3.2.1 Total Debt / EBITDA**
- **Check**: Leverage ratio
- **Pass** 🟢: <3.0x
- **Caution** 🟡: 3.0-5.0x
- **Fail** 🔴: >5.0x
- **N/A**: No debt or negative EBITDA
- **Sources**: Balance sheet, income statement
- **Benchmark**: >5.0x = high leverage risk

**3.2.2 Net Debt / EBITDA**
- **Check**: (Total debt - cash) / EBITDA
- **Pass** 🟢: <2.5x or negative (net cash)
- **Caution** 🟡: 2.5-4.0x
- **Fail** 🔴: >4.0x
- **Sources**: Balance sheet, income statement
- **Red Flag**: High net leverage + declining EBITDA = covenant risk

**3.2.3 Interest Coverage Ratio**
- **Check**: EBIT / Interest expense
- **Pass** 🟢: >4.0x
- **Caution** 🟡: 2.0-4.0x
- **Fail** 🔴: <2.0x
- **N/A**: No debt
- **Sources**: Income statement
- **Benchmark**: <2.0x = critical stress

**3.2.4 Debt Maturity Profile**
- **Check**: Percentage of debt maturing within 12 months
- **Pass** 🟢: <20% maturing in next 12 months
- **Caution** 🟡: 20-40% maturing in next 12 months
- **Fail** 🔴: >40% maturing in next 12 months (refinancing risk)
- **Sources**: Debt note in 10-K, balance sheet
- **Red Flag**: Large maturity + weak credit = refinancing crisis

**3.2.5 Covenant Compliance**
- **Check**: Headroom on debt covenants
- **Pass** 🟢: >20% headroom on all covenants
- **Caution** 🟡: 10-20% headroom
- **Fail** 🔴: <10% headroom or covenant breach/waiver
- **N/A**: No debt covenants
- **Sources**: Debt note in 10-K, MD&A
- **Red Flag**: Covenant waiver = severe distress

## CATEGORY 4: CASH FLOW ANALYSIS (15 Checks)

- **Pass** 🟢: Positive and growing
- **Caution** 🟡: Positive but declining

- **Pass** 🟢: >10% and stable/improving
- **Caution** 🟡: 5-10%
- **Fail** 🔴: <5% or negative

**4.1.3 Operating Cash Flow / Net Income**
- **Check**: Cash conversion ratio
- **Pass** 🟢: >1.0x (cash exceeds earnings)

## CATEGORY 5: ACCOUNTING QUALITY & AUDIT INTEGRITY (12 Checks)

### 5.1 Audit Quality Indicators

**5.1.1 Audit Integrity AGR Score**
- **Check**: Third-party accounting quality score (if available)
- **Pass** 🟢: AGR score >70 ("Conservative" or "Average")
- **Caution** 🟡: AGR score 50-70 ("Aggressive")
- **Fail** 🔴: AGR score <50 ("Very Aggressive")
- **Unknown** 🟣: AGR score not available
- **Sources**: Audit Integrity service, Bloomberg
- **Benchmark**: AGR <50 = 2.5x higher litigation probability

**5.1.2 Auditor Quality**
- **Check**: Auditor firm and tenure
- **Pass** 🟢: Big 4 auditor with >3 years tenure
- **Caution** 🟡: Big 4 auditor with <3 years tenure or mid-tier firm
- **Fail** 🔴: Small/unknown auditor or frequent auditor changes
- **Sources**: 10-K audit opinion
- **Red Flag**: Non-Big 4 for large public company = quality concern

**5.1.3 Auditor Changes**
- **Check**: Auditor changes in past 5 years
- **Pass** 🟢: No auditor changes
- **Caution** 🟡: One auditor change with benign reason
- **Fail** 🔴: Multiple auditor changes or change due to disagreement
- **Sources**: Form 8-K Item 4.01 filings
- **Red Flag**: Auditor resignation or dismissal = serious issue

**5.1.4 Audit Fees Trend**
- **Check**: Audit fees relative to company size
- **Pass** 🟢: Audit fees stable and reasonable
- **Caution** 🟡: Audit fees increasing faster than revenue
- **Fail** 🔴: Audit fees spiking (>50% increase) = complex issues
- **Sources**: Proxy statement (DEF 14A)
- **Red Flag**: Spiking audit fees = accounting problems

**5.1.5 Non-Audit Fees**
- **Check**: Non-audit fees paid to auditor
- **Pass** 🟢: Non-audit fees <25% of audit fees
- **Caution** 🟡: Non-audit fees 25-50% of audit fees
- **Fail** 🔴: Non-audit fees >50% of audit fees (independence concern)
- **Sources**: Proxy statement
- **Red Flag**: High non-audit fees = auditor independence questioned
