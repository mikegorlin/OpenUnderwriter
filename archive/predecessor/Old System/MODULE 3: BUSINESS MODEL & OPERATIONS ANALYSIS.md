# MODULE 3: BUSINESS MODEL & OPERATIONS ANALYSIS

**Purpose**: Assess business model sustainability, competitive position, operational risks, customer/supplier dependencies, and M&A integration quality.

**Total Checks**: ~70 enumerated checks across 7 categories

**When to Use**:
- Run after Module 2 passes (no financial distress auto-decline)
- Critical for understanding long-term viability
- Identifies business model risks that may not show in financials yet

**Auto-Decline Triggers in This Module**:
- Core product failure + no viable alternatives + declining revenue
- Loss of customer representing >50% of revenue + no replacement
- Failed M&A integration + goodwill impairment + restatement
- Obsolete business model + market share collapse + no pivot strategy

---

## CATEGORY 1: BUSINESS MODEL SUSTAINABILITY (12 Checks)

### 1.1 Business Model Fundamentals

**1.1.1 Business Model Clarity**
- **Check**: Can the business model be clearly explained?
- **Pass** 🟢: Clear value proposition and revenue model
- **Caution** 🟡: Complex model requiring detailed explanation
- **Fail** 🔴: Unclear how company makes money or creates value
- **Sources**: 10-K Business section, investor presentations
- **Red Flag**: If analysts/investors don't understand model = credibility risk

**1.1.2 Revenue Model Quality**
- **Check**: Sustainability and predictability of revenue streams
- **Pass** 🟢: Recurring/subscription revenue >60% of total
- **Caution** 🟡: Mix of recurring and transactional revenue
- **Fail** 🔴: Entirely transactional/one-time revenue with no visibility
- **Sources**: Revenue disclosures, business model description
- **Benchmark**: Recurring revenue = higher quality, more predictable

**1.1.3 Unit Economics**
- **Check**: Profitability at the unit/customer level
- **Pass** 🟢: Positive unit economics with improving trends
- **Caution** 🟡: Breakeven unit economics
- **Fail** 🔴: Negative unit economics with no path to profitability
- **Sources**: Investor presentations, earnings calls
- **Red Flag**: Losing money on every sale = unsustainable

**1.1.4 Scalability**
- **Check**: Ability to grow without proportional cost increases
- **Pass** 🟢: High operating leverage (software, platforms)
- **Caution** 🟡: Moderate scalability
- **Fail** 🔴: Linear cost growth with revenue (no leverage)
- **Sources**: Financial statements, margin trends
- **Red Flag**: No operating leverage = limited profit potential

**1.1.5 Regulatory Dependency**
- **Check**: Business model dependent on regulatory approval/framework
- **Pass** 🟢: Minimal regulatory dependency
- **Caution** 🟡: Operates in regulated industry with stable framework
- **Fail** 🔴: Business model depends on uncertain regulatory approval
- **Sources**: Risk factors, business description
- **Example**: Biotech with single drug in trials = binary regulatory risk

**1.1.6 Network Effects**
- **Check**: Does business benefit from network effects?
- **Pass** 🟢: Strong network effects (value increases with users)
- **Caution** 🟡: Moderate network effects
- **Fail** 🔴: No network effects + commoditized offering
- **N/A**: Not applicable to business type
- **Sources**: Business model analysis
- **Context**: Network effects create competitive moats

## CATEGORY 2: COMPETITIVE POSITION & MARKET DYNAMICS (12 Checks)

### 2.1 Market Position

**2.1.1 Market Share**
- **Check**: Company's share of addressable market
- **Pass** 🟢: #1 or #2 market position
- **Caution** 🟡: Top 5 market position
- **Fail** 🔴: Small player with declining share
- **Sources**: Industry reports, company disclosures
- **Benchmark**: Market leaders have pricing power

**2.1.2 Market Share Trend**
- **Check**: Gaining or losing market share
- **Pass** 🟢: Gaining share over past 2 years
- **Caution** 🟡: Stable market share
- **Fail** 🔴: Losing share to competitors
- **Sources**: Industry reports, revenue growth vs. market growth
- **Red Flag**: Losing share = competitive weakness

**2.1.3 Competitive Intensity**
- **Check**: Number and strength of competitors
- **Pass** 🟢: Oligopoly with 2-3 major players
- **Caution** 🟡: Competitive but stable market
- **Fail** 🔴: Highly fragmented or intense price competition
- **Sources**: Industry analysis, competitive disclosures
- **Red Flag**: Intense competition = margin pressure

**2.1.4 Barriers to Entry**
- **Check**: Difficulty for new competitors to enter market
- **Pass** 🟢: High barriers (regulation, capital, network effects)
- **Caution** 🟡: Moderate barriers
- **Fail** 🔴: Low barriers to entry = commoditization risk
- **Sources**: Industry analysis, business model
- **Context**: High barriers protect market position

**2.1.5 Competitive Advantages (Moats)**
- **Check**: Sustainable competitive advantages
- **Pass** 🟢: Multiple moats (brand, patents, network effects, scale)
- **Caution** 🟡: One or two competitive advantages
- **Fail** 🔴: No sustainable competitive advantages
- **Sources**: Business analysis, competitive positioning
- **Red Flag**: No moat = vulnerable to competition

**2.1.6 Pricing Power**
- **Check**: Ability to raise prices without losing customers
- **Pass** 🟢: Demonstrated pricing power (recent price increases)
- **Caution** 🟡: Limited pricing power
- **Fail** 🔴: No pricing power or price declines
- **Sources**: Pricing trends, gross margin trends
- **Benchmark**: Pricing power = strong competitive position

- **Fail** 🔴: Low/no switching costs = easy to leave

**2.2.4 Competitive Product Launches**
- **Check**: Competitor product launches in past 12 months
- **Pass** 🟢: No major competitive launches
- **Caution** 🟡: Competitive launches but company responding
- **Fail** 🔴: Superior competitive products launched
- **Sources**: Industry news, product announcements
- **Red Flag**: Competitor leapfrogging technology = share loss

**2.2.5 Price Competition**
- **Check**: Industry pricing trends
- **Pass** 🟢: Stable or increasing prices
- **Caution** 🟡: Modest price competition
- **Fail** 🔴: Price war or significant price declines
- **Sources**: Industry reports, company pricing
- **Red Flag**: Price wars destroy profitability

**2.2.6 Market Saturation**
- **Check**: Room for market growth
- **Pass** 🟢: Large addressable market with low penetration
- **Caution** 🟡: Moderate growth market
- **Fail** 🔴: Saturated market with declining demand
- **Sources**: Industry growth forecasts, penetration rates
- **Red Flag**: Saturation = growth only from share gains

## CATEGORY 3: CUSTOMER CONCENTRATION & DEPENDENCY (10 Checks)

**3.1.2 Top 3 Customers Revenue %**
- **Check**: Revenue from top 3 customers

- **Caution** 🟡: 25-50% from top 3
- **Fail** 🔴: >50% from top 3

- **Red Flag**: High concentration = vulnerability

- **Fail** 🔴: Major customer in bankruptcy or severe distress

## AUTO-DECLINE TRIGGERS FOR MODULE 3

If ANY of the following are true, recommend **IMMEDIATE DECLINE**:

1. **Business Model Collapse**: Core product failed (FDA rejection, market rejection) + no viable alternative products + revenue declining >50%

2. **Customer Concentration Crisis**: Lost customer representing >50% of revenue + no replacement identified + revenue guidance cut >40%

3. **Failed M&A Integration**: Major acquisition (>50% of market cap) + goodwill impairment >30% + customer losses >25% + restatement

4. **Obsolete Business Model**: Market share declining >30% over 2 years + no competitive response + new entrants taking share + no pivot strategy

5. **Supply Chain Collapse**: Single-source supplier failed + no alternative + production halted >60 days + revenue impact >40%

6. **Product Safety Crisis**: Major product recall + safety deaths/injuries + regulatory action + class action litigation

## VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

### Primary Sources (Required):
- **10-K Business Section**: Business model description
- **10-K Risk Factors**: Competitive and operational risks
- **Segment Reporting**: Product/customer concentration
- **MD&A**: Operational trends and challenges
- **Earnings Calls**: Management commentary on operations

### Secondary Sources (Verification):
- **Industry Reports**: Market share, competitive position
- **News Sources**: Product launches, customer wins/losses, M&A
- **Customer Reviews**: Product quality, service delivery
- **Glassdoor**: Employee feedback on integration, culture

### Verification Standard:
- **Business Model Assessment**: 1 authoritative source (10-K) + industry analysis
