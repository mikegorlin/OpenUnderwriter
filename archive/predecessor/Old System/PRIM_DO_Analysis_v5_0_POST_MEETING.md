## PART 1: OPERATIONAL CLARITY - HOW THE BUSINESS WORKS

### 1.1 Business Model Overview

**Company Type**: **Specialty Contractor** (NOT a large EPC contractor)

**Key Distinction**:
- **Average contract size**: <$3 million (vs. $50M+ for large EPC contractors)
- **Contract duration**: Typically 6-12 months (vs. multi-year for EPC)
- **Risk profile**: Lower single-project exposure due to small contract size
- **Execution model**: High-volume, short-duration projects vs. few large, long-duration projects

**Why This Matters for D&O**:
- ✅ **Lower project execution risk**: Small contracts limit single-project losses
- ✅ **Faster revenue recognition**: 6-12 month projects vs. multi-year (less forecasting risk)
- ✅ **Diversified revenue**: Thousands of small contracts vs. handful of large contracts
- ❌ **Higher operational complexity**: Managing thousands of projects simultaneously

### 1.3 Contract Structure & Revenue Model

**Contract Types** (Mix reduces risk):

1. **Master Service Agreements (MSAs)** - **49% of backlog**
   - **Structure**: Multi-year agreements for recurring services
   - **Pricing**: Typically unit price or time-and-materials
   - **Revenue predictability**: HIGH (recurring, predictable)
   - **Execution risk**: LOW (small, repetitive tasks)
   - **Examples**: Utility line maintenance, gas system maintenance, refinery turnarounds
   - **D&O Implication**: ✅ **49% of backlog is low-risk, predictable revenue**

2. **Unit Price Contracts** - **~30% of backlog** (estimated)
   - **Structure**: Fixed price per unit (e.g., $X per linear foot of pipeline)
   - **Risk**: MODERATE (quantity risk, not price risk)
   - **Execution risk**: MODERATE (productivity drives profitability)
   - **Examples**: Transmission line construction ($X per mile), solar PV installation ($X per MW)
   - **D&O Implication**: ⚠️ **Moderate risk** - productivity issues can erode margins

3. **Reimbursable (Cost-Plus) Contracts** - **~10% of backlog** (estimated)
   - **Structure**: Cost reimbursement plus fixed fee or percentage
   - **Risk**: LOW (customer bears cost risk)
   - **Execution risk**: LOW (profitability protected)
   - **Examples**: Emergency repairs, complex engineering projects
   - **D&O Implication**: ✅ **Low risk** - customer bears cost overruns

4. **Fixed-Price (Lump-Sum) Contracts** - **~11% of backlog** (estimated)
   - **Structure**: Fixed total price for defined scope
   - **Risk**: HIGH (contractor bears all cost risk)
   - **Execution risk**: HIGH (cost overruns = losses)
   - **Examples**: Some solar PV EPC projects, some highway projects
   - **D&O Implication**: ⚠️ **Highest risk** - cost overruns can cause material losses
   - **Mitigation**: Primoris uses comprehensive risk review process (CFO involved) before bidding

**Key Insight**: **Primoris' contract mix is FAVORABLE** - 49% MSAs (low risk) + 10% reimbursable (low risk) = **59% of backlog is low-risk**. Only ~11% is high-risk fixed-price.
