## ADDRESSING PL'S SPECIFIC CONCERNS

### 1. Oracle Debt Concern

**PL's Statement**: "Oracle was down as much as 15% today due to concerns on their debt in order to support their AI spending"

**Our Response**:
- **Decline Oracle** until debt concerns resolved
- **Screen all accounts** for AI-specific debt issuance
- **Decline any account** with >$5B AI-specific debt and coverage <1.5x
- **Add underwriting question**: "What portion of total debt was issued specifically for AI investments, and what is the maturity profile?"

### 2. Hyperscaler Exposure Limits

**PL's Question**: "How much exposure do we want to the hyper-scalers?"

**Our Response**:
- **Individual caps**: $8M per Tier 1 hyperscaler (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Apple)
- **Individual caps**: $5M per Tier 2 hyperscaler (Meta, Nvidia)
- **Aggregate cap**: $35M total across all hyperscalers
- **Rationale**: Limits concentration risk while allowing meaningful participation

### 3. Circular Investing / Antitrust

**PL's Statement**: "Circular investing in this arena potentially creating antitrust issues"

**Our Response**:
- **Add underwriting question**: "What percentage of revenue comes from customers who are also investors?"
- **Decline if >20%** revenue from circular arrangements
- **Monitor for antitrust risk**: Track DOJ/FTC investigations; adjust underwriting if enforcement increases
- **Specific concern**: OpenAI/Microsoft/Nvidia circular arrangements - decline these pure-plays

### 4. Lending Proliferation Impact on Financial Institutions

**PL's Statement**: "Lending in this space is concerning and that will likely have ramifications for financial institutions"

**Our Response**:
- **For FI customers**: Add AI lending exposure question to renewals
- **For AI borrowers**: Screen for vendor financing >20% of revenue
- **Monitor**: SPV proliferation, covenant-lite terms, PIK toggle features
- **Action**: If FI customer has material AI lending exposure, assess credit quality and regulatory risk

### 5. Workforce Displacement / EPL Exposure

**PL's Statement**: "Howard Marks post script expresses concerns about the impact on our workforce which could bleed into EPL claims"

**Our Response**:
- **Add EPL surcharge** for companies planning >20% workforce reduction due to AI
- **Assess board oversight**: Does board have process for managing workforce transition?
- **Disclosure risk**: Are workforce displacement plans adequately disclosed?
- **Specific concern**: Companies replacing developers with AI (coding automation) or customer service with AI chatbots
- **Monitor**: Age discrimination claims (older workers displaced by AI), ADA claims (AI replacing accommodated workers)
