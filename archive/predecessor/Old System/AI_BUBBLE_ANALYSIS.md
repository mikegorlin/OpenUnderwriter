# AI BUBBLE ANALYSIS: IS THIS 2000 ALL OVER AGAIN?
## The Central Underwriting Question for D&O Insurance

**Date**: December 2025  
**Purpose**: Analyze whether AI represents a speculative bubble or genuine transformation, and justify optionality-driven underwriting approach

---

## THE FUNDAMENTAL QUESTION

> **"Is AI the next electricity (transformative inflection), or the next dot-com (speculative bubble)?"**

**Why This Matters for D&O Underwriting**:
- If AI is **transformative** → Companies without AI exposure face obsolescence → D&O risk for failing to adapt
- If AI is a **bubble** → Companies with heavy AI exposure face collapse → D&O risk for overstating AI capabilities
- **Our answer**: We don't know, so we underwrite companies that **succeed in EITHER scenario**

---

## PART 1: WHAT MAKES THIS LOOK LIKE A BUBBLE

### **1.1 Extreme Valuation Disconnects**

#### **Evidence**:

| Metric | Dot-com Bubble (1999-2000) | AI Boom (2023-2025) | Assessment |
|--------|---------------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Pure-play valuations** | 100-500x revenue (Pets.com: 485x) | Pre-revenue AI startups at $1B+ valuations | ⚠️ **SIMILAR** |
| **Stock run-ups** | Nasdaq +400% (1995-2000) | Nvidia +700% (2022-2024) | ⚠️ **SIMILAR** |
| **"New paradigm" rhetoric** | "Internet changes everything" | "AI changes everything" | ⚠️ **SIMILAR** |
| **Profitability ignored** | "Revenue growth is all that matters" | "AI investment is all that matters" | ⚠️ **SIMILAR** |

#### **Specific Examples**:

**Dot-com Bubble**:
- **Pets.com**: $300M valuation, $619K revenue = **485x revenue** → Bankruptcy within 9 months
- **Webvan**: $1.2B IPO valuation, minimal revenue → Bankruptcy within 2 years
- **eToys**: $8B market cap at peak → Bankruptcy within 2 years
- **Lesson**: Extreme valuations without profits = bubble

**AI Boom**:
- **OpenAI**: $157B valuation (Oct 2024), ~$5B revenue = **31x revenue** (better than dot-com but still extreme)
- **Anthropic**: $18B valuation, minimal revenue
- **Character.AI**: $1B valuation, no clear revenue model
- **Lesson**: Similar valuation patterns to dot-com, but not as extreme (yet)

#### **D&O Risk Connection**:
- **Extreme valuations** → When bubble bursts → Stock collapses 80-100% → Massive securities litigation
- **Dot-com aftermath**: $10B+ in D&O settlements (WorldCom $6B, Enron $7B+, others)
- **AI risk**: If AI bubble bursts, similar litigation wave expected

### **1.2 Circular Deals & Revenue Quality Concerns**

#### **Evidence**:

**Dot-com Bubble**:
- **Enron**: Circular deals with SPEs (Special Purpose Entities) to inflate revenue
- **WorldCom**: Related party transactions to boost revenue
- **Global Crossing**: Swapped fiber capacity with other telecoms to create circular revenue
- **Lesson**: Circular deals = revenue fraud = bankruptcy = massive D&O claims

**AI Boom**:
- **Microsoft/OpenAI**: Microsoft invests $13B in OpenAI → OpenAI commits to $300B cloud spend with Microsoft
  - Is this **real revenue** (OpenAI genuinely needs Azure) or **circular** (Microsoft funding its own revenue)?
  - **Concern**: If OpenAI fails, Microsoft's AI revenue evaporates
  
- **Oracle/OpenAI**: Oracle building data centers primarily for OpenAI (~40% of AI capex)
  - OpenAI is **both** customer AND strategic partner
  - **Concern**: Single customer concentration + circular deal structure
  - **Reality check**: Stock declined -15% in Dec 2025; bonds at junk levels

- **Amazon/Anthropic**: Amazon invests $4B in Anthropic → Anthropic uses AWS
  - Similar circular structure

**Regulatory Scrutiny**:
- DOJ/FTC investigating circular deals in AI sector
- Antitrust concerns about "pay-to-play" cloud arrangements
- **D&O risk**: If deals deemed anticompetitive → Regulatory enforcement → Securities litigation

#### **Key Difference from Dot-com**:
- **Dot-com**: Circular deals were **pure fraud** (no real services exchanged)
- **AI**: Circular deals involve **real services** (cloud computing is real), but revenue quality is questionable
- **Verdict**: ⚠️ **CONCERNING but not yet fraudulent**

### **1.3 Infrastructure Buildout Without Proven Demand**

- **Result**:

- **Lesson**: Debt-financed infrastructure without proven demand = disaster

**AI Boom (2023-2025)**:
- **Thesis**: "AI will transform everything" → Need massive data center infrastructure
- **Reality**: Companies borrowing/spending hundreds of billions on AI infrastructure
- **Problem**: Revenue from AI not yet proven at scale

**Specific Examples**:

## PART 3: HOWARD MARKS' FRAMEWORK - "INFLECTION VS. MEAN-REVERSION"

### **The Central Question**

> **"Is AI an inflection point (genuine transformation) or mean-reversion (return to normal after hype)?"**

### **Howard Marks' Insight (Dec 2024 Memo)**

**Two Possible Scenarios**:

1. **INFLECTION (AI is transformative like electricity)**:
   - AI genuinely transforms productivity, business models, society
   - Companies with AI exposure thrive
   - Companies without AI exposure become obsolete
   - **Investment implication**: Should invest heavily in AI companies
   
2. **MEAN-REVERSION (AI is overhyped; bubble bursts)**:
   - AI is useful but not transformative
   - Valuations return to normal
   - Companies with heavy AI exposure face stock declines
   - **Investment implication**: Should avoid AI companies

**The Problem**: **We don't know which scenario will play out**

### **Howard Marks' Solution**

> "No one should go all-in without acknowledging risk of ruin. But no one should stay all-out and risk missing out. **A moderate position, applied with selectivity and prudence, seems like the best approach.**"

**Translation**: 
- Don't bet everything on AI (risk of ruin if bubble)
- Don't avoid AI entirely (risk of missing out if transformative)
- **Take a moderate position in companies that succeed in EITHER scenario**

---

### **D&O Underwriting Translation**

#### **The Optimal Strategy**:

> **Underwrite companies with OPTIONALITY - those that succeed whether AI is inflection OR mean-reversion**

**Companies That Succeed in BOTH Scenarios**:

|----------|--------------------------------|----------------------------|----------------|
| **Microsoft** | Azure AI grows; Copilot succeeds | Office, Windows, Gaming remain profitable | ✅ **SURVIVES BOTH** |
| **Salesforce** | AI features command premium | CRM core remains profitable | ✅ **SURVIVES BOTH** |

**Key Insight**: 
- **Microsoft, Salesforce, Apple, Adobe** = **LOW D&O RISK** (optionality)
- **Oracle** = **HIGH D&O RISK** (existential AI bet due to debt)
- **C3.ai** = **EXTREME D&O RISK** (pure-play; no survival path if AI fails)

- **Pure-play AI companies**: Collapse → Stock crashes 80-100% → **EXTREME D&O RISK**

**Conclusion**: **Companies with optionality have LOWEST D&O risk across scenarios**

## PART 4: THE UNDERWRITING ANSWER

### **Our Position: "Selective Participation with Optionality Focus"**

#### **What We Believe**:

1. ✅ **AI is real** (not pure speculation like dot-com)
2. ✅ **AI is generating real revenue** (unlike dot-com vaporware)
3. ⚠️ **AI valuations are elevated** (but not as extreme as dot-com)
4. ⚠️ **Some AI investments are speculative** (circular deals, pure-plays)
5. ❓ **We don't know if AI is inflection or mean-reversion**

#### **What We Do**:

**✅ TARGET (Green Bucket)**:
- Companies with **profitable non-AI cores** (optionality)
- Companies that can **afford AI from operating cash flow** (sustainable)
- Companies with **proven execution** (track record)
- Companies with **no critical red flags** (clean structure)

**Examples**: Microsoft, Apple, Salesforce, Adobe, Google (selective)

**⚠️ SELECTIVE PARTICIPATION (Yellow Bucket)**:
- Companies with **some optionality** but elevated concerns
- Companies with **margin pressure** but manageable (Broadcom)
- Companies with **execution risk** but strong fundamentals
- **Pricing**: +30-75% load to compensate for volatility

**Examples**: Amazon, Nvidia, Broadcom, Google (with concerns)

**❌ DECLINE (Red Bucket)**:
- Companies that **cannot afford AI from OCF** (Oracle: 320% capex/OCF)
- Companies with **circular deals >20%** (revenue quality concerns)
- Companies with **single customer >40%** (concentration risk)
- Companies with **AI washing** (fraud risk)
- **Pure-play AI companies** with no fallback (C3.ai, most AI startups)

**Examples**: Oracle, C3.ai, most AI pure-plays, circular deal participants

---

### **Why This Approach Minimizes D&O Risk**

#### **If AI Is Transformative (Inflection)**:
- ✅ Our portfolio companies (Green/Yellow) benefit from AI growth
- ✅ They have profitable cores, so they're not "left behind"
- ✅ We participate in upside while maintaining discipline

#### **If AI Is a Bubble (Mean-Reversion)**:
- ✅ Our portfolio companies (Green/Yellow) have profitable non-AI cores → Survive

- ✅ Elevated pricing (+30-75% on Yellow) compensates for volatility

#### **Either Way**:
- ✅ **We maintain ~75% decline rate** on AI-exposed companies (discipline)
- ✅ **We focus on optionality** (companies that succeed in BOTH scenarios)
- ✅ **We avoid existential bets** (Oracle-style debt-financed AI without proven revenue)
