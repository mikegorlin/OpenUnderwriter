# AI Company Portfolio Strategy: 5-Factor Binary Framework
## Public Company Management Liability Underwriting

**Date**: December 2025  
**Purpose**: Simplified, practical framework for classifying AI-exposed companies into 3 risk buckets

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### The Framework

**5 Binary Risk Factors** → **3 Simple Buckets** (Green/Yellow/Red) → **Clear Pricing/Decline Decisions**

| Bucket | Risk Level | Portfolio Allocation | Pricing Load | Action |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|--------------|--------|
| **GREEN** | Low Risk | 60-70% | +10-25% | Accept - Target these |
| **YELLOW** | Medium Risk | 25-30% | +30-75% | Accept with caution |
| **RED** | High Risk | 0-5% | N/A | **DECLINE** |

**Key Principle**: Answer 5 simple YES/NO questions → Bucket placement is automatic

---

## PART 1: THE 5 BINARY RISK FACTORS

### **FACTOR 1: Can They Afford AI from Operating Cash Flow?**

#### **The Question**:
> "Can the company fund its AI investments (capex + debt service) from operating cash flow, or are they betting on AI revenue growth to pay back borrowing?"

#### **Binary Assessment**:

| Answer | Criteria | D&O Risk Level |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| ✅ **YES** (GREEN) | • AI capex <50% of annual operating cash flow<br>• Minimal or zero AI-specific debt<br>• Free cash flow remains positive after AI investments<br>• Can service any AI debt from current operations | **LOW RISK** |
| ⚠️ **MAYBE** (YELLOW) | • AI capex 50-75% of OCF<br>• Some AI debt but coverage >3x from OCF<br>• FCF slightly positive or breakeven after AI | **MEDIUM RISK** |
| ❌ **NO** (RED) | • AI capex >75% of OCF<br>• Significant AI debt (>$10B) with coverage <3x<br>• FCF negative after AI investments<br>• Betting on AI revenue to service debt | **HIGH RISK** |

#### **WHY This Matters for D&O Risk**:

1. **Securities Litigation Risk**: If AI investments fail to generate expected returns, stock price declines → securities class actions alleging:
   - Misleading statements about AI strategy
   - Failure to disclose AI investment risks
   - Overstating AI revenue potential

2. **Debt Covenant Risk**: Companies with high AI debt may breach covenants if AI revenue disappoints → bondholder lawsuits against directors

3. **Solvency Risk**: Companies burning cash on AI without revenue may face liquidity crisis → derivative suits for breach of fiduciary duty

4. **Oracle Example**: $100B AI debt + $48B annual AI capex vs. $15B OCF = **Cannot afford** → Stock declined -15%, bonds at junk levels → **D&O exposure**

#### **How to Assess**:

**Data Sources**:
- 10-K/10-Q: Cash Flow Statement (Operating Cash Flow, Capex)
- 10-K/10-Q: Balance Sheet (Debt levels, specifically AI-related debt)
- Earnings calls: Management commentary on AI capex plans
- Credit rating agencies: Debt coverage ratios

**Calculation**:
```
AI Capex % of OCF = (Annual AI Capex) / (Annual Operating Cash Flow)

AI Debt Service Coverage = (Operating Cash Flow) / (Annual AI Debt Service)

Free Cash Flow After AI = OCF - Total Capex - Dividends - Debt Service
```

**Examples**:
- **Microsoft**: $100B OCF, $30B AI capex = 30% → ✅ **YES** (GREEN)
- **Amazon**: $80B OCF, $40B AI capex = 50% → ⚠️ **MAYBE** (YELLOW)
- **Oracle**: $15B OCF, $48B AI capex, $100B debt = 320% → ❌ **NO** (RED)

### **FACTOR 2: Are AI Margins Sustainable or Improving?**

#### **The Question**:
> "Is the company making money on AI, or are margins under pressure / negative?"

#### **Binary Assessment**:

| Answer | Criteria | D&O Risk Level |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| ✅ **YES** (GREEN) | • AI gross margins stable (not declining)<br>• AI business profitable or clear path to profitability<br>• No warnings about margin pressure<br>• Unit economics improving over time | **LOW RISK** |
| ⚠️ **MAYBE** (YELLOW) | • AI margins declining but still positive<br>• Management warns of "near-term margin pressure"<br>• Path to profitability unclear but plausible<br>• Competitive pricing pressure | **MEDIUM RISK** |
| ❌ **NO** (RED) | • AI margins negative and worsening<br>• No clear path to profitability<br>• Burning cash on AI with no end in sight<br>• Structural margin issues (e.g., must buy 3rd party components at high cost) | **HIGH RISK** |

#### **WHY This Matters for D&O Risk**:

1. **Profitability Scrutiny**: Market is shifting from "AI revenue growth" to "AI profitability" → Companies with margin pressure face stock declines → securities litigation

2. **Misrepresentation Risk**: If company overstates AI profitability or fails to disclose margin pressures → securities fraud claims

3. **Viability Risk**: Companies with negative AI margins may not survive if bubble bursts → derivative suits for wasting corporate assets

4. **Broadcom Example**: Record revenue but "gross margins will be lower" warning → Stock declined -11% → **D&O exposure**

#### **How to Assess**:

**Data Sources**:
- 10-K/10-Q: Segment reporting (if AI is separate segment)
- Earnings calls: Management commentary on AI margins
- Analyst reports: Margin analysis and trends
- Competitor comparison: How do margins compare to peers?

**Key Questions**:
- Is gross margin on AI products/services stable, improving, or declining?
- Does management warn about margin pressure?
- Are AI margins better or worse than non-AI business?
- What's the trajectory - improving or worsening?

**Examples**:
- **Microsoft**: Azure AI margins stable/improving → ✅ **YES** (GREEN)
- **Broadcom**: "Gross margins will be lower" warning → ❌ **NO** (RED) or ⚠️ **MAYBE** (YELLOW)
- **C3.ai**: Negative margins, unclear path to profitability → ❌ **NO** (RED)

---

### **FACTOR 3: Do They Have a Profitable Non-AI Core Business?**

#### **The Question**:
> "If AI completely fails, does the company survive? Or is AI existential?"

#### **Binary Assessment**:

| Answer | Criteria | D&O Risk Level |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| ✅ **YES** (GREEN) | • Profitable core business independent of AI<br>• AI is <30% of revenue<br>• Company was profitable before AI boom<br>• Multiple revenue streams; diversified | **LOW RISK** |
| ⚠️ **MAYBE** (YELLOW) | • AI is 30-60% of revenue<br>• Core business profitable but AI is significant<br>• Heavy pivot to AI but fallback exists<br>• Moderate diversification | **MEDIUM RISK** |
| ❌ **NO** (RED) | • AI is >60% of revenue<br>• Pre-revenue or unprofitable without AI<br>• "Pure-play" AI company<br>• No fallback if AI fails | **HIGH RISK** |

#### **WHY This Matters for D&O Risk**:

1. **Optionality = Lower Risk**: Companies with profitable non-AI cores can survive AI bubble burst → Lower securities litigation risk

2. **Existential Risk**: Pure-play AI companies face existential risk if bubble bursts → Bankruptcy → derivative suits, bondholder suits

3. **Strategic Flexibility**: Companies with diversified revenue can pivot away from AI if needed → Directors have options → Lower breach of duty risk

4. **Howard Marks Principle**: "Moderate position with optionality" → Companies that survive in EITHER scenario (AI succeeds OR fails) are lower risk

#### **How to Assess**:

**Data Sources**:
- 10-K: Business description, segment reporting
- 10-K/10-Q: Revenue breakdown by product/service
- Historical financials: Was company profitable before AI?
- Management commentary: How dependent is strategy on AI?

**Key Questions**:
- What % of revenue comes from AI vs. non-AI?
- Was the company profitable before the AI boom (2022)?
- If AI revenue disappeared tomorrow, would company survive?
- How diversified are revenue streams?

**Examples**:
- **Microsoft**: Office, Azure (non-AI), Windows, Gaming → ✅ **YES** (GREEN) - AI is enhancement, not existential
- **Salesforce**: CRM is profitable core, AI is add-on → ✅ **YES** (GREEN)
- **OpenAI** (if public): 100% AI revenue, no fallback → ❌ **NO** (RED)
- **C3.ai**: Pure-play AI, unprofitable → ❌ **NO** (RED)

### **FACTOR 4: Any Critical Red Flags?**

#### **The Question**:
> "Are there any automatic disqualifiers: circular deals, single customer concentration, or AI washing?"

#### **Binary Assessment**:

| Answer | Criteria | D&O Risk Level |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| ✅ **NO RED FLAGS** (GREEN) | • No circular deals (>20% revenue from investor-backed customers)<br>• No single customer >40% of revenue/capex<br>• No AI washing (real AI products/revenue)<br>• Transparent business model | **LOW RISK** |
| ⚠️ **MINOR CONCERNS** (YELLOW) | • Some circular deals (10-20% revenue)<br>• Customer concentration 25-40%<br>• Some AI marketing hype but substance exists<br>• Business model mostly clear | **MEDIUM RISK** |
| ❌ **YES RED FLAGS** (RED) | • Circular deals >20% of revenue<br>• Single customer >40% of revenue/capex<br>• AI washing (rebranding as "AI" without substance)<br>• Opaque business model | **HIGH RISK** |

#### **WHY This Matters for D&O Risk**:

##### **Red Flag 1: Circular Deals**

**Definition**: Company A invests in Company B, then Company B buys services from Company A

**Example**: Microsoft invests $13B in OpenAI → OpenAI buys $300B of cloud services from Microsoft

**D&O Risk**:
- **Antitrust**: DOJ/FTC investigating circular deals as anti-competitive → Regulatory enforcement → D&O claims
- **Revenue Quality**: Circular revenue may not be sustainable → Revenue restatements → Securities litigation
- **Conflicts of Interest**: Board approving deals with investor-backed customers → Derivative suits for breach of fiduciary duty

**PL's Concern**: "Circular investing in this arena potentially creating antitrust issues"

##### **Red Flag 2: Single Customer Concentration**

**Definition**: One customer represents >40% of revenue or capex

**Example**: Oracle's $300B OpenAI deal represents majority of AI capex

**D&O Risk**:
- **Customer Loss Risk**: If customer leaves/delays → Stock decline → Securities litigation
- **Dependency**: Company's fate tied to single customer's success → Lack of diversification → Breach of duty claims
- **Oracle Example**: OpenAI data center delays → Oracle stock -15% → **D&O exposure**

##### **Red Flag 3: AI Washing**

**Definition**: Company overstates AI capabilities or rebrands as "AI company" without substance

**Example**: Traditional companies adding "AI" to name/strategy without real AI products

**D&O Risk**:
- **Securities Fraud**: Misleading investors about AI capabilities → SEC enforcement + class actions
- **Theranos Parallel**: Overstating technology capabilities → Criminal/civil liability
- **Reputation Damage**: When AI washing exposed → Stock collapse → Litigation

#### **How to Assess**:

**Circular Deals**:
- Review 10-K: Related party transactions
- Check investor lists: Are customers also investors?
- Estimate % of revenue from investor-backed customers

**Single Customer Concentration**:
- 10-K: Customer concentration disclosures (required if >10%)
- Earnings calls: Major customer announcements
- Calculate: Largest customer as % of revenue/capex

**AI Washing**:
- Compare AI claims to actual products/revenue
- Check technical substance (patents, papers, real products)
- Look for vague AI marketing vs. specific AI capabilities
- Red flags: "AI strategy" without products, sudden rebranding as "AI company"

**Examples**:
- **Microsoft/OpenAI**: Circular deal (Microsoft invests, OpenAI buys cloud) → ⚠️ **MINOR CONCERN** (YELLOW)
- **Oracle/OpenAI**: Single customer concentration (>40% of AI capex) → ❌ **YES RED FLAG** (RED)
- **Traditional companies rebranding as "AI"**: AI washing → ❌ **YES RED FLAG** (RED)

### **FACTOR 5: Can They Execute? (Operational/Physical Risk)**

#### **The Question**:
> "Does the company have proven ability to execute on AI strategy, or are there operational/physical constraints?"

#### **Binary Assessment**:

| Answer | Criteria | D&O Risk Level |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| ✅ **YES** (GREEN) | • Proven track record of execution<br>• No physical infrastructure delays<br>• Strong governance and management<br>• Manageable complexity | **LOW RISK** |
| ⚠️ **MAYBE** (YELLOW) | • Some execution concerns (e.g., margin pressure)<br>• Moderate complexity<br>• Governance concerns (e.g., CEO dominance)<br>• Supply chain pressures but manageable | **MEDIUM RISK** |
| ❌ **NO** (RED) | • Data center delays (labor/material shortages)<br>• Significant governance red flags (Tesla/Musk-style)<br>• "World of atoms" constraints (physical infrastructure)<br>• Unproven management team | **HIGH RISK** |

#### **WHY This Matters for D&O Risk**:

1. **Execution Failures → Stock Declines**: Delays, cost overruns, missed targets → Stock price drops → Securities litigation

2. **"World of Atoms" Constraint**: Physical infrastructure (data centers, chips) takes time to build → Delays are common → Missed guidance → Litigation

3. **Governance Risk**: Weak boards, CEO dominance, lack of oversight → Caremark duty failures → Derivative suits

4. **Oracle Example**: Data center delays (2027 → 2028) due to "labor and material shortages" → Stock -15% → **D&O exposure**

#### **How to Assess**:

**Data Sources**:
- 10-K: Risk factors (execution risks, supply chain)
- Earnings calls: Delays, cost overruns, guidance misses
- News: Reports of project delays, labor shortages
- Governance: Proxy statement (board independence, CEO power)

**Key Questions**:
- Has company delivered on past AI commitments?
- Any data center or infrastructure delays?
- Is governance strong (independent board) or weak (CEO dominance)?
- Are there physical constraints (labor, materials, permits)?

**Physical Infrastructure Risk Levels**:
- **HIGH**: Building data centers from scratch (Oracle, Meta) → Long lead times, labor/material shortages
- **MODERATE**: Chip manufacturing (Nvidia, Broadcom) → Supply chain constraints
- **LOW**: Software/SaaS (Salesforce, Adobe) → Digital delivery, no physical constraints

**Examples**:
- **Microsoft**: Proven execution, existing data centers → ✅ **YES** (GREEN)
- **Oracle**: Data center delays, labor shortages → ❌ **NO** (RED)
- **Tesla/Musk**: Governance concerns (CEO dominance, board lacks independence) → ❌ **NO** (RED)
- **Broadcom**: Margin execution concerns → ⚠️ **MAYBE** (YELLOW)

## PART 2: BUCKET DETERMINATION LOGIC

### **How to Determine Bucket Placement**

#### **Step 1: Answer All 5 Factors (YES/MAYBE/NO)**

For each company, answer all 5 binary questions:

| Factor | Question | Answer |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **1** | Can they afford AI from OCF? | ✅ YES / ⚠️ MAYBE / ❌ NO |
| **2** | Are AI margins sustainable? | ✅ YES / ⚠️ MAYBE / ❌ NO |
| **3** | Profitable non-AI core? | ✅ YES / ⚠️ MAYBE / ❌ NO |
| **4** | Any critical red flags? | ✅ NO / ⚠️ MINOR / ❌ YES |
| **5** | Can they execute? | ✅ YES / ⚠️ MAYBE / ❌ NO |

#### **Step 2: Count GREEN (✅), YELLOW (⚠️), and RED (❌) Answers**

| Score | Count |
|-------|-------|
| **GREEN answers** | # of ✅ |
| **YELLOW answers** | # of ⚠️ |
| **RED answers** | # of ❌ |

#### **Step 3: Apply Decision Rules**

| Rule | Condition | Bucket | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Rule 1** | **2+ RED answers** | **RED** | **DECLINE** |
| **Rule 2** | 4-5 GREEN answers + 0-1 RED | **GREEN** | Accept, +10-25% pricing |
| **Rule 3** | 3 GREEN answers + 0-1 RED | **YELLOW** | Accept, +30-50% pricing |
| **Rule 4** | 0-2 GREEN answers + 0-1 RED | **YELLOW** | Accept, +50-75% pricing |

**Key Principle**: **Any 2+ RED answers = AUTOMATIC DECLINE**

### **BUCKET CHARACTERISTICS**

#### **GREEN BUCKET** (Low Risk - Target These)

**Profile**:
- 4-5 GREEN answers
- 0-1 RED answers
- Can afford AI from OCF
- Sustainable margins
- Profitable non-AI core
- No critical red flags
- Proven execution

**Examples**: Microsoft, Apple, Google, Amazon (established hyperscalers)

**Portfolio Allocation**: 60-70% of AI portfolio

**Pricing**: Standard + 10-25% load

**D&O Risk**: LOW - These companies can survive AI bubble burst

---

#### **YELLOW BUCKET** (Medium Risk - Selective Participation)

**Profile**:
- 0-3 GREEN answers
- 0-1 RED answers
- Mixed risk profile
- Some concerns but manageable
- May have margin pressure, execution concerns, or moderate red flags

**Examples**: Broadcom, Salesforce, Adobe, Nvidia, Palantir

**Portfolio Allocation**: 25-30% of AI portfolio

**Pricing**: Standard + 30-75% load (depending on severity)

**D&O Risk**: MODERATE - Elevated pricing compensates for volatility

---

#### **RED BUCKET** (High Risk - DECLINE)

**Profile**:
- 2+ RED answers
- Cannot afford AI from OCF
- OR significant red flags (circular deals, single customer, AI washing)
- OR execution failures
- Existential risk if AI fails

**Examples**: Oracle, most AI pure-plays, circular deal participants, AI washing companies

**Portfolio Allocation**: 0-5% (essentially decline)

**Action**: **DECLINE**

**D&O Risk**: HIGH - Likely to face securities litigation if AI bubble bursts
