# Securities Class Action Litigation Against Life Sciences Companies: 2024 Review

## Compiled from: Sidley Austin, Cornerstone Research, Cooley SLE, D&O Diary

---

## KEY FINDINGS

- Life sciences securities class actions surged 29% in 2024 (44 new cases vs 34 in 2023)
- Biotech/pharma accounted for 21.1% of all federal securities class actions filed in 2024 (47 of 222)
- Consumer Non-Cyclical sector (including biotech) saw 67 filings, a 24% increase year-over-year
- Defendants won 59% of district court motions to dismiss (24 of 41 decisions) in life sciences
- Appellate dismissal affirmance rate was 100% (8 of 8 decisions) in life sciences
- Median biotech settlement (2020-2024): $8.5M, significantly below all-sector median of $14M
- Settlement dollars for biotech increased 5% compared to prior 5-year period despite lower medians
- 56% of life sciences securities class actions dismissed at motion-to-dismiss stage
- Pre-approval stage companies: 57% dismissal success rate
- Mature product companies: 61% dismissal success rate (first time matching pre-approval)

## CONCLUSIONS

- Biotech/pharma companies face the HIGHEST volume of securities class actions but achieve LOWER median settlements
- Clinical trial failures and FDA Complete Response Letters are the primary trigger events
- Strong dismissal rates favor defendants, especially at the appellate level
- D&O underwriters should calibrate biotech base rates to reflect high frequency / lower severity profile
- AI-related filings doubled from 7 to 15 in 2024, creating emerging cross-sector exposure
- Short seller report-triggered lawsuits represent 10% of all core filings (21 cases)
- SPAC-related settlements running at 19% of total settlements with $12M median

## SETTLEMENT DATA

### All-Sector Settlement Trends (2024)
- Median settlement: $14.0M (down 10% from $15.4M in 2023)
- Average settlement: $42.4M (down 13% from $48.7M in 2023)
- Total settlement count: 88 (up 6% from 83 in 2023)
- Aggregate total: $3.7B (down 8% from $4.0B in 2023)
- 10-year median average (2015-2023): $11.3M
- 10-year average settlement (2015-2023): $50.7M
- Mega-settlements ($100M+): 7 in 2024 (54% of total value) vs 9 in 2023
- Largest 2024 settlement: $490M (vs $1B in 2023)
- Median case duration: 3.2 years (down from 3.7 in 2023)
- Settlement as % of plaintiff damages: 7.3% median
- Small cases (under $25M damages): 28.2% recovery rate (highest since 2017)

### Biotech/Pharma Specific Settlements
- Median biotech settlement (2020-2024 5-year): $8.5M
- Maximum observed: up to $400M
- Pharma sector: highest number of settlements, lowest median amounts
- Settlement dollars increased 5% vs prior 5-year period
- D&O insurance factor: limited coverage availability contributing to lower SPAC settlements

### Case Outcome Distribution (1997-2024)
- 46% settled
- 43% dismissed
- 10% continuing
- 0.5% remanded
- 0.4% went to trial (only 21 cases in 27 years)

## TRIGGER EVENTS FOR BIOTECH/PHARMA

RISK: Clinical trial failure (Phase 2/3 miss on primary endpoint) — most common trigger, often 30-75% stock decline
RISK: FDA Complete Response Letter (CRL) — rejection of BLA/NDA triggers immediate litigation
RISK: FDA safety concerns or clinical hold — regulatory action during trial
RISK: Revenue/sales fraud allegations — especially post-commercialization for mature products
RISK: Insider trading by officers ahead of negative clinical data disclosure
RISK: COVID-19 vaccine/treatment efficacy claims that failed to materialize
RISK: Manufacturing quality issues (GMP violations) disclosed by FDA
RISK: Short seller reports alleging fraud in clinical data or business model

NOTE: Pre-approval stage companies (drug candidates) face different risk profile than mature product companies
NOTE: Mature product companies now face similar dismissal rates (61%) as pre-approval (57%), converging in 2024
NOTE: FDA actions generated approximately 30% more lawsuits in 2024 compared to prior year
NOTE: Most biotech SCA cases are won on motion to dismiss or withdrawal rather than settlement

## D&O UNDERWRITING CALIBRATION IMPLICATIONS

CHECK: Biotech base claim rate should be higher than all-sector average (21% of filings from ~10% of market)
CHECK: Biotech severity model should use $8.5M median (not all-sector $14M) for settlement estimation
CHECK: Factor F1 (Prior Litigation) for biotech should weight repeat-litigation risk (57% of multi-suit companies are biotech)
CHECK: Factor F4 (IPO/SPAC/M&A) reduced relevance as SPAC filings declined 59%
CHECK: AI-related disclosure risk is emerging cross-sector factor (doubled to 15 filings in 2024)
CHECK: Short seller report trigger should be weighted at 10% base probability for all companies
CHECK: Clinical trial timeline proximity should be a biotech-specific risk amplifier
CHECK: FDA regulatory calendar events should be monitored as trigger date proxies
CHECK: Mature product biotech companies should NOT receive automatic risk reduction vs pre-approval
CHECK: Recovery rate for small cases (under $25M damages) is 28.2% — higher than mega-cases at 7.3%

## SCORING CALIBRATION DATA POINTS

### Claim Base Rates by Sector (2024)
- All sectors overall: 4.0% litigation rate
- S&P 500 companies: 6.1% litigation rate
- Biotech/pharma (Consumer Non-Cyclical): ~9.5% implied rate (67 filings / ~700 public biotech companies)
- Technology: ~5.3% implied rate

### Dismissal Rates for Scoring Calibration
- All sectors: 43% historically dismissed
- Life sciences: 59% dismissed at MTD stage (2024)
- Life sciences appellate: 100% dismissals affirmed (2024)
- Pre-approval biotech: 57% MTD success
- Mature product biotech: 61% MTD success

### Settlement Size Distribution for Severity Model
- All-sector median: $14.0M (2024)
- All-sector average: $42.4M (2024)
- Biotech 5-year median: $8.5M
- Biotech maximum observed: $400M
- SPAC median: $12.0M
- Small case recovery: 28.2% of damages
- Large case recovery: 7.3% of damages
- Mega-settlement ($100M+) probability: 8% of all settlements (7 of 88)

### Duration and Timeline
- Median case duration to settlement: 3.2 years (2024)
- IPO-related filing delay: 371 days median (down from 508 in 2023)
- Institutional lead plaintiff involvement: 39% (lowest in 10 years)
