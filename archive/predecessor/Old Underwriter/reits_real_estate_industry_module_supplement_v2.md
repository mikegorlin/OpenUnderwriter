# SECTION 1: WHY REITs ARE DIFFERENT

## 1.1 The REIT Litigation Landscape

REITs account for **~3-4% of securities class actions** while representing ~3% of S&P 500 market cap. This proportionate filing rate masks critical nuances:

**The reality:**
- REIT securities litigation increased 14% in 2024 vs. historical averages
- Average market cap loss during class periods has expanded significantly
- Non-GAAP metrics (FFO, AFFO, NAV) create unique manipulation opportunities
- Interest rate sensitivity creates disclosure timing challenges
- Subsector variation is EXTREME (office vs. data center vs. mREIT)

**Historical Mega-Cases:**

| Company | Settlement | Year | Root Cause |
|---------|------------|------|------------|
| American Realty Capital Properties (ARCP) | $1.025B | 2020 | AFFO manipulation |
| Cole Credit Property Trust | $120M | 2015 | Non-traded REIT fraud |
| KBS Real Estate Investment Trust | Class action | 2020 | NAV manipulation |
| Starwood Property Trust | Dismissed | 2016 | Interest rate hedging |

**What this means:** REITs require sector-specific underwriting that accounts for: (1) unique accounting metrics, (2) dividend payout requirements, (3) interest rate sensitivity, and (4) property type concentration risk.

# SECTION 2: mREIT SPECIFIC ANALYSIS (v2.1 ENHANCEMENT)

## 2.1 Why mREITs Are Different

Mortgage REITs (mREITs) have fundamentally different risk profiles than equity REITs:

| Factor | Equity REIT | mREIT |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Primary Asset | Physical properties | Mortgage securities/loans |
| Key Metric | FFO/AFFO | Book Value, Net Interest Margin |
| Leverage | 4-6x typical | 6-10x typical (Agency), 3-6x (Commercial) |
| Interest Rate Sensitivity | Moderate | EXTREME |
| Dividend Sustainability | Rental income driven | Spread income driven |
| Volatility | Lower | Higher |

## 2.2 mREIT Subsector Breakdown

### Agency mREIT (Annaly, AGNC, Orchid Island)
- **Assets**: Agency MBS (Fannie, Freddie, Ginnie)
- **Credit Risk**: Zero (government guarantee)
- **Interest Rate Risk**: EXTREME (duration mismatch)
- **Typical Leverage**: 7-10x
- **Key Metrics**: Book Value, Hedge Ratio, Duration Gap
- **Risk Tier**: ðŸŸ  ELEVATED

### Commercial/Hybrid mREIT (Starwood, Ladder Capital)
- **Assets**: Commercial mortgages, bridge loans, mezzanine
- **Credit Risk**: Significant (CRE exposure)
- **Interest Rate Risk**: High
- **Typical Leverage**: 3-5x
- **Key Metrics**: LTV, Debt Yield, NPL Ratio
- **Risk Tier**: ðŸ”´ HIGH

## 2.3 mREIT-Specific Red Flags

| Flag | Threshold | Scoring Impact |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Book Value Decline >15% (1 Year) | BV destruction | +4 pts |
| Book Value Decline >25% (3 Year) | Structural erosion | +6 pts |
| Dividend/Book Value Payout >100% | Returning capital as "dividends" | +6 pts |
| 8+ Dividend Cuts Since IPO | Pattern of unsustainability | +5 pts |
| Hedge Ratio <50% (Agency) | Speculative rate exposure | +4 pts |
| External Management w/ AUM Fees | Growth incentive conflict | +3 pts |
| Competing Portfolios Under Manager | Capital allocation conflicts | +2 pts |
| Yield Significantly Above Peers | Risk premium or dividend trap | +2 pts |

## 2.4 mREIT Book Value Tracking

**Historical Context - ORC Example:**

**Underwriting Implication:** mREITs with persistent book value destruction face elevated securities risk if shareholders allege management misrepresented dividend sustainability or interest rate risk management.

# SECTION 3: EXTERNAL MANAGEMENT ANALYSIS

## 3.1 External vs. Internal Management

| Factor | Internal | External |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Fee Structure | Salary + bonus | AUM-based fees |
| Incentive Alignment | Shareholders | Growth (fee income) |
| Related Party Risk | Lower | Higher |
| Governance Control | Direct | Contractual |
| Risk Tier Impact | Neutral | +3-4 pts |

## 3.2 External Management Red Flags

| Flag | Concern | Scoring |
|------|---------|---------|
| Manager has multiple competing vehicles | Capital allocation conflicts | +3 pts |
| Fee based purely on AUM | Incentive to grow regardless of returns | +2 pts |
| Manager has prior litigation history | Pattern of issues | +4 pts |
| Related party property transactions | Self-dealing potential | +3 pts |
| Internalization discussions ongoing | Transaction risk, litigation potential | +2 pts |

## 3.3 External Management Discovery Questions

1. **Fee Structure**: "Walk me through your management fee calculation. Is there any performance component?"
2. **Competing Portfolios**: "Does your manager manage other REITs or vehicles? How are investment opportunities allocated?"
3. **Related Party Transactions**: "Describe any transactions with affiliates in the past 12 months."
4. **Internalization Plans**: "Has the board evaluated internalization? What would trigger that evaluation?"

# SECTION 7: MANAGEMENT INTERVIEW QUESTIONS

## 7.1 All REITs

1. **AFFO Definition**: "Walk me through how you calculate AFFO. What adjustments do you make that differ from NAREIT guidelines?"
2. **Dividend Sustainability**: "Under what circumstances would you consider cutting the dividend? What's your coverage cushion?"
3. **Refinancing Strategy**: "You have $X maturing in the next 24 months. What's your refinancing plan?"
4. **Tenant Health**: "How do you monitor tenant credit quality? Have any tenants requested rent relief recently?"
5. **Valuation Process**: "How do you value your properties? When was the last third-party appraisal?"

## 7.2 mREIT-Specific

6. **Hedging Strategy**: "What's your current hedge ratio? How do you think about duration management?"
7. **Book Value Communication**: "How do you set shareholder expectations around book value volatility?"
8. **Leverage Philosophy**: "What's your target leverage ratio? What would cause you to de-lever?"
9. **Portfolio Drift**: "Any plans to add non-Agency assets? How would that change your risk profile?"

## 7.3 Externally Managed REITs

10. **Fee Alignment**: "Is there any performance component to management fees?"
11. **Competing Vehicles**: "Does your manager manage other REITs? How are opportunities allocated?"
12. **Related Party Deals**: "Walk me through any related party transactions in the past 12 months."
13. **Internalization**: "Has the board evaluated internalization? What would trigger that?"

## 9.4 REIT-Specific Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **FFO (Funds From Operations)** | Net income + depreciation + amortization - gains on property sales; standardized by NAREIT |
| **AFFO (Adjusted FFO)** | FFO - recurring CapEx - straight-line rent adjustments; NOT standardized |
| **NAV (Net Asset Value)** | Estimated value of properties minus liabilities |
| **Cap Rate** | NOI / Property Value; used to value properties |
| **NOI (Net Operating Income)** | Property revenue - operating expenses (before interest, depreciation) |
| **Same-Store NOI** | NOI growth for properties owned >12 months |
| **Payout Ratio** | Dividend / FFO or AFFO; measures sustainability |
| **ROC (Return of Capital)** | Distribution portion that reduces basis (not taxable income) |
| **Renewal Spread** | Rent change on lease renewals |
| **Lease Expiration Schedule** | Timeline of when leases mature |
| **UPREIT** | Umbrella Partnership REIT; allows tax-deferred property contributions |
| **TRS (Taxable REIT Subsidiary)** | Entity for non-qualifying REIT income |
| **Book Value (mREIT)** | Equity per share; key valuation metric for mREITs |
| **Hedge Ratio (mREIT)** | Percentage of interest rate risk hedged |
| **Duration Gap (mREIT)** | Mismatch between asset and liability duration |

# APPENDIX A: ARCP CASE STUDY ($1.025B Settlement)

## A.1 Timeline of Events

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2011 | ARCP founded by Nicholas Schorsch |
| 2012 | AFFO manipulation begins |
| 2013-2014 | Aggressive acquisition spree ($21B market cap) |
| Oct 2014 | Company discloses "accounting errors" |
| Oct 2014 | CEO and CFO resign |
| Oct 2014 | Stock drops 30%+ |
| 2015-2019 | Litigation proceeds |
| Jan 2020 | $1.025B settlement approved |

## A.2 Key Allegations

1. **AFFO Manipulation:**
   - Systematically inflated AFFO from 2012-2014
   - Excluded items that should have been deducted
   - Created appearance of strong, growing cash flows
   - Supported aggressive dividend policy

2. **Related Party Transactions:**
   - Acquired properties from Schorsch-controlled entities
   - Paid inflated prices
   - Created circular cash flows to support metrics

3. **Dividend Sustainability:**
   - Represented dividend as sustainable
   - Actually funded by borrowings and related party transactions
   - Masked true cash flow performance

## A.3 Underwriting Lessons

| Lesson | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| AFFO scrutiny essential | Compare definition to peers, track changes |
| Related party risk | External management creates conflict potential |
| Rapid growth red flag | $260M to $21B in 2 years should trigger concern |
| Individual executive risk | $200M+ paid by individuals in settlement |
| Dividend coverage | Payout ratio alone insufficient; source of funds matters |
