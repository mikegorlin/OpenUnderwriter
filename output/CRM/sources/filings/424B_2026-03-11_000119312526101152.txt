Section 203 defines a "business combination" to include: &#149; any merger or consolidation involving us and the interested stockholder; &#149; any sale, transfer, pledge or other disposition involving the interested stockholder of 10 percent or more of our assets; &#149; in general, any transaction that results in the issuance or transfer by us of any of our stock to the interested stockholder; &#149; any transaction involving us that has the effect of increasing the proportionate share of our stock owned by the interested stockholders; and &#149; the receipt by the interested stockholder of the benefit of any loans, advances, guarantees, pledges, or other financial benefits provided by or through us. In general, Section 203 defines an "interested stockholder" as any person who, together with the person's affiliates and associates, owns, or within three years prior to the time of determination of interested stockholder status did own, 15 percent or more of a corporation's voting stock.
